Title: A Game-based Upper Limb AROM Measurement System for Older Adults
Authors: Mingze Li, Tatsuki Tsuchiya, Akira Urashima, Shin Morishima and Tomoji Toriyama, Toyama Prefectural University, Japan
ID: zenodo.4273778
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/zenodo.4273778

<p>He world&#39;s population is aging rapidly. According to the latest data from
WHO, from 2020 to 2050,<br> the proportion of people over 60 years old will
double from 11% to 22%. In the same period, the absolute number of people aged
60 years old and over is expected to increase from 605 million to 2 billion
[1,2]. The 2002 Madrid International Plan of Action on Aging (MIPAA) issued in
the Second World Assembly on Aging, highlighted the essential to consider older
persons in development planning. It also stressed that older persons should be
able to participate equitably in and benefit from development outcomes to
promote their health and well-being. Society should provide them with a
favorable environment. Due to the deterioration of aging, more and more scholars
begin to pay attention to the quality of life of the elderly, which&nbsp;is
called the successful aging [4-10]. Generally speaking, successful aging refers
to that the elderly can have good physical movement function, good cognitive
function, positive mental health, good social function and healthy and happy
life. In order to achieve this goal, ROM is an important parameter to judge the
ability of daily functional activities of the elderly [11].<br> The ability of
the upper limb to participate in sports and activities of daily life depends on
its range of motion [12, 13, 14]. ROM has a significant effect on flexibility,
balance and muscle strength of the elderly [15, 16, 17]. ROM measurement can be
divided into active range of motion (AROM) and passive range of motion (PROM).
AROM was measured without any help and PROM was measured with the assistance of
someone to move the subject&#39;s upper limb. So the main difference between
AROM and PROM is the initiative of joint motion. At present, PROM is mainly
measured.</p>  <p>Keywords: AROM, Game, older Adults, Interest</p>
