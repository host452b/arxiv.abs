Title: Filling the Gaps: Vacancy Chains and Agent-Based Models
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.nehzj_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.nehzj_v1

This paper introduces agent-based model (ABMs) for studying vacancy-based
mobility, such as finding a new apartment or getting a promotion. This approach
outperforms current, canonical analytic vacancy models by: 1) modelling off-
equilibrium cases; 2) simulating multiple, overlapping, exogenous shocks of
varying durations and intensities; 3) instantiating dynamic systems with an
inherent tendency towards stability, ex. prototypical bureaucracies; 4)
increasing realism by relaxing standard Markovian assumptions; and 5) providing
confidence bands for point estimates. Applying ABM vacancy models to an
empirical example of occupational mobility reveals a previously undocumented
cohort mechanism, the purge boomerang. These novel models also further the idiom
of ABMs by explicitly coding vacancies as agents, thereby introducing a formal
representation of absence, akin to the zero symbol in arithmetic.
