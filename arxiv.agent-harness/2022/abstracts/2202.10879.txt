Title: Evaluating Persian Tokenizers
Authors: Danial Kamali, Behrooz Janfada, Mohammad Ebrahim Shenasa, Behrouz Minaei-Bidgoli
ID: 2202.10879
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.10879

Tokenization plays a significant role in the process of lexical analysis. Tokens
become the input for other natural language processing tasks, like semantic
parsing and language modeling. Natural Language Processing in Persian is
challenging due to Persian's exceptional cases, such as half-spaces. Thus, it is
crucial to have a precise tokenizer for Persian. This article provides a novel
work by introducing the most widely used tokenizers for Persian and comparing
and evaluating their performance on Persian texts using a simple algorithm with
a pre-tagged Persian dependency dataset. After evaluating tokenizers with the
F1-Score, the hybrid version of the Farsi Verb and Hazm with bounded morphemes
fixing showed the best performance with an F1 score of 98.97%.
