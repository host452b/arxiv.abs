Title: Evaluating AI Evaluation: Perils and Prospects
Authors: John Burden
ID: 2407.09221
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.09221

As AI systems appear to exhibit ever-increasing capability and generality,
assessing their true potential and safety becomes paramount. This paper contends
that the prevalent evaluation methods for these systems are fundamentally
inadequate, heightening the risks and potential hazards associated with AI. I
argue that a reformation is required in the way we evaluate AI systems and that
we should look towards cognitive sciences for inspiration in our approaches,
which have a longstanding tradition of assessing general intelligence across
diverse species. We will identify some of the difficulties that need to be
overcome when applying cognitively-inspired approaches to general-purpose AI
systems and also analyse the emerging area of "Evals". The paper concludes by
identifying promising research pathways that could refine AI evaluation,
advancing it towards a rigorous scientific domain that contributes to the
development of safe AI systems.
