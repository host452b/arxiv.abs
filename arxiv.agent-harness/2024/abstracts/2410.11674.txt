Title: LLM-Mixer: Multiscale Mixing in LLMs for Time Series Forecasting
Authors: Md Kowsher, Md. Shohanur Islam Sobuj, Nusrat Jahan Prottasha, E. Alejandro Alanis, Ozlem Ozmen Garibay et al.
ID: 2410.11674
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.11674

Abstract:
Time series forecasting remains a challenging task, particularly in the context
of complex multiscale temporal patterns. This study presents LLM-Mixer, a
framework that improves forecasting accuracy through the combination of
multiscale time-series decomposition with pre-trained LLMs (Large Language
Models). LLM-Mixer captures both short-term fluctuations and long-term trends by
decomposing the data into multiple temporal resolutions and processing them with
a frozen LLM, guided by a textual prompt specifically designed for time-series
data. Extensive experiments conducted on multivariate and univariate datasets
demonstrate that LLM-Mixer achieves competitive performance, outperforming
recent state-of-the-art models across various forecasting horizons. This work
highlights the potential of combining multiscale analysis and LLMs for effective
and scalable time-series forecasting.
