Title: Secret Use of Large Language Model (LLM)
Authors: Zhiping Zhang, Chenxinran Shen, Bingsheng Yao, Dakuo Wang, Tianshi Li
ID: 2409.19450
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.19450

Abstract:
The advancements of Large Language Models (LLMs) have decentralized the
responsibility for the transparency of AI usage. Specifically, LLM users are now
encouraged or required to disclose the use of LLM-generated content for varied
types of real-world tasks. However, an emerging phenomenon, users' secret use of
LLM, raises challenges in ensuring end users adhere to the transparency
requirement. Our study used mixed-methods with an exploratory survey (125
real-world secret use cases reported) and a controlled experiment among 300
users to investigate the contexts and causes behind the secret use of LLMs. We
found that such secretive behavior is often triggered by certain tasks,
transcending demographic and personality differences among users. Task types
were found to affect users' intentions to use secretive behavior, primarily
through influencing perceived external judgment regarding LLM usage. Our results
yield important insights for future work on designing interventions to encourage
more transparent disclosure of the use of LLMs or other AI technologies.
