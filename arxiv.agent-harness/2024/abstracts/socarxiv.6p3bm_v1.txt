Title: Evaluation of the effectiveness of a multicomponent integrated early childhood development intervention in Siaya County, Kenya
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.6p3bm_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.6p3bm_v1

Responsive care and stimulation between conception and the first two years can
promote optimal child growth and development and buffer negative outcomes. The
health system, which has a wide reach, provides a crucial starting point for
early childhood interventions. We undertook an impact evaluation using a three-
armed cluster randomized controlled trial to establish the effectiveness of a
health facility-based early childhood development (ECD) intervention in relation
to changes in maternal and child outcomes. The study was carried out in Siaya
County in Kenya where the Department of Health implemented an innovative
integrated ECD intervention. Our main estimation strategy was the analysis of
covariance. There was a balance in the sociodemographic characteristics and main
outcomes at baseline. At midline, the intervention showed an impact on caregiver
and child outcomes. The endline results showed no causal impact of the
intervention. Our results have implications on the way interventions in real-
life settings should be implemented.
