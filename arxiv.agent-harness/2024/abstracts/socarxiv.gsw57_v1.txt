Title: Evaluation of artistic inspiration by biodiversity in lyrics of Japanese popular songs
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.gsw57_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.gsw57_v1

Cultural ecosystem services are non-material benefits people obtain from
ecosystems; yet the indirect inspirational values of biodiversity, including
both ecosystems and biological organisms, in creative literary works remain
underexplored. This study investigated occurrences of biodiversity in the lyrics
of Japanese popular songs based on monetary estimation, as an exploration of the
inspirational value of biodiversity. I analyzed the lyrics of the top 50 songs
from the Japanese CD singles chart over 22 years (1999–2020), extracting nouns
related to ecosystems and organisms. The extracted words were classified into
ecosystems and taxonomic groups, and the total sales amount of songs related to
each class was calculated. Statistical analyses compared the occurrences and
total sales amounts of songs containing biodiversity. Finally, I investigated
the effect of richness of ecosystems and biological organisms within a song on
its sales amount, using generalized linear mixed modeling to assess the
prevalence of songs inspired by biodiversity. Out of 1,100 songs, 403 songs
included references to ecosystems or biological organisms, with plants being the
most frequently mentioned. Ecosystems that are familiar to Japanese people and
biological organisms which they frequently encounter in their cultural and daily
lives contributed to aesthetic inspiration in the lyrics of Japanese popular
songs. Most importantly, I indicated that the existence of a single biological
organisms within a song lyric can enhance its sales amount, namely prevalence to
people. My result implies that the inclusion of a less well-known biological
species within a song can indirectly inspire people, increasing recognition and
willingness to conserve it.
