Title: An Evaluation Benchmark for Autoformalization in Lean4
Authors: Aryan Gulati, Devanshu Ladsaria, Shubhra Mishra, Jasdeep Sidhu, Brando Miranda
ID: 2406.06555
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.06555

Large Language Models (LLMs) hold the potential to revolutionize
autoformalization. The introduction of Lean4, a mathematical programming
language, presents an unprecedented opportunity to rigorously assess the
autoformalization capabilities of LLMs. This paper introduces a novel evaluation
benchmark designed for Lean4, applying it to test the abilities of state-of-the-
art LLMs, including GPT-3.5, GPT-4, and Gemini Pro. Our comprehensive analysis
reveals that, despite recent advancements, these LLMs still exhibit limitations
in autoformalization, particularly in more complex areas of mathematics. These
findings underscore the need for further development in LLMs to fully harness
their potential in scientific research and development. This study not only
benchmarks current LLM capabilities but also sets the stage for future
enhancements in autoformalization.
