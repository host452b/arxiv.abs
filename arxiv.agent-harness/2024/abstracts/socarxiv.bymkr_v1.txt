Title: When science hurts, do we still love it? Quality of life implications affect evaluations of research
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.bymkr_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.bymkr_v1

The mechanisms at play when people evaluate scientific information are central
to science-society relations. While multiple aspects of such processes are well
understood, the ways in which people rely on perceived implications for their
lives are not. This study tests the proposal that in evaluating scientific
results, people draw on cues regarding the implications of research for the
quality of their daily lives. This is tested in a survey experiment which
manipulates cues on quality of life implications in descriptions of research
articles. Findings demonstrate that negative quality of life implications do
affect evaluation of research, but that such effects are largely confined to
individuals for whom the perceived severity of negative quality of life
implications is likely to be high.
