Title: Slippery Slopes from ascriptions of agency to agentic capacities - Methodological and political risks in the operationalization of agency in interview studies
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.7k5sm_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.7k5sm_v1

The notion of agency has been the subject of extensive debate in social theory,
gaining its reputation for being a particularly “slippery”  concept. Reflections
on operationalizing agency in field-based empirical studies, however, have been
rare. Using qualitative interview studies on academic careers as an example, I
will distinguish between four different ways to relate interviewee statements to
the theoretical notion of agency: (2) representational relation, (2) causal
relation, (3) biased relation and (4) ideological relation. While the
representational and causal arguments support the operationalization of agency
by means of interviewee statements, it will be argued that the third and fourth
relation represents certain slippery slopes which impair the analytical value
and political implications of the concept.
