Title: Coming of Age: Large Language Models (LLMs) as Cognitive Supports for an Aging Population
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.p6mkd_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.p6mkd_v1

This paper explores the potential of Large Language Models (LLMs) as tools for
cognitive support in aging populations, focusing on their ability to enhance
memory, provide task reminders, and facilitate social engagement. As the global
population ages, the demand for adaptive technologies that support aging in
place increases, while healthcare resources grow increasingly strained. LLMs
represent a promising avenue for addressing this gap by offering culturally
competent, conversational interfaces capable of supporting cognitive function.
Despite current limitations in context retention and response accuracy,
advancements in LLM technology could enhance personalization and continuity of
care, potentially transforming LLMs into significant companions for older adults
experiencing cognitive decline. This paper provides an overview of existing LLM
capabilities, the challenges that remain, and a projection of how these models
might evolve to meet the growing needs of an aging society.
