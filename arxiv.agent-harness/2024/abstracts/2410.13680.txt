Title: Pessimistic Evaluation
Authors: Fernando Diaz
ID: 2410.13680
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.13680

Traditional evaluation of information access systems has focused primarily on
average utility across a set of information needs (information retrieval) or
users (recommender systems). In this work, we argue that evaluating only with
average metric measurements assumes utilitarian values not aligned with
traditions of information access based on equal access. We advocate for
pessimistic evaluation of information access systems focusing on worst case
utility. These methods are (a) grounded in ethical and pragmatic concepts, (b)
theoretically complementary to existing robustness and fairness methods, and (c)
empirically validated across a set of retrieval and recommendation tasks. These
results suggest that pessimistic evaluation should be included in existing
experimentation processes to better understand the behavior of systems,
especially when concerned with principles of social good.
