Title: A Behavioural and Representational Evaluation of Goal-Directedness in Language Model Agents
Authors: Raghu Arghal, Fade Chen, Niall Dalton, Evgenii Kortukov, Calum McNamara et al.
ID: 2602.08964
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.08964

Abstract:
Understanding an agent's goals helps explain and predict its behaviour, yet
there is no established methodology for reliably attributing goals to agentic
systems. We propose a framework for evaluating goal-directedness that integrates
behavioural evaluation with interpretability-based analyses of models' internal
representations. As a case study, we examine an LLM agent navigating a 2D grid
world toward a goal state. Behaviourally, we evaluate the agent against an
optimal policy across varying grid sizes, obstacle densities, and goal
structures, finding that performance scales with task difficulty while remaining
robust to difficulty-preserving transformations and complex goal structures. We
then use probing methods to decode the agent's internal representations of the
environment state and its multi-step action plans. We find that the LLM agent
non-linearly encodes a coarse spatial map of the environment, preserving
approximate task-relevant cues about its position and the goal location; that
its actions are broadly consistent with these internal representations; and that
reasoning reorganises them, shifting from broader environment structural cues
toward information supporting immediate action selection. Our findings support
the view that introspective examination is required beyond behavioural
evaluations to characterise how agents represent and pursue their objectives.
