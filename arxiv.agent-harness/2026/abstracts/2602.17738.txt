Title: Reasoning-Native Agentic Communication for 6G
Authors: Hyowoon Seo, Joonho Seon, Jin Young Kim, Mehdi Bennis, Wan Choi et al.
ID: 2602.17738
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.17738

Abstract:
Future 6G networks will interconnect not only devices, but autonomous machines
that continuously sense, reason, and act. In such environments, communication
can no longer be understood solely as delivering bits or even preserving
semantic meaning. Even when two agents interpret the same information correctly,
they may still behave inconsistently if their internal reasoning processes
evolve differently. We refer to this emerging challenge as belief divergence.
This article introduces reasoning native agentic communication, a new paradigm
in which communication is explicitly designed to address belief divergence
rather than merely transmitting representations. Instead of triggering
transmissions based only on channel conditions or data relevance, the proposed
framework activates communication according to predicted misalignment in agents
internal belief states. We present a reasoning native architecture that augments
the conventional communication stack with a coordination plane grounded in a
shared knowledge structure and bounded belief modeling. Through enabling
mechanisms and representative multi agent scenarios, we illustrate how such an
approach can prevent coordination drift and maintain coherent behavior across
heterogeneous systems. By reframing communication as a regulator of distributed
reasoning, reasoning native agentic communication enables 6G networks to act as
an active harmonizer of autonomous intelligence.
