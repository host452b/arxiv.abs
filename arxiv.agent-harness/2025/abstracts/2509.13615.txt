Title: See, Think, Act: Teaching Multimodal Agents to Effectively Interact with GUI by Identifying Toggles
Authors: Zongru Wu, Rui Mao, Zhiyuan Tian, Pengzhou Cheng, Tianjie Ju et al. (+5)
ID: 2509.13615
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.13615

The advent of multimodal agents facilitates effective interaction within
graphical user interface (GUI), especially in ubiquitous GUI control. However,
their inability to reliably execute toggle control instructions remains a key
bottleneck. To investigate this, we construct a state control benchmark with
binary toggle instructions derived from public datasets. Evaluation results of
existing agents demonstrate their notable unreliability, particularly when the
current toggle state already matches the desired state. To address the
challenge, we propose State-aware Reasoning (StaR), a multimodal reasoning
method that enables agents to perceive the current toggle state, infer the
desired state from the instruction, and act accordingly. Experiments on four
multimodal agents demonstrate that StaR can improve toggle instruction execution
accuracy by over 30\%. Further evaluations on three public agentic benchmarks
show that StaR also enhances general agentic task performance. Finally,
evaluations on a dynamic environment highlight the potential of StaR for real-
world applications. Code and benchmark: https://github.com/ZrW00/StaR.
