Title: Sympathetic Rebels: Agent-based Simulation of Resistance under China’s Information Censorship Apparatus
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.c7xtg_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.c7xtg_v1

This study aims to investigate the relationship between censorship policies,
public emotional responses, and resistance in the specific context of
information censorship in China. We employ an agent-based model to monitor
resistance within the context of the Chinese censorship apparatus. In doing so,
this study investigates the impact of sympathy on resistance and examines the
divisions within the public by creating two groups: "little pink" representing
pro-government forces and "normal citizens" representing apolitical forces. Our
findings reveal that when apolitical citizens exhibit sympathy towards each
other, a significant portion of the population is likely to engage in
resistance. Sympathy towards individuals facing state coercion undermines the
efficacy of censorship’s mitigating effects, thereby facilitating resistance.
Furthermore, public divisions have a positive influence on the proliferation of
resistance groups, contributing to their radicalization and subsequently
delegitimizing the government.
