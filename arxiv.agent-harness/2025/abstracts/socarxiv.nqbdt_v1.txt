Title: Limit Cycles in Opinion Dynamic Networks with Competing Stubborn Agents
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.nqbdt_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.nqbdt_v1

The Counter-Optimal Stubborn Agent Placement (COSAP) problem concerns how
competing agents attempt to position themselves within a network to maximize
their influence over collective opinion dynamics. While prior work has primarily
examined optimal placements in static or one-shot contexts, less is known about
how opposing agents dynamically adjust their strategies in competitive
environments. In this study, we model COSAP systems as a competitive diffusion
process where two stubborn agents seek to influence a population through
iterated network positioning. Simulations across networks of varying structure
reveal that while agents generally race to occupy the most central node,
networks with short path lengths create conditions in which neither agent can
maintain an uncontested advantage. Instead, agents engage in adaptive cycles,
repeatedly shifting positions in response to one other. These limit cycles vary
in length and complexity depending on global network properties, with highly
connected networks supporting persistent cycling and longer cycles, whereas
networks with longer path lengths cause cycles to collapse into fixed points.
These results underscore the importance of network structure in shaping
influence dynamics and suggest that efforts to counter misinformation or
manipulate opinion may grow more difficult in an increasingly connected world.
