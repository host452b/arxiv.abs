Title: Turning Conversations into Workflows: A Framework to Extract and Evaluate Dialog Workflows for Service AI Agents
Authors: Prafulla Kumar Choubey, Xiangyu Peng, Shilpa Bhagavath, Caiming Xiong, Shiva Kumar Pentyala et al.
ID: 2502.17321
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.17321

Abstract:
Automated service agents require well-structured workflows to provide consistent
and accurate responses to customer queries. However, these workflows are often
undocumented, and their automatic extraction from conversations remains
unexplored. In this work, we present a novel framework for extracting and
evaluating dialog workflows from historical interactions. Our extraction process
consists of two key stages: (1) a retrieval step to select relevant
conversations based on key procedural elements, and (2) a structured workflow
generation process using a question-answer-based chain-of-thought (QA-CoT)
prompting. To comprehensively assess the quality of extracted workflows, we
introduce an automated agent and customer bots simulation framework that
measures their effectiveness in resolving customer issues. Extensive experiments
on the ABCD and SynthABCD datasets demonstrate that our QA-CoT technique
improves workflow extraction by 12.16\% in average macro accuracy over the
baseline. Moreover, our evaluation method closely aligns with human assessments,
providing a reliable and scalable framework for future research.
