Title: Internal evaluation of the Smart Tourism Capital initiative and Green Pioneer Programme
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.mkz72_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.mkz72_v1

Participating entities in smart tourism competitions found the process a
valuable internal audit, consolidating existing initiatives and identifying
strengths and weaknesses. It often acted as a catalyst for new strategies and
enhanced collaboration. Benefits included increased international visibility and
prestige, fostering internal awareness and pride, and strengthening networking
among various organisations. To improve, cities consistently requested detailed
feedback on their applications, including scores and comparative data. They also
urged the creation of a community of practice for all participants, increased
dissemination of results, and the introduction of financial incentives.
Suggestions for adjustment included competition categories and more structured
application guidelines.
