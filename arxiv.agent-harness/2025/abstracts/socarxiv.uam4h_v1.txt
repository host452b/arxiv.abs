Title: Expert and Learner Evaluation of a Mobile-Assisted ESP Speaking Module: A Formative Study
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.uam4h_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.uam4h_v1

This paper reports the formative evaluation of a mobile-assisted English for
Specific Purposes (ESP) speaking module, ‘TourSpeak’, conducted during its
development process. The study explores how experts and learners perceive the
content, pedagogy, and usability of the module, as well as their feedback on its
strengths, weaknesses, and suggestions for improvement. The two-stage formative
evaluation consists of a review by six experts and a walkthrough by two learners
in the first stage, followed by micro-evaluations from four learners in the
second stage. Data was collected using evaluation checklists with structured
items and open-ended questions. The responses to the structured items were
analysed based on frequency, and feedback was analysed thematically. The
findings indicate that the experts and learners had a positive perception of the
module’s content, pedagogy, and usability. They perceived the module as
pedagogically sound, enabling knowledge construction, sharing of experiences,
interaction, discussion, and self-directed learning. They perceive the module’s
learning objectives, content, and lesson structure as suitable for improving
speaking skills.  The module was usable in terms of its user interface, user-
friendliness, and user acceptance.  They also emphasized relevant content,
realistic scenarios, clear structure, and user-friendliness as strengths of the
module, while reliance on the internet is considered a weakness. Suggestions for
improvement include expanding content with diverse scenarios, incorporating
gamification elements, revising the feedback time, and incorporating live chat
or calls.  The findings provide valuable insights for language instructional
designers, ESP practitioners, and mobile module developers, informing the
development of ESP modules and expanding technological integration in ESP.
