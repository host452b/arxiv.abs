Title: ToolGrad: Efficient Tool-use Dataset Generation with Textual "Gradients"
Authors: Zhongyi Zhou, Kohei Uehara, Haoyu Zhang, Jingtao Zhou, Lin Gu et al.
ID: 2508.04086
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.04086

Abstract:
Prior work synthesizes tool-use LLM datasets by first generating a user query,
followed by complex tool-use annotations like DFS. This leads to inevitable
annotation failures and low efficiency in data generation. We introduce
ToolGrad, an agentic framework that inverts this paradigm. ToolGrad first
constructs valid tool-use chains through an iterative process guided by textual
"gradients", and then synthesizes corresponding user queries. This
"answer-first" approach led to ToolGrad-5k, a dataset generated with more
complex tool use, lower cost, and 100% pass rate. Experiments show that models
trained on ToolGrad-5k outperform those on expensive baseline datasets and
proprietary LLMs, even on OOD benchmarks.
