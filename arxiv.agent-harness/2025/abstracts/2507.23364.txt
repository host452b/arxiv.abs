Title: Holistic Evaluations of Topic Models
Authors: Thomas Compton
ID: 2507.23364
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.23364

Topic models are gaining increasing commercial and academic interest for their
ability to summarize large volumes of unstructured text. As unsupervised machine
learning methods, they enable researchers to explore data and help general users
understand key themes in large text collections. However, they risk becoming a
'black box', where users input data and accept the output as an accurate summary
without scrutiny. This article evaluates topic models from a database
perspective, drawing insights from 1140 BERTopic model runs. The goal is to
identify trade-offs in optimizing model parameters and to reflect on what these
findings mean for the interpretation and responsible use of topic models
