Title: Multi-Agent Systems for Robotic Autonomy with LLMs
Authors: Junhong Chen, Ziqi Yang, Haoyuan G Xu, Dandan Zhang, George Mylonas
ID: 2505.05762
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.05762

Abstract:
Since the advent of Large Language Models (LLMs), various research based on such
models have maintained significant academic attention and impact, especially in
AI and robotics. In this paper, we propose a multi-agent framework with LLMs to
construct an integrated system for robotic task analysis, mechanical design, and
path generation. The framework includes three core agents: Task Analyst, Robot
Designer, and Reinforcement Learning Designer. Outputs are formatted as
multimodal results, such as code files or technical reports, for stronger
understandability and usability. To evaluate generalizability comparatively, we
conducted experiments with models from both GPT and DeepSeek. Results
demonstrate that the proposed system can design feasible robots with control
strategies when appropriate task inputs are provided, exhibiting substantial
potential for enhancing the efficiency and accessibility of robotic system
development in research and industrial applications.
