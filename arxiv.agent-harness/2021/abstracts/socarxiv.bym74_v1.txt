Title: How to Improve Network Science: the Potential of (Empirically Calibrated and Validated) Agent-Based Modelling
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.bym74_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.bym74_v1

This article argues that the potential of Agent-Based Modelling (the capability
for empirical justification of computer programmes representing social processes
as dynamically unfolding individual cognition, action and interaction to reveal
emerging aggregate outcomes) is not yet fully realised in the scientific study
of social networks. By critically analysing several existing studies, it shows
why the technique’s distinctive methodology (involving empirical calibration and
validation) is just as important to its scientific contribution as its novel
technical capabilities. The article shows the advantages of Agent-Based Models
following this methodology and distinguishes these clearly from the implications
of apparently similar techniques (like actor-based approaches). The article also
discusses the limitations of existing Agent-Based Modelling applied to social
networks, enabling the approach to make a more effective contribution to Network
Science in future.
