Title: Using Non-Verbal Expressions as a Tool in Naming Research
Authors: Omer Regev, Michael Soloveitchik, Dror G. Feitelson
ID: 2103.08701
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.08701

Abstract:
Variable and function names are extremely important for program comprehension.
It is therefore also important to study how developers select names. But
controlled experiments on naming are hindered by the need to describe to
experimental subjects what it is they need to name. Words appearing in these
descriptions may then find their way into the names, leading to a bias in the
results. We suggest that this problem can be alleviated by using emojis or other
small graphics in lieu of key words in the descriptions. A replication of
previous work on naming, this time including such emojis and graphics, indeed
led to a more diverse and less biased choice of words in the names than when
using English descriptions.
