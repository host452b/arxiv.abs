Title: Lessons Learned: A Program Evaluation of a Female Residential Facility
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.y4fpj_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.y4fpj_v1

“She was about to be 18 and cried day and night because she did not know where
she was gone go after here” is the common reality for girls who are committed to
Department of Juvenile Justice residential facilities. The lack of preparedness
to be reintegrated back into communities is customary according to the girls who
participated in the following program evaluation of a Department of Juvenile
Justice residential facility. To date very few if any program evaluations have
amplified the youth’s voice. Even fewer have incorporated the voice of staff and
direct care professionals alike. This program evaluation presents data on the
identified factors that will help promote change within the residential facility
and beyond from the youth perspectives. The major implicit findings included the
need for a trauma informed system that focuses on helping girls and the
provision of opportunities to grow hope within youth.
