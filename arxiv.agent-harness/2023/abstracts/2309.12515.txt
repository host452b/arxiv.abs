Title: A Diamond Machine For Strong Evaluation
Authors: Beniamino Accattoli, Pablo Barenbaum
ID: 2309.12515
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.12515

Abstract machines for strong evaluation of the $λ$-calculus enter into arguments
and have a set of transitions for backtracking out of an evaluated argument. We
study a new abstract machine which avoids backtracking by splitting the run of
the machine in smaller jobs, one for argument, and that jumps directly to the
next job once one is finished.   Usually, machines are also deterministic and
implement deterministic strategies. Here we weaken this aspect and consider a
light form of non-determinism, namely the diamond property, for both the machine
and the strategy. For the machine, this introduces a modular management of jobs,
parametric in a scheduling policy. We then show how to obtain various
strategies, among which leftmost-outermost evaluation.
