Title: Assessing the Evaluation: Biocultural Community Protocols, Inclusive and Prospective Circular Business Innovation in Mexico
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.t8pk4_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.t8pk4_v1

Purpose. A theoretical-argumentative approach is made on the importance of
annexing in the biocultural community protocols (PCB), the realization of
circular and inclusive business innovation (iNCI) in Mexico. Methodology.
Literature on PCBs is reviewed, with methods for evaluating biological resources
or traditional knowledge to protect, including social impact assessment methods
to design and implement the iNCI based on technology-based enterprises or social
impact startups (SIS). The strategic prospective is highlighted as a generator
of business scenarios to carry them out. Findings. Proposal of functional
conceptual model PCB-iNCI through a SIS. Originality. The possibility both of
attracting investors to the community and vice versa to achieve mutual benefits.
