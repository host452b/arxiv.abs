Title: An Evaluation of Software Sketches
Authors: Roy Friedman
ID: 2309.03045
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.03045

This work presents a detailed evaluation of Rust (software) implementations of
several popular sketching solutions, as well as recently proposed optimizations.
We compare these solutions in terms of computational speed, memory consumption,
and several approximation error metrics. Overall, we find a simple hashing based
solution employed with the Nitro sampling technique [22] gives the best trade-
off between memory, error and speed. Our findings also include some novel
insights about how to best combine sampling with Counting Cuckoo filters
depending on the application.
