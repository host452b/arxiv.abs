Title: Ensuring Reliable Participation in Subjective Video Quality Tests Across Platforms
Authors: Babak Naderi, Ross Cutler
ID: 2509.20001
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.20001

Abstract:
Subjective video quality assessment (VQA) is the gold standard for measuring
end-user experience across communication, streaming, and UGC pipelines. Beyond
high-validity lab studies, crowdsourcing offers accurate, reliable, faster, and
cheaper evaluation-but suffers from unreliable submissions by workers who ignore
instructions or game rewards. Recent tests reveal sophisticated exploits of
video metadata and rising use of remote-desktop (RD) connections, both of which
bias results. We propose objective and subjective detectors for RD users and
compare two mainstream crowdsourcing platforms on their susceptibility and
mitigation under realistic test conditions and task designs.
