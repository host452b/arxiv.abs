Title: Can LLMs Generate High-Quality Test Cases for Algorithm Problems? TestCase-Eval: A Systematic Evaluation of Fault Coverage and Exposure
Authors: Zheyuan Yang, Zexi Kuang, Xue Xia, Yilun Zhao
ID: 2506.12278
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.12278

Abstract:
We introduce TestCase-Eval, a new benchmark for systematic evaluation of LLMs in
test-case generation. TestCase-Eval includes 500 algorithm problems and 100,000
human-crafted solutions from the Codeforces platform. It focuses on two pivotal
tasks: (1) Fault Coverage, which measures how well LLM-generated test sets probe
diverse input scenarios and cover a wide range of potential failure modes. (2)
Fault Exposure, which evaluates whether LLMs can craft a tailored test input
that reveals a specific incorrect code implementation. We provide a
comprehensive assessment of 19 state-of-the-art open-source and proprietary LLMs
on TestCase-Eval, offering insights into their strengths and limitations in
generating effective test cases for algorithm problems.
