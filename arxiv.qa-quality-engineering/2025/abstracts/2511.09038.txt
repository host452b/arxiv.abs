Title: Test Plan Generation for Live Testing of Cloud Services
Authors: Oussama Jebbar, Ferhat Khendek, Maria Toeroe
ID: 2511.09038
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09038

Abstract:
Live testing is performed in the production environment ideally without causing
unacceptable disturbance to the production traffic. Thus, test activities have
to be orchestrated properly to avoid interferences with the production traffic.
A test plan is the road map that specifies how the test activities need to be
orchestrated. Developing a test plan includes tasks such as test configuration
selection/generation, test configuration deployment planning, creating the test
runs schedule, choosing strategies to mitigate the risk of interferences, etc.
The manual design of a test plan is tedious and error prone. This task becomes
harder especially when the systems are large and complex. In this paper we
propose an approach for automating test plans generation. With this approach we
aim at reducing service disruption that may be induced by the testing activities
in production. We illustrate our approach with a case study and discuss its
different aspects.
