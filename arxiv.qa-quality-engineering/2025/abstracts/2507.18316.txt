Title: YATE: The Role of Test Repair in LLM-Based Unit Test Generation
Authors: Michael Konstantinou, Renzo Degiovanni, Jie M. Zhang, Mark Harman, Mike Papadakis
ID: 2507.18316
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.18316

Abstract:
Recent advances in automated test generation utilises language models to produce
unit tests. While effective, language models tend to generate many incorrect
tests with respect to both syntax and semantics. Although such incorrect tests
can be easily detected and discarded, they constitute a "missed opportunity" --
if fixed, they are often valuable as they directly add testing value (they
effectively target the underlying program logic to be tested) and indirectly
form good seeds for generating additional tests. To this end, we propose a
simple technique for repairing some of these incorrect tests through a
combination of rule-based static analysis and re-prompting. We evaluate this
simple approach, named YATE, on a set of 6 open-source projects and show that it
can effectively produce tests that cover on average 32.06% more lines and kill
21.77% more mutants than a plain LLM-based method. We also compare YATE with
four other LLM-based methods, namely HITS, SYMPROMPT, TESTSPARK and COVERUP and
show that it produces tests that cover substantially more code. YATE achieves
22% higher line coverage, 20% higher branch coverage and kill 20% more mutants
at a comparable cost (number of calls to LLMs).
