Title: Evaluating Objective Speech Quality Metrics for Neural Audio Codecs
Authors: Luca A. Lanzendörfer, Florian Grötschla
ID: 2511.19734
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.19734

Abstract:
Neural audio codecs have gained recent popularity for their use in generative
modeling as they offer high-fidelity audio reconstruction at low bitrates. While
human listening studies remain the gold standard for assessing perceptual
quality, they are time-consuming and impractical. In this work, we examine the
reliability of existing objective quality metrics in assessing the performance
of recent neural audio codecs. To this end, we conduct a MUSHRA listening test
on high-fidelity speech signals and analyze the correlation between subjective
scores and widely used objective metrics. Our results show that, while some
metrics align well with human perception, others struggle to capture relevant
distortions. Our findings provide practical guidance for selecting appropriate
evaluation metrics when using neural audio codecs for speech.
