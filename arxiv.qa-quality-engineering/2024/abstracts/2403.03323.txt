Title: Automated Software Verification of Hyperliveness
Authors: Raven Beutner
ID: 2403.03323
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.03323

Abstract:
Hyperproperties relate multiple executions of a program and are commonly used to
specify security and information-flow policies. Most existing work has focused
on the verification of $k$-safety properties, i.e., properties that state that
all $k$-tuples of execution traces satisfy a given property. In this paper, we
study the automated verification of richer properties that combine universal and
existential quantification over executions. Concretely, we consider
$\forall^k\exists^l$ properties, which state that for all $k$ executions, there
exist $l$ executions that, together, satisfy a property. This captures important
non-$k$-safety requirements, including hyperliveness properties such as
generalized non-interference, opacity, refinement, and robustness. We design an
automated constraint-based algorithm for the verification of
$\forall^k\exists^l$ properties. Our algorithm leverages a sound-and-complete
program logic and a (parameterized) strongest postcondition computation. We
implement our algorithm in a tool called ForEx and report on encouraging
experimental results.
