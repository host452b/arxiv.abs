Title: Evaluation Metric for Quality Control and Generative Models in Histopathology Images
Authors: Pranav Jeevan, Neeraj Nixon, Abhijeet Patil, Amit Sethi
ID: 2411.01034
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.01034

Abstract:
Our study introduces ResNet-L2 (RL2), a novel metric for evaluating generative
models and image quality in histopathology, addressing limitations of
traditional metrics, such as Frechet inception distance (FID), when the data is
scarce. RL2 leverages ResNet features with a normalizing flow to calculate RMSE
distance in the latent space, providing reliable assessments across diverse
histopathology datasets. We evaluated the performance of RL2 on degradation
types, such as blur, Gaussian noise, salt-and-pepper noise, and rectangular
patches, as well as diffusion processes. RL2's monotonic response to increasing
degradation makes it well-suited for models that assess image quality, proving a
valuable advancement for evaluating image generation techniques in
histopathology. It can also be used to discard low-quality patches while
sampling from a whole slide image. It is also significantly lighter and faster
compared to traditional metrics and requires fewer images to give stable metric
value.
