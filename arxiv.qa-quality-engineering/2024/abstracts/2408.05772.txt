Title: An analysis of HOI: using a training-free method with multimodal visual foundation models when only the test set is available, without the training set
Authors: Chaoyi Ai
ID: 2408.05772
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05772

Abstract:
Human-Object Interaction (HOI) aims to identify the pairs of humans and objects
in images and to recognize their relationships, ultimately forming $\langle
human, object, verb \rangle$ triplets. Under default settings, HOI performance
is nearly saturated, with many studies focusing on long-tail distribution and
zero-shot/few-shot scenarios. Let us consider an intriguing problem:``What if
there is only test dataset without training dataset, using multimodal visual
foundation model in a training-free manner? '' This study uses two experimental
settings: grounding truth and random arbitrary combinations. We get some
interesting conclusion and find that the open vocabulary capabilities of the
multimodal visual foundation model are not yet fully realized. Additionally,
replacing the feature extraction with grounding DINO further confirms these
findings.
