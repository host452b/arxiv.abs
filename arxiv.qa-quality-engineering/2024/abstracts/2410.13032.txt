Title: Hypothesis Testing the Circuit Hypothesis in LLMs
Authors: Claudia Shi, Nicolas Beltran-Velez, Achille Nazaret, Carolina Zheng, Adrià Garriga-Alonso et al.
ID: 2410.13032
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.13032

Abstract:
Large language models (LLMs) demonstrate surprising capabilities, but we do not
understand how they are implemented. One hypothesis suggests that these
capabilities are primarily executed by small subnetworks within the LLM, known
as circuits. But how can we evaluate this hypothesis? In this paper, we
formalize a set of criteria that a circuit is hypothesized to meet and develop a
suite of hypothesis tests to evaluate how well circuits satisfy them. The
criteria focus on the extent to which the LLM's behavior is preserved, the
degree of localization of this behavior, and whether the circuit is minimal. We
apply these tests to six circuits described in the research literature. We find
that synthetic circuits -- circuits that are hard-coded in the model -- align
with the idealized properties. Circuits discovered in Transformer models satisfy
the criteria to varying degrees. To facilitate future empirical studies of
circuits, we created the \textit{circuitry} package, a wrapper around the
\textit{TransformerLens} library, which abstracts away lower-level manipulations
of hooks and activations. The software is available at
\url{https://github.com/blei-lab/circuitry}.
