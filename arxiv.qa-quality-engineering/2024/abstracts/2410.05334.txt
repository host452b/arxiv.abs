Title: TA3: Testing Against Adversarial Attacks on Machine Learning Models
Authors: Yuanzhe Jin, Min Chen
ID: 2410.05334
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.05334

Abstract:
Adversarial attacks are major threats to the deployment of machine learning (ML)
models in many applications. Testing ML models against such attacks is becoming
an essential step for evaluating and improving ML models. In this paper, we
report the design and development of an interactive system for aiding the
workflow of Testing Against Adversarial Attacks (TA3). In particular, with TA3,
human-in-the-loop (HITL) enables human-steered attack simulation and
visualization-assisted attack impact evaluation. While the current version of
TA3 focuses on testing decision tree models against adversarial attacks based on
the One Pixel Attack Method, it demonstrates the importance of HITL in ML
testing and the potential application of HITL to the ML testing workflows for
other types of ML models and other types of adversarial attacks.
