Title: A Survey of Verification, Validation and Testing Solutions for Smart Contracts
Authors: Chaïmaa Benabbou, Önder Gürcan
ID: 2112.03426
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.03426

Abstract:
Smart contracts are programs stored on a blockchain that run when predetermined
conditions are met. However, designing and implementing a smart contract is not
trivial since upon deployment on a blockchain, it is no longer possible to
modify it (neither for improving nor for bug fixing). It is only possible by
deploying a new version of the smart contract which is costly (deployment cost
for the new contract and destruction cost for the old contract). To this end,
there are many solutions for testing the smart contracts before their
deployment. Since realizing bug-free smart contracts increase the reliability,
as well as reduce the cost, testing is an essential activity. In this paper, we
group the existing solutions that attempt to tackle smart contract testing into
following categories: public test networks, security analysis tools, blockchain
emulators and blockchain simulators. Then, we analyze these solutions,
categorize them and show what their pros and cons are.
