Title: A Distributed Process Model of Cryptographic Protocols
Authors: Andrew M. Mironov
ID: 2109.08980
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.08980

Abstract:
Cryptographic Protocols (CP) are distributed algorithms intended for secure
communication in an insecure environment. They are used, for example, in
electronic payments, electronic voting procedures, systems of confidential data
processing, etc. Errors in CPs can bring to great financial and social damage,
therefore it is necessary to use mathematical methods to substantiate the
correctness and safety of CPs. In this paper, a distributed process model of CPs
is presented, which allows one to formally describe CPs and their properties. It
is shown how to solve the problems of verification of CPs on the base of this
model.
