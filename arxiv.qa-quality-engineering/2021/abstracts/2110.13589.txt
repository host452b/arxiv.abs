Title: AQP: An Open Modular Python Platform for Objective Speech and Audio Quality Metrics
Authors: Jack Geraghty, Jiazheng Li, Alessandro Ragano, Andrew Hines
ID: 2110.13589
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.13589

Abstract:
Audio quality assessment has been widely researched in the signal processing
area. Full-reference objective metrics (e.g., POLQA, ViSQOL) have been developed
to estimate the audio quality relying only on human rating experiments. To
evaluate the audio quality of novel audio processing techniques, researchers
constantly need to compare objective quality metrics. Testing different
implementations of the same metric and evaluating new datasets are fundamental
and ongoing iterative activities. In this paper, we present AQP - an
open-source, node-based, light-weight Python pipeline for audio quality
assessment. AQP allows researchers to test and compare objective quality metrics
helping to improve robustness, reproducibility and development speed. We
introduce the platform, explain the motivations, and illustrate with examples
how, using AQP, objective quality metrics can be (i) compared and benchmarked;
(ii) prototyped and adapted in a modular fashion; (iii) visualised and checked
for errors. The code has been shared on GitHub to encourage adoption and
contributions from the community.
