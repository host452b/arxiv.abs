Title: Estimating Test Performance for AI Medical Devices under Distribution Shift with Conformal Prediction
Authors: Charles Lu, Syed Rakin Ahmed, Praveer Singh, Jayashree Kalpathy-Cramer
ID: 2207.05796
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2207.05796

Abstract:
Estimating the test performance of software AI-based medical devices under
distribution shifts is crucial for evaluating the safety, efficiency, and
usability prior to clinical deployment. Due to the nature of regulated medical
device software and the difficulty in acquiring large amounts of labeled medical
datasets, we consider the task of predicting the test accuracy of an arbitrary
black-box model on an unlabeled target domain without modification to the
original training process or any distributional assumptions of the original
source data (i.e. we treat the model as a "black-box" and only use the predicted
output responses). We propose a "black-box" test estimation technique based on
conformal prediction and evaluate it against other methods on three medical
imaging datasets (mammography, dermatology, and histopathology) under several
clinically relevant types of distribution shift (institution, hardware scanner,
atlas, hospital). We hope that by promoting practical and effective estimation
techniques for black-box models, manufacturers of medical devices will develop
more standardized and realistic evaluation procedures to improve the robustness
and trustworthiness of clinical AI tools.
