Title: An Automated Testing and Debugging Toolkit for Gate-Level Logic Synthesis Applications
Authors: Siang-Yun Lee, Heinz Riener, Giovanni De Micheli
ID: 2207.13487
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2207.13487

Abstract:
Correctness and robustness are essential for logic synthesis applications, but
they are often only tested with a limited set of benchmarks. Moreover, when the
application fails on a large benchmark, the debugging process may be tedious and
time-consuming. In some fields such as compiler construction, automatic testing
and debugging tools are well-developed to support developers and provide minimal
guarantees on program quality. In this paper, we adapt fuzz testing and delta
debugging techniques and specialize them for gate-level netlists commonly used
in logic synthesis. Our toolkit improves over similar tools specialized for the
AIGER format by supporting other gate-level netlist formats and by allowing a
tight integration to provide 10x speed-up. Experimental results show that our
fuzzer captures defects in mockturtle, ABC, and LSOracle with 10x smaller
testcases and our testcase minimizer extracts minimal failure-inducing cores
using 2x fewer oracle calls.
