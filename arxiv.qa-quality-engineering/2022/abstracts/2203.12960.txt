Title: Evaluation of IoT Self-healing Mechanisms using Fault-Injection in Message Brokers
Authors: Miguel Duarte, João Pedro Dias, Hugo Sereno Ferreira, André Restivo
ID: 2203.12960
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.12960

Abstract:
The widespread use of Internet-of-Things (IoT) across different application
domains leads to an increased concern regarding their dependability, especially
as the number of potentially mission-critical systems becomes considerable.
Fault-tolerance has been used to reduce the impact of faults in systems, and
their adoption in IoT is becoming a necessity. This work focuses on how to
exercise fault-tolerance mechanisms by deliberately provoking its malfunction.
We start by describing a proof-of-concept fault-injection add-on to a commonly
used publish/subscribe broker. We then present several experiments mimicking
real-world IoT scenarios, focusing on injecting faults in systems with (and
without) active self-healing mechanisms and comparing their behavior to the
baseline without faults. We observe evidence that fault-injection can be used to
(a) exercise in-place fault-tolerance apparatus, and (b) detect when these
mechanisms are not performing nominally, providing insights into enhancing
in-place fault-tolerance techniques.
