Title: Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing on IP cameras
Authors: Pietro Biondi, Stefano Bognanni, Giampaolo Bella
ID: 2202.06597
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.06597

Abstract:
IP cameras have always been part of the Internet of Things (IoT) and are among
the most widely used devices in both home and professional environments.
Unfortunately, the vulnerabilities of IP cameras have attracted malicious
activities. For example, in 2016, a massive attack resulted in thousands of
cameras and IoT devices being breached and used to create a botnet. Given this
history and the extremely sensitive nature of the data these devices have access
to, it is natural to question what security measures are in place today. In this
paper, a vulnerability assessment and penetration testing is performed on a
specific model of IP camera, the TP-Link Tapo C200. More in detail, our findings
show that the IP camera in question suffers from three vulnerabilities such as:
denial of service, video eavesdropping and, finally, a new type of attack called
"Motion Oracle". Experiments are not limited to the offensive part but also
propose countermeasures for the camera in question and for all those that may
suffer from the same vulnerabilities. The countermeasure is based on the use of
another IoT device, a Raspberry Pi.
