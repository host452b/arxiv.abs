Title: Process algebra, process scheduling, and mutual exclusion
Authors: C. A. Middelburg
ID: 2003.00473
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.00473

Abstract:
In the case of multi-threading as found in contemporary programming languages,
parallel processes are interleaved according to what is known as a
process-scheduling policy in the field of operating systems. In a previous
paper, we extend ACP with this form of interleaving. In the current paper, we do
so with the variant of ACP known as ACP$_ε$. The choice of ACP$_ε$ stems from
the need to cover more process-scheduling policies. We show that a
process-scheduling policy supporting mutual exclusion of critical subprocesses
is now covered.
