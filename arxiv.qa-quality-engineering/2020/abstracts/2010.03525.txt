Title: Empirical Standards for Software Engineering Research
Authors: Paul Ralph, Nauman bin Ali, Sebastian Baltes, Domenico Bianculli, Jessica Diaz et al.
ID: 2010.03525
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.03525

Abstract:
Empirical Standards are natural-language models of a scientific community's
expectations for a specific kind of study (e.g. a questionnaire survey). The ACM
SIGSOFT Paper and Peer Review Quality Initiative generated empirical standards
for research methods commonly used in software engineering. These living
documents, which should be continuously revised to reflect evolving consensus
around research best practices, will improve research quality and make peer
review more effective, reliable, transparent and fair.
