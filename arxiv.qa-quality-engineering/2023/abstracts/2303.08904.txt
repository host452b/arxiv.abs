Title: Process Equivalence Problems as Energy Games
Authors: Benjamin Bisping
ID: 2303.08904
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.08904

Abstract:
We characterize all common notions of behavioral equivalence by one
6-dimensional energy game, where energies bound capabilities of an attacker
trying to tell processes apart. The defender-winning initial credits
exhaustively determine which preorders and equivalences from the (strong)
linear-time--branching-time spectrum relate processes. The time complexity is
exponential, which is optimal due to trace equivalence being covered. This
complexity improves drastically on our recent approach for deciding groups of
equivalences where exponential sets of distinguishing HML formulas are
constructed on top of a super-exponential reachability game. In experiments
using the VLTS benchmarks, the algorithm performs on par with the best
similarity algorithm.
