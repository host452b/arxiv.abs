Title: Using the Built-in iPhone Body Tracking System for Neurological Tests: The Example of Assessing Arm Weakness in Stroke Patients. A Preliminary Evaluation of Accuracy and Performance
Authors: Vittorio Lippi, Isabelle Daniela Walz, Tobias Heimbach, Simone Meier, Jochen Brich et al.
ID: 2311.09716
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09716

Abstract:
Timely treatment of stroke is critical to minimize brain damage. Therefore,
efforts are being made to educate the public on detecting stroke symptoms, e.g.,
face, arms, and speech test (FAST). In this position paper, we propose to
perform the arm weakness test using the integrated video tracking from an iPhone
- some general tests to assess the tracking quality and discuss potential
critical points. The test has been performed on 4 stroke patients. The result is
compared with the report of the clinician. Although presenting some limitations,
the system proved to be able to detect arm weakness as a symptom of stroke. We
envisage that introducing a portable body tracking system in such clinical tests
will provide advantages in terms of objectivity, repeatability, and the
possibility to record and compare groups of patients.
