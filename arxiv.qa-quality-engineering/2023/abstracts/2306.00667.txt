Title: Predicting the Quality of Revisions in Argumentative Writing
Authors: Zhexiong Liu, Diane Litman, Elaine Wang, Lindsay Matsumura, Richard Correnti
ID: 2306.00667
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.00667

Abstract:
The ability to revise in response to feedback is critical to students' writing
success. In the case of argument writing in specific, identifying whether an
argument revision (AR) is successful or not is a complex problem because AR
quality is dependent on the overall content of an argument. For example, adding
the same evidence sentence could strengthen or weaken existing claims in
different argument contexts (ACs). To address this issue we developed
Chain-of-Thought prompts to facilitate ChatGPT-generated ACs for AR quality
predictions. The experiments on two corpora, our annotated elementary essays and
existing college essays benchmark, demonstrate the superiority of the proposed
ACs over baselines.
