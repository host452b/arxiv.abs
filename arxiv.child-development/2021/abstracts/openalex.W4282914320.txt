Title: Intracultural Bullying among Latinx High School Students Building Engaging Communities with Cultural Wealth Leadership
Authors: Jesús Angulo
ID: openalex.W4282914320
URL: https://doi.org/10.46569/20.500.12680/0c483q54f

This dissertation utilized narrative inquiry and portraiture to examine the
intra-cultural bullying in conflict between Latino students at two high schools
in California. The dissertation also employed the framework of community
cultural wealth to understand underlying causes of the tensions between the
students. Eight high school students were interviewed and their perspectives
were represented in the dissertation in the form of individual portraits of each
student, as well as a discussion of key themes evolved from the interviews. The
dissertation findings indicate that Latino students are suffering from certain
forms of intracultural bullying and tensions, which cause harm in terms of their
social inclusion as well as their academic achievement. The implications from
this research include the need for further research on the underlying causes of
these tensions, and to understand new ways to support these students and leaders
to affirm their cultural identities and their languages in the school settings
and within the community. In terms of specific leadership implications, the
dissertation suggests that the school leaders need to broaden and deepen
counseling services, a robust ethnic studies curriculum, linguistic preservation
and celebration in courses and school events, and new forms of studentinitiated
and student-led dialogue and reconciliation.
