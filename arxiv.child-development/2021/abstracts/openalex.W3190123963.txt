Title: The lamination machine and laminating as thing-power in early childhood pedagogical practice
Authors: Lisbeth Ljosdal Skreland, Tale Steen-Johnsen
ID: openalex.W3190123963
URL: https://doi.org/10.1080/14733285.2021.1965089

This article considers the different ways in which the lamination machine
influences early childhood practice. By using Jane Bennett’s notion of ‘thing-
power’, we focus on how preschool teachers and preschool teacher-students use a
lamination machine and why they use it. We claim that both the process of
lamination and the machine have a great impact on pedagogical practices by
giving power to the teachers’ choices and priorities. The article contributes to
the discussion on how technology and materiality become a vital part of the
everyday lives of both teachers and children in kindergartens and prompts a need
to highlight the use of technology in early childhood education.
