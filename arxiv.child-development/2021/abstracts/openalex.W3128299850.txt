Title: Does the timing and duration of mental health problems during childhood and adolescence matter for labour market participation of young adults?
Authors: Samira de Groot, Karin Veldman, Benjamin C. Amick, Tineke Oldehinkel, Iris Arends
ID: openalex.W3128299850
URL: https://doi.org/10.1136/jech-2020-215994

The duration of MHPs during childhood and adolescence is strongly associated
with not having paid work in young adulthood. This emphasises the necessity of
applying a life-course perspective when investigating the effect of MHPs on LMP.
Early monitoring, mental healthcare and the (early) provision of employment
support may improve young adult's participation in the labour market.
