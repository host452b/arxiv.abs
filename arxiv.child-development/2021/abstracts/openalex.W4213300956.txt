Title: Crisis of Cultural Identity in Khaled Hosseini’s The Kite Runner
Authors: Ramesh Preasad Adhikary
ID: openalex.W4213300956
URL: http://doi.org/10.36347/sjahss.2021.v09i05.006

The novel The Kite Runner by Khaled Hosseini deals with the cultural crisis of
the characters. Migration, hybridity, acculturation, transculturation and
diaspora are responsible for cultural crisis. It presents the suffering of a
Muslim boy named Amir of Afghanistan who had migrated to USA where he finds
alone and lacks his cultural performance. The scattered people of USA have
accepted multicultural aspects. When they followed American culture, they lost
their original native culture. The long living in the foreign land and distract
from the native land and contact with other culture resulted in cultural loss.
The compulsion for adjustment and contacts with foreign brings loss of pure
native culture. When they feel identity crisis, they try to retrieve the native
culture.
