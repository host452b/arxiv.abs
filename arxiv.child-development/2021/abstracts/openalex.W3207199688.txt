Title: Social Robot Co-Design Canvases: A Participatory Design Framework
Authors: Minja Axelsson, Raquel Oliveira, Mattia Racca, Ville Kyrki
ID: openalex.W3207199688
URL: https://doi.org/10.1145/3472225

Design teams of social robots are often multidisciplinary, due to the broad
knowledge from different scientific domains needed to develop such complex
technology. However, tools to facilitate multidisciplinary collaboration are
scarce. We introduce a framework for the participatory design of social robots
and corresponding canvas tool for participatory design. The canvases can be
applied in different parts of the design process to facilitate collaboration
between experts of different fields, as well as to incorporate prospective users
of the robot into the design process. We investigate the usability of the
proposed canvases with two social robot design case studies: a robot that played
games online with teenage users and a librarian robot that guided users at a
public library. We observe through participants’ feedback that the canvases have
the advantages of (1) providing structure, clarity, and a clear process to the
design; (2) encouraging designers and users to share their viewpoints to
progress toward a shared one; and (3) providing an educational and enjoyable
design experience for the teams.
