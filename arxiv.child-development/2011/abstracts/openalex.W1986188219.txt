Title: Dance program for physical rehabilitation and participation in children with cerebral palsy
Authors: Citlali López‐Ortiz, Kim Gladden, Laura L. Deon, Jennifer Schmidt, Gay L. Girolami
ID: openalex.W1986188219
URL: https://doi.org/10.1080/17533015.2011.564193

<i>Objective</i>: This pilot study aimed to examine a classical ballet program
created for children with cerebral palsy (CP) as an emerging physical
rehabilitation modality. The main program goals were to promote participation
and to provide an artistic, physically therapeutic activity. <i>Methods</i>: The
study was conducted in collaboration with a tertiary rehabilitation hospital,
one outpatient physical therapy clinic, and one community center. As a pilot
exploratory study, the research design included questionnaires to assess the
participants' (children (<i>n</i> = 16), parents (<i>n</i> = 16), and therapists
(<i>n</i> = 13)) perceptions on the therapeutic benefit of the dance program. A
binomial statistical model was adopted for the analysis of the results.
<i>Results</i>: Main results were that the children reported high enjoyment
level (<i>p</i> < .0001) and desire for more classes (.0001); the parents
reported perceived therapeutic benefit (<i>p</i> < .0001); and the therapists
viewed the class as a positive adjunct to therapy (<i>p</i> < .0001).
<i>Conclusions</i>: The main limitation of this work was the utilization of
subjective outcome measures. However, this is the first step toward the
development of objective measures of an intervention that, to our knowledge, has
not been analyzed in the past. We conclude that the program has the potential of
developing into an evidence based rehabilitation resource for children with CP.
