Title: Transculturalism as a Model for Examining Migration to Cyprus and Philistia at the End of the Bronze Age
Authors: Louise A. Hitchcock
ID: openalex.W3008584372
URL: http://hdl.handle.net/11343/123205

The ethnic identity of the Philistines and their relationship to Greece, Cyprus,
Anatolia, and the Sea Peoples continues to be a very lively and interesting area
of scholarly debate. This contribution reviews recent work on general categories
of cultural interaction with regard to the east Mediterranean including
colonisation, migration, and cultural diffusion. The relationship between these
categories of interaction and the formation of cultural identity such as
creolization, hybridity, assimilation, and acculturation is also considered. An
argument in favor of transculturalism, multivocality, and long-term approaches
to the formation of cultural identity is then proposed.
