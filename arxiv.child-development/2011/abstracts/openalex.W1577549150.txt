Title: Shadows of the Plantation? A Social History of Suriname’s Bauxite Town Moengo
Authors: Anouk de Koning
ID: openalex.W1577549150
URL: https://doi.org/10.1163/13822373-90002430

This article explores the social history of Suriname’s first bauxite town,
Moengo, founded in the late 1910s. It recounts the rise of a new industry that
drew workers away from the plantations and urban artisanal occupations to work
in a massive, highly organized and orchestrated organization-cum-social
community. Using oral narratives about life in Moengo, as well as census and
other statistical data, this contribution asks whether everyday life in the
mining enclave echoed features of the plantation.
