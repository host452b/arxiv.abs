Title: Movement/ing Provocations in Early Childhood Education
Authors: Nicole Land, Ildikó Danis
ID: openalex.W2597034927
URL: https://doi.org/10.18357/jcs.v41i3.16304

&lt;div class="page" title="Page 1"&gt;&lt;div class="section"&gt;&lt;div
class="layoutArea"&gt;&lt;div class="column"&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;span&gt;Drawing on
an inquiry-based project focused on experimenting &lt;/span&gt;&lt;span&gt;with
movement in Canadian early childhood education, this article imagines how
educators might think (with) movement pedagogically. The authors detail our
practices of pedagogical inquiry, confront contemporary movement-related
policies and pedagogies in Euro-Western Canadian early childhood education, and
discuss three encounters with movement: (1) &lt;/span&gt;&lt;span&gt;creating
conditions with movement(s); (2) movement does...; &lt;/span&gt;&lt;span&gt;and
(3) attending to uncommon movements. We conclude by gesturing toward the
uncertain, contingent, and generative potentialities of thinking with movement
in early childhood education.
