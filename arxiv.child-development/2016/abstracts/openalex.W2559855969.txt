Title: Narratives of Promise, Narratives of Caution: A Review of the Literature on Social Impact Bonds
Authors: Alec Fraser, Stefanie Tan, Mylène Lagarde, Nicholas Mays
ID: openalex.W2559855969
URL: https://doi.org/10.1111/spol.12260

Abstract Social Impact Bonds (SIBs) are a new mechanism for delivering public
services. This article reviews the emerging SIB literature in high‐income
settings. It identifies three distinct narratives: a public sector reform
narrative; a financial sector reform narrative; and a cautionary narrative.
These are analyzed relative to three themes: public versus private values;
outcomes contracting; and risk allocation. The first two narratives are
complementary and offer a ‘win‐win’ portrayal of SIBs. The third narrative
challenges this dominant commentary by highlighting potential risks. There is
limited empirical evidence on active SIBs to support these narratives. SIBs may
have the potential to align public and private interests while improving
outcomes for people affected by entrenched social problems, but this is yet to
be established and appears less probable than the third more cautionary
narrative. More empirical research is needed to consider the potential risks,
drawbacks, benefits and alternatives of SIBs in different settings.
