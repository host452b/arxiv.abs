Title: A Touch of Paint: Transgressing Unspoken Boundaries
Authors: Kim Atkinson
ID: openalex.W2552318137
URL: https://doi.org/10.18357/jcs.v41i2.16100

&lt;p&gt;Contemporary early childhood contexts are rife with unspoken rules,
boundaries, and expectations that are understood to be best practice within
discourses of quality in early years settings. These habitual practices are not
apolitical: They flow from powerful discourses purporting to be scientific and
reliable, with systematically defined ways of thinking, seeing, and speaking
about children. Drawing on a body/paint/brush/forest encounter, the author looks
to disrupt these discourses and transgress unspoken boundaries. By engaging with
an ethic of resistance, acknowledging the relationality of early childhood
practice and the entanglements of human and nonhuman forces, the author seeks to
transgress fixed identities and be open to otherness in an ongoing process of
becoming.&lt;/p&gt;
