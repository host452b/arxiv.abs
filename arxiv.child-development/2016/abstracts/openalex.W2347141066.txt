Title: Art Appreciation for Developing Communication Skills among Preschool Children
Authors: Matjaž Duh
ID: openalex.W2347141066
URL: https://doi.org/10.26529/cepsj.105

In the contemporary process of teaching fine arts, children's own creative
expression and art appreciation are used to encourage learners towards both
perception and reception; consequently, the evaluation and internalization of
works of art play an equally important role. In a qualitative empirical research
study that takes the form of a case study, we studied the response of children
to works of art and their demonstrated communication skills in this. The results
have shown that children respond to works of art on multiple levels. With non-
standardized narrative group interviews, we observed children's associations.
Children perceived and internalized the given artworks and also put their
emotions into words. The study has shown that systematic development of art
appreciation among pre-school children can have a positive impact on their
communication skills.
