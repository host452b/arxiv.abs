Title: A qualitative study examining the benefits and challenges of incorporating patient-reported outcome substance use and mental health questionnaires into clinical practice to improve outcomes on the HIV care continuum
Authors: Anne K. Monroe, Sarah M. Jabour, Sebastian Peña, Jeanne Keruly, Richard D. Moore
ID: openalex.W2806277040
URL: https://doi.org/10.1186/s12913-018-3203-x

Both patients and providers indicated that PROs are potentially useful clinical
tools to improve detection of SU/MH. However, patients and providers expressed
conflicting viewpoints about disclosure of SU/MH using computerized PROs. Future
studies implementing PROs screening interventions must assess concerns over
confidentiality and honest disclosure of SU/MH to understand the effectiveness
of PROs as a clinical tool. More research is also needed on patient-centered
integration of the results of PROs in HIV care.
