The methods described in this chapter cover the cohort data collection,
which\nconstituted the common data set for all three studies reported in this
paper. A sample of 430\nbilingual toddlers, learning British English and one of
13 target Additional Languages (N =\n372), or any other Additional Language (N =
58), were identified over a 2-year period. To\nincrease variation in both
English/Additional Language pairs and in the situational factors\noutlined above
(language exposure, mode of exposure and demographic factors), data
was\ncollected through trained research assistants recruited in the six
universities involved in this\nproject (Bangor, Birmingham, Kent, Liverpool,
Oxford and Plymouth), as well as in Bristol\nand Leicester, each having access
to multilingual populations to various degrees. However,\nsince the testing
platform was remotely accessible, the final sample comprised families from\nall
areas of the UK, apart from Scotland and Northern Ireland. Bangor had the
additional\nadvantage of being located in a region with 75% bilingual Welsh-
English children, providing\na unique opportunity to compare language skills in
bilinguals growing in a region with\npredominant bilingualism, to those whose
bilingualism is linked to immigration.
