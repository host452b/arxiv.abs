Title: Thumbs Up: The effective use of music in health and well-being education for Australian Aboriginal youth in remote communities
Authors: Brendan Anthony, Donna Weston, Samuel Vallen
ID: openalex.W2792535174
URL: https://doi.org/10.1386/ijcm.11.1.71_1

Abstract The academic literature on Australian Indigenous health and well-being
suggests an overwhelming need for education to alleviate ‘lifestyle’ diseases
and social dysfunction. Yet, the socio-cultural diversity of Australian
Aboriginal communities provides a challenging landscape for efficacious and
long-lasting support processes. The ‘Uncle Jimmy Thumbs Up!’ (UJTU) programme is
facing these issues from an innovative perspective. ‘Thumbs up’, addresses
health and well-being in remote Aboriginal communities from the ground up by
engaging in music education programmes with the youth of the community, which
subsequently filters through to the broader community. Based on an analysis of
the effectiveness of a ‘Thumbs Up’ case study, this article will present
preliminary recommendations for working with Australian Indigenous youth and
their broader communities through facilitated musical activities. Informal
learning frameworks, which incorporate music-making shared between educators and
community members, are shown to be a constructive means of engaging the youth
and empowering them in the management of their health and well-being.
