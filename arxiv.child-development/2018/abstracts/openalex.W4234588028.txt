Title: Educación parvularia chilena: efectos por género y años de participación
Authors: Alejandra Cortázar, Constanza Vielma
ID: openalex.W4234588028
URL: https://doi.org/10.31619/caledu.n47.29

The present study aims to estimate the effect of Chilean public early childhood
education on the academic outcomes of 4th grade children on the Education
Quality Measurement System (Simce, for its abbreviation in Spanish). The study
seeks to understand if these effects vary according to the child's gender and by
the number of years of participation in early childhood education. Through
Propensity Score Matching, it is observed that attending public early childhood
education positively affects 4th grade academic results in Mathematics and
Language. The early childhood education effect was greater for boys than for
girls, and for those who assisted Transition Level 1.
