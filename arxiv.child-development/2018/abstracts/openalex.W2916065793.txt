Title: Naming the Early Childhood Professional: Artefacts, Childish Pursuits and Critical Questions
Authors: A. Gibbons
ID: openalex.W2916065793
URL: http://hdl.handle.net/10292/12272

This article addresses the problem of naming those who work with children in
early childhood education setting. The article works through some perceived
problems associated with shifting boundaries in the profession of early
childhood teaching. In order to do so, it employs three artefacts that are quite
familiar to early childhood centre communities. These artefacts give the
discussion a sense of immediacy, and highlight the role that the immediate
environment can play in opening up critical questions concerning the early
childhood education teaching profession.
