Title: V: GENERAL DISCUSSION
Authors: Caroline Floccia, Thomas D. Sambrook, Claire Delle Luche, Rosa Kwok, Jeremy Goslin
ID: openalex.W2794148547
URL: https://doi.org/10.1111/mono.12352

Ours is the first study to directly measure the relative contribution of
linguistic distance\nto the acquisition of a bilingual toddler's two languages,
which was the aim of Study 1. To\naddress this question we tested 372 24-month-
olds learning British English and one of 13 target\nAdditional Languages. We
found that a higher phonological overlap between these Additional\nLanguages and
British English led to higher levels of Additional Language CDI
vocabulary\nproduction. Similar effects in comprehension were found for our
other measures of linguistic\ndistance, namely degree of similar in
morphological complexity and word order. Importantly,\nlinguistic distance
contributed unique variance even when other key factors (proportion of\nEnglish
in child-directed input, proportion of English in parental overheard speech and
gender)\nwere entered into the same model.
