Title: Newborns, Infants, and Adolescents in Postmedieval Northern Finland: A Case Study from Keminmaa
Authors: Sanna Lipkin, Sirpa Niinimäki, Saara Tuovinen, Heli Maijanen, Erika Ruhl
ID: openalex.W3098109093
URL: https://doi.org/10.1007/s41636-020-00265-1

Abstract Four coffins dating from the period between the mid-18th and mid-19th
centuries from Keminmaa in northern Ostrobothnian Finland were examined using
computed-tomography (CT) scanning, a methodology with research applications for
exploring human remains, coffins, and funerary textiles. The age and, in two
cases, sex of the remains were estimated, and both historical sources and
archaeological material suggest that socialization through gender was apparent
in this collection. This article explores both the material testimony of care,
dedication, and innocence of newborn and infant burials, as well as the
socialization process and role of women adolescents in providing children’s
funerary materials.
