Title: Alone on stage: How one LGBTIQ+ educator uses poetic performative autoethnography for social change
Authors: J. Scott Baker
ID: openalex.W3019806916
URL: https://doi.org/10.17159/2520-9868/i78a02

In this article, I analyse my self-reflexive examination of my poetry
performance calling for the need to have lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender,
intersex, and queer (LGBTIQ+) educators serve as role models in the secondary
classroom setting as a mechanism to promote social change. I presented an
original poetry collection in front of 400+ high school students and educators
advocating for the need for queer educators to come out of the proverbial
closet, and here I reflect on this act as both poetic inquiry and performance
autoethnography, providing a hybrid methodology-Poetic Performative
Autoethnography (PPA), while framing the experience and its meaning to both me
as participant-researcher and the audience. I include discussion about the
importance of/and political nature of representation, the need for space and
dialogue, as well as obstacles facing LGBTIQ+ educators in the classroom.
