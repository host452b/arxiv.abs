Title: Child Helpers : A Multidisciplinary Perspective
Authors: David F. Lancy
ID: openalex.W2980463825
URL: https://digitalcommons.usu.edu/sswa_facpubs/699

In most of the worlds' distinct cultures, children - from toddlerhood - eagerly
volunteer to help others with their chores. Laboratory research in child
psychology supports the claim that the helper "stage" is biologically based.
This Element examines the development of helping in varied cultural contexts, in
particular, reviewing evidence for supportive environments in the ethnographic
record versus an environment that extinguishes the drive to be helpful in WEIRD
children. In the last section, the benefits of the helper stage are discussed,
specifically the development of an ability to work and learn collaboratively
