Title: Rituals in the Time of COVID‐19: Imagination, Responsiveness, and the Human Spirit
Authors: Evan Imber‐Black
ID: openalex.W3041271416
URL: https://doi.org/10.1111/famp.12581

Following the format put forth by Imber-Black and Roberts, I examine daily
rituals, family traditions, holidays, and life cycle rituals during the pandemic
of COVID-19. Marked by symbols capable of carrying multiple meanings, symbolic
actions, special time and special place, and newly invented and adapted rituals
are illustrated through stories of couples, families, and communities.
