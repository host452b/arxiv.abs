Title: Exploration Of Collective Efficacy Beliefs In Student Project Teams: Implications For Student And Team Outcomes
Authors: Robert W. Lent, Janet Schmidt, G.A. Pertmer, Linda C. Schmidt
ID: openalex.W203356392
URL: https://doi.org/10.18260/1-2--10947

This study extends Bandura's (1997 2 ) concept of collective efficacy to the
context of student project teams in engineering education. Students
participating in an introductory engineering design or a senior-level
engineering course completed measures of collective efficacy (beliefs about the
capabilities of their project teams), team cohesion, and satisfaction with the
team experience. Findings indicated that collective efficacy (a) was strongly
related to team cohesion and satisfaction; (b) partially mediated the
relationship between team cohesion and satisfaction, and (c) was moderated
related to other social cognitive measures (e.g., self-efficacy, interest)
regarding pursuit of an engineering major.
