the late Mycenaean settlement on the Acropolis of Aigeira in the region of
Achaia on the Corinthian Gulf, the other the Mycenaean chamber tomb cemetery at
Elateia in Central Greece, where she worked together with Phanouria Dakoronia.
Sigrid Deger-Jalkotzy has also been a prolific organiser of conferences
(frequently together with other colleagues) that were dedicated to Linear B
studies, the Late Helladic (LH) IIIC period of the 12th and early 11th century
BC and the Early Iron Age (middle of the 11th to end of 8th century BC). Not
only did she bring together renowned scholars in the field discussing current
academic topics, but she also managed to publish the important volumes in a
short time after the conferences and thus make them accessible to a broader
academic public. She has co-organised also other conferences in her various
prominent roles in the hierarchy of the Austrian Academy of Sciences. We wish to
make her rich oeuvre available to a broad public, who will certainly appreciate
the open access. We have organised the writings of Sigrid Deger-Jalkotzy into
sections that cover the Late Bronze Age Aegean, Linear B, LH IIIC and the Early
Iron Age, Aigeira, Elateia, editorship, and finally obituaries and laudations.
