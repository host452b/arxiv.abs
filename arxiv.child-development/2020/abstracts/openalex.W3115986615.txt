Title: The Field as a Black Box: Ethnographic Research in the Age of Platforms
Authors: Tiziano Bonini, Alessandro Gandini
ID: openalex.W3115986615
URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/2056305120984477

This article aims to provide a detailed rendering of the struggles we
experienced while undertaking ethnographic research for the study of music
curators working at online music streaming platforms. Based on the field notes
generated during a multi-sited ethnography, the article will critically discuss
the “black boxing” strategies employed by these platforms in order to protect
themselves from public scrutiny, and how media scholars can counteract in order
to (partially) circumvent the restrictions posed by them. In light of this
discussion, we propose five tactics that we argue can be employed in order to
perform ethnographic research in the age of platforms. We conclude with a
reflection on what we can learn from “failures in the field” and why it is
important to advance ethnographic studies of the new places of cultural
production.
