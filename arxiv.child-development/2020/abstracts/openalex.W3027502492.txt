Title: How Schools With a Focus on Experiential Education in Comparison to Traditional Schools Affect Adolescent Identity Formation
Authors: Lisa Meerts-Brandsma, Jim Sibthorp
ID: openalex.W3027502492
URL: https://doi.org/10.18666/jorel-2020-v12-i2-9884

Identity formation and consolidation is considered one of the key developmental
tasks of adolescence The "problem of identity" appears in writing as early as
the 1800s and has grown as a discussion point since then For most of humanity,
"who am I" has been a function of a person's environmental circumstances. Modern
life increasingly allows youth to pursue their individual interests rather than
to be channeled into predefined roles, but that freedom requires youth be active
in determining their life trajectory.
