Title: Ukrainian piano music paradigm of the past and present in the performance dimension
Authors: Zoryana Lastovetska-Solanska, Nina Dyka, Mariya Lypetska, Svitlana Saldan, Iryna Makovetska
ID: openalex.W4399199353
URL: http://dx.doi.org/10.53681/c1514225187514391s.33.236

The research relevance on the specificity of Ukrainian piano composition, both
solo and ensemble, is predefined by its demand for modern society, as well as in
the content and significance of this sphere of musical art. The research aims to
highlight the phenomenon in question as a voluminous, complex and, at the same
time, holistic system, as well as to identify its leading components, their
content, functional significance, and principles of interconnection between
themselves and with the society. To achieve the above objective, the following
methods were used: historical, culturological, analysis, structuring, synthesis,
comparative characterization, and systematization. The research analyses the
complexity of the phenomenon. The study examined several components of the
phenomenon, namely the leading components of the foundation of piano performance
in Ukraine, the sectors of pianism implementation and the nature of its impact
on them, the modernisation of social life directions under the influence of
music, Ukrainian piano art and performance in different historical eras,
elements of the art of piano performance, software functions in the sphere of
modern pianism. The research results illuminate processes of Ukrainian academic
national piano art integration into world professional creative and performing
culture as well as the uniqueness of Ukrainian music which embodied stylistic
and technical achievements of classicism, romanticism, directions of the
twentieth century and modernity on the ground of original colourful ethnic
origins. The practical significance of the proposed study consists in the fact
that its materials can be used in the concert activities of pianists, in
compiling educational programs of secondary and higher music schools in piano
performance, as well as in the implementation of research projects aimed at
finding discoveries in the field of pianism, its specificity, manifested in
different areas.
