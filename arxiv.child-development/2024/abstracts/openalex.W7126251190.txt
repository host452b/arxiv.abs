Title: Perception of The Community of Tegowanu Wetan Village, Tegowanu District, Grobogan District Towards The Tradition of Cucuk Lampah In Traditional Javanese Weddings
Authors: Yusita Kurnia Novitasari
ID: openalex.W7126251190
URL: https://doi.org/10.30659/ijsunissula.3.2.10-26

The cucuk lampah tradition is one of the traditions inherited from ancestors
which has been passed down from generation to generation in Tegowanu Wetan
Village, Tegowanu District, Grobogan Regency. This tradition is believed to
repel evil spirits or interference from evil spirits when holding a wedding.
Cucuk lampah is played by a man who has an authoritative character like a
senopati or kesatriyo. But the cucuk lampah found in Tegowanu Wetan Village is a
man whose character resembles a woman in terms of clothing, speaking style and
walking style. This research is included in qualitative research and uses field
research , namely research carried out by direct observation in the field.The
result of this research is that the cucuk lampah tradition is carried out after
the bridal panggih ceremony. The cucuk lampah dancer is followed by two
bridesmaids , four ayu pagers , 2 satriyo twins , the bride and groom, each
bride's parents, siblings, and bridesmaids . The cucuk lampah dancers are also
accompanied by music with the aim that the dance matches the rhythm of the music
and the group can also follow the movements. Cucuk lampah's cross- gender
practice is motivated by people who are starting to get bored with their
appearance. The perception of the people of Tegowanu Wetan Village regarding the
function of cucuk lampah cross gender , including as a means of friendship, a
means of entertainment, a means of economic value, and cultural
preservation.Keywords : Community Perception, Tradition, Cucuk Lampah, Cross
Gender .
