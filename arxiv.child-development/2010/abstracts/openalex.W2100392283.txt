Title: Helping Children Cross Cultural Boundaries in the Borderlands: Arts Program at Freese Elementary in San Diego Creates Cultural Bridge
Authors: Liane Brouillette, Lynne Jennings
ID: openalex.W2100392283
URL: https://doi.org/10.21977/d96110020

This article describes the unique multicultural arts program that has developed
at Freese Elementary School, located only 20 minutes from the United States-
Mexico border, in the southeastern corner of the San Diego Unified School
District. The Arts and Culture Magnet Program at Freese grew out of the need
build bridges in a neighborhood where rapid demographic change had created
explosive tensions. The magnet program teaches visual and performing arts,
literacy, and social studies through in-class artist residencies, workshops,
field trips, and assemblies that have been developed in collaboration with local
arts organizations. Through the arts, Freese has become a bright and cheery
school where children are busy learning, an island of hope in a neighborhood
beset by conflict.
