Title: How and why did I get here? : a narrative inquiry into the paths to teaching of a group of EFL teachers in Hong Kong
Authors: Betty Jean Gran

The genesis for this exploratory narrative inquiry was high teacher attrition in
a&#13;\nnew vocational English programme in Hong Kong. The study sought to
discover&#13;\nwhether the paths to teaching of the remaining, successful
teachers, as construed&#13;\nin their own accounts, might include commonalities
acting as contributors to&#13;\nsuccess. An understanding of such contributors
could inform and enhance the&#13;\nteacher selection process. Seven non-native
English speaking teachers of&#13;\nEnglish participated in the study, in which
they were interviewed and observed&#13;\nin their classrooms. Polkinghorne’s
(1995) framework comprising ‘analysis of&#13;\nnarratives’ (thematic analysis)
and ‘narrative (storied) analysis’ was adopted to&#13;\nrespond to the research
aim and questions. Thematic analysis of narratives&#13;\nacross participants
revealed four pertinent themes: (1) perception of a good fit&#13;\nbetween
person and profession, (2) centrality of the student in the
teachers’&#13;\naccounts (3) perception of teaching as an enjoyable and
satisfying ‘virtuous&#13;\ncycle’ related to student achievement and (4) a high
level of comfort with&#13;\nEnglish as the medium of communication in the
classroom. It was suggested that&#13;\nquestions crafted to probe for these
characteristics could enhance results in&#13;\nteacher recruitment interviews.
Analysis of individual participant narratives&#13;\nusing structural and
literary techniques buttressed by observation data produced&#13;\nnuanced
interpretations of the teachers set against the conceptual template

taking into&#13;\naccount their cultural context. Portraits of these teachers
add to the very limited&#13;\nliterature to date on non-native English speaking
teachers of English working in&#13;\ntheir home countries with students sharing
their first language. The teachers’&#13;\nstories and stories of teachers
produced in this study illuminate the lives of these&#13;\nmembers of the
teaching profession. Finally, it is suggested that interrogating&#13;\nsuccess
is an appealing approach to uncovering knowledge about teachers
and&#13;\naddressing educational problems.
