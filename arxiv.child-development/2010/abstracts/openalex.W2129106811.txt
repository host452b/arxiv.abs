Title: Memory texts and memory work: Performances of memory in and with visual media
Authors: Annette Kühn
ID: openalex.W2129106811
URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/1750698010370034

This essay focuses on re-enactments of the past through performances of memory
both in and with visual media , and looks at how these may embody, express, work
through, and even unpick, interconnections between the private, the public and
the personal. It explores some questions around visual media/visual discourses,
memory and collective identity by looking at filmic and photographic examples
from England, Scotland, Canada and China. It also raises some questions around
appropriate research methodologies and about how institutions such as museums
and archives may figure in some of these collective activities, practices and
performances.
