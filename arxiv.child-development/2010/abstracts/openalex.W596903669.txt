Title: What young children say about art: a comparative study
Authors: Felicity McArdle, Kit Mei Betty Wong
ID: openalex.W596903669
URL: https://researchoutput.csu.edu.au/en/publications/59019056-d70f-460f-a7fe-1217409e0ac5

Given the opportunities, young children can be prolific in their productions of
drawings and paintings. In the study reported in this paper, we had two
questions about this. Why do young children draw and paint? And, what does this
prolific activity do? We consider that particular ways of seeing art position
children, and children use their artistic activities to position themselves,
producing their identities. We interviewed a group of children in Hong Kong,
aged between 4 and 5 years, (n=27), and a group of children in Brisbane,
Australia, who were of similar ages (n=15). The cross-cultural dimension added
another dimension to our thinking and conversations around art and young
children.
