Title: What Adolescents Have to Say about Problematic Internet Use: A Qualitative Study Based on Focus Groups
Authors: Simone Rollo, Claudia Venuleo, Lucrezia Ferrante, Raffaele De Luca Picione
ID: openalex.W4388297950
URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph20217013

In this paper, the study presented is designed to gain a deeper insight into how
adolescents describe, understand, and suggest dealing with Problematic Internet
Use (PIU). Eight focus groups were activated with a total of 70 students from
the 9th and 11th grades (Mean <sub>Age</sub> = 15.53 ± 1.202; Female = 44.4%) in
four different schools in Southern Italy. A Thematic Analysis was applied to the
verbatim transcripts, and seven macro-categories were identified throughout the
discourses collected: definition of PIU, symptomatology, impact, determinants,
intervention strategy, opportunities and limits of the digital world, and needs
that adolescents try to satisfy by surfing the net and which the offline world
does not fulfill. Participants converge in seeing PIU in terms of addiction but
adopt heterogeneous viewpoints in talking about the reasons for problematic
engagement and possible preventive intervention strategies. In the overall
picture emerging from the responses, PIU appeared to be the outcome of a
psychological dynamic emerging from the interaction of individual,
interpersonal, and sociocultural dimensions.
