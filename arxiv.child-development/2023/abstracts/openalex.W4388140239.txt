Title: Navigating the Real World: A Grounded Theory-Based Exploration of Autistic Adolescents’ Identity Formation in Marcelo in the Real World
Authors: Shuo Yang, Manimangai Mani, Noritah Omar
ID: openalex.W4388140239
URL: http://dx.doi.org/10.17507/tpls.1311.32

This study analyses the depiction of autism in Marcelo in the Real World, a
young adult fiction novel, to critically explore the identity formation of the
character Marcelo who navigates the challenges and complexities of the real
world. By using the grounded theory and drawing on ideas from disability studies
and social identity theory, the research demonstrates that parental guidance,
encouragement and normal treatment of friends, and exposure to conflicts and
contradictions are the most significant factors and can help autistic
adolescents understand social life, improve social competence, and obtain power
and control in social relations, promoting positive identity formation. The use
of grounded theory methodology enables a full examination of Marcelo's
experiences and the fundamental mechanisms guiding the development of his
identity. This research deepens our comprehension of identity construction under
the setting of autism, advancing both theoretical understanding and useful
interventions for autistic adolescents.
