Title: The wear and tear of racism: Self-silencing from the perspective of young Black women
Authors: Jewel Scott, Kortney Floyd James, Dara D. Méndez, Ragan Johnson, Esa M. Davis
ID: openalex.W4363674381
URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ssmqr.2023.100268

The findings highlight that many Black women may use or resist self-silencing as
a vigilance-based coping strategy to preserve their mental and physical well-
being. We present measurement considerations for research on health impacts of
racism and other forms of oppression.
