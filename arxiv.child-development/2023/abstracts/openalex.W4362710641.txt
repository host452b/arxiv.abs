Title: Dynamics of Identity of Adolescents with Different Character Accentuations
Authors: A. A. Kiseleva, Елена Ивановна Миронова, O. A. Osipenok, M. Y. Kuzmin
ID: openalex.W4362710641
URL: https://doi.org/10.26516/2304-1226.2023.43.33

The article discusses the features of the dynamics of the identity of
adolescents with different types of character accentuations. The authors suggest
that the prevalence of certain accentuations of the character of adolescents
have an impact on the formation of identity in general. Based on the results of
empirical research, the authors come to the conclusion that character
accentuations affect the dynamics of identity in different ways. For adolescents
with a high severity of hyperthymic accentuation, a decrease in the
manifestations of an identity crisis is more characteristic; persons with
emotive, exalted and cyclothymic accentuations showed an increase in crisis
manifestations of identity. The results obtained can be applied in the
development and improvement of methods of psychological influence, to create
individual programs of psychological support for adolescents with various
manifestations of character accentuations.
