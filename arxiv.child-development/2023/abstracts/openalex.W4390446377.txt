Title: The dynamics of self-identity among habaib lovers: An interpretative phenomenological analysis of Indonesian Generation Z
Authors: Dedy Surya, Zulkarnain Zulkarnain, Dessy Rahmawati, Ranji Julian Santika
ID: openalex.W4390446377
URL: http://dx.doi.org/10.32505/inspira.v4i2.5850

This article explores the self-identity dynamics of Generation Z habaib lovers
in Indonesia. Data were collected through 60 to 120 minutes of in-depth
interviews with ten members of Habib-led assemblies identified as part of
Generation Z (aged 12-27 years) selected through a purposive sampling technique.
A qualitative approach was chosen, with Interpretative Phenomenological Analysis
(IPA) with the help of NVivo 12 software to organize the data. Three subordinate
themes emerged: the motivation to become a habaib lover, the process of
searching for self-identity, and cohesiveness towards the habaib assembly. These
themes are related to the formation of self-identity in Generation Z. This study
concludes that the self-identity of the youth members of the community is shaped
and catalyzed by the meaning of holiness of the habaib as a prophet's descendant
figure. The findings contribute to developing the concept of Muslim youth self-
identity through empirical studies on adolescent self-identity formation.
