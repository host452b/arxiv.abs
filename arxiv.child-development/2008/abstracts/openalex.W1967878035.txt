The analyses suggest that acculturation is associated with weight gain, and that
neighborhood characteristics are only associated with BMI among Hispanics.
However, we suggest that changes in body size currently interpreted as post-
migration effects of acculturation to U.S. norms may in fact reflect changes in
norms that are taking place internationally.
