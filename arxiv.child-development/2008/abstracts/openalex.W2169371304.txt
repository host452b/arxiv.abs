Title: The Generation of Postmemory
Authors: Marianne Hirsch
ID: openalex.W2169371304
URL: https://doi.org/10.1215/03335372-2007-019

Postmemory describes the relationship of the second generation to powerful,
often traumatic, experiences that preceded their births but that were
nevertheless transmitted to them so deeply as to seem to constitute memories in
their own right. Focusing on the remembrance of the Holocaust, this essay
elucidates the generation of postmemory and its reliance on photography as a
primary medium of transgenerational transmission of trauma. Identifying tropes
that most potently mobilize the work of postmemory, it examines the role of the
family as a space of transmission and the function of gender as an idiom of
remembrance.
