Title: Aboriginal epistemologies and new teacher induction: The context of a bi-epistemic research endeavour
Authors: Lorenzo Cherubini, Julian Kitchen, John Hodson
ID: openalex.W2127877229
URL: https://doi.org/10.26522/brocked.v18i1.113

Teacher preparation and induction have been the subject of much scholarly
investigation in the mainstream, however, relatively little research has focused
on Aboriginal epistemologies and new teacher experiences. From a bi-epistemic
perspective, this research project attends precisely to this void in the
scholarship. The project represents an innovative partnership between an
Aboriginal research centre, an esteemed elder, university scholars, graduate
students, and Aboriginal educators in Ontario. Furthermore, it calls attention
to the successes of new Aboriginal teachers and the dedication they exemplify to
advance Aboriginal epistemologies and student learning from culturally-sensitive
perspectives.
