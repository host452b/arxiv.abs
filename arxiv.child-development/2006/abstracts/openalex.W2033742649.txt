Title: Young People's Constructions of the Self
Authors: Pat O’Connor
ID: openalex.W2033742649
URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/0038038506058437

This article looks at the ways in which young people reflexively construct their
self within a rapidly changing society. Drawing on texts written by young people
aged 14–17 years, it explores the existence of patterns identified by theorists
of late modernity as regards relationships, fateful moments, a search for
authenticity, life plans and life styles and looks at gender-differentiated
trends in these areas drawing on a ‘weak cultural feminist tradition’ (Evans,
1995: 91).These texts are part of a sub-sample of approximately 34,000 texts
written by young people in a school context in response to an invitation to
‘tell their life stories’ by writing a page ‘describing themselves and the
Ireland they inhabit’.The article suggests that gender is a repressed but
crucially important framework in the construction of young people's sense of
self, while also identifying areas where consumer society is eroding gender
difference.
