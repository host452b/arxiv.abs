Title: Liminality and Fantasy in Marie Darrieussecq, Marie NDiaye and Marie Redonnet
Authors: Jean H. Duffy
ID: openalex.W2028390154
URL: https://doi.org/10.1353/mln.0.0172

Drawing on the anthropological theory of Arnold van Gennep and Victor Turner,
the article will explore the recurrent references in the work of Darrieussecq,
NDiaye and Redonnet to liminal locations, times, characters and conditions and
will show that analysis of this allusional pattern not only sheds light on the
role played by the fantastic in their texts, but also permits more precise
identification both of shared thematic preoccupations and of personal priorities
and inflections.
