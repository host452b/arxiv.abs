Title: Imaging vulnerability: the iconography of climate change
Authors: Kate Manzo
ID: openalex.W2037272626
URL: https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1475-4762.2009.00887.x

This paper explores the iconography of climate change in contemporary climate
action campaigns in the UK. I aim to show how sample images are simultaneously
scientific denotations of global warming and cultural connotations of danger and
vulnerability. I further demonstrate that while similar images are associated
with different agendas and geographical visions, they attach to a shared
discourse of vulnerability that has Western (colonial) roots. The paper
concludes with an overview of possible ways for climate action campaigns to
effectively convey their political messages without recycling colonial visions
of the world.
