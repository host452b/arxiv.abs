Title: The Bracey Report on the Condition of Public Education, 2009
Authors: Gerald W. Bracey
ID: openalex.W1563195666
URL: https://scholar.colorado.edu/concern/defaults/ms35t9473

This year’s Bracey Report identifies and discusses the research support for what
the author considers to be three of the most important assumptions about how to
reform public education: 1. High-quality schools can eliminate the achievement
gap between whites and minorities. 2. Mayoral control of public schools is an
improvement over the more common elected board governance systems. 3. Higher
standards will improve the performance of public schools.
