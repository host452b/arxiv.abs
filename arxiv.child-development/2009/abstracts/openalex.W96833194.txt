Title: From Russia with Blat: Can Informal Networks Help Modernize Russia?
Authors: Alena Ledeneva
ID: openalex.W96833194
URL: https://doi.org/10.1353/sor.2009.0052

Since the collapse of the Soviet Union, Moscow has become a global city with a
vibrant urban and cultural life-one of the most expensive capitals in the world
with famous clubs and restaurants, as well as one of the most popular
destinations for city workers and diplomats. Has corruption been instrumental in
Moscow's development? The answer is complicated and in many ways a matter of
definitions. It depends on whether one considers informal practices-inherited
from Soviet times as well as new ones-as corrupt and how one conceptualizes
corruption. I will illustrate some of these complications for the case of the
Soviet practice of blat, explain its "monetization" and its evolving
relationship with corruption in the post-Soviet transition, and analyze the role
of informal networks in present-day Russia.
