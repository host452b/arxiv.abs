Title: Digital Natives vs. Digital Immigrants: Myth or Reality?
Authors: Ravi Rikhye, Sean P. Cook, Zane L. Berge
ID: openalex.W2985744981
URL: http://hdl.handle.net/11603/16244

Marc Prensky (1998; 2001a; 200b) argues that students today, digital natives as
he calls them, having grown up in the Digital Age, learn differently from their
predecessors, or digital immigrants as he terms them. As such, the pedagogical
tools we use to educate the Natives are outdated. Intuitively it seems that
Prensky is correct: few people who teach digital natives fail to note their
students seem to think and learn differently. Attractive as this thesis is,
there is little evidence to support the proposition. That does not mean Prensky
is wrong. He is onto something, perhaps something seminal, regarding today’s
pedagogies and the need to change them. But further research is required before
we can conclude with any certainty that digital natives learn differently.
