Title: But that was in another country: agents and intercultural empathy
Authors: Ruth Aylett, Natalie Vannini, Elisabeth André, Ana Paiva, Sibylle Enz
ID: openalex.W1936853119
URL: http://sure.sunderland.ac.uk/view/creators/Aylett=3ARuth=3A=3A.html>,

This paper discusses the development of a believable agent-based educational
application designed to develop inter-cultural empathy for 13-14 year old
students. It considers relevant work in cultural taxonomy and adaptation to
other cultures as well as work showing that users are sensitive to the perceived
culture of believable interactive characters. It discusses how an existing
affective agent architecture was developed to model culturally- specific agent
behaviour. Finally, it considers the role of interaction modalities in
supporting an empathic engagement with culturally-specific characters.
