Title: Narrative Inquiry as a Decolonising Methodology
Authors: Amani K. Hamdan Alghamdi
ID: openalex.W1671319664
URL: https://doi.org/10.5070/d452000652

As a distinct form of qualitative research, narrative can be used as a method of
inquiry in order to examine past experiences and decolonise minds regarding the
“still persistent colonial mission” (Willinsky in Abdi &amp; Richardson, 2008,
p. viii). Narrative acts as a lens through which we see anew – it is a means to
explore unfamiliar sociohistorical context. A significant characteristic of
narrative is that it can allow for new meanings and diverse ways of knowing to
emerge. In this paper, I highlight how I use narrative as a decolonising
methodology in which, according to Edward Said (1978), indigenous people are
responsible to provide their narratives to counter the perspective of outsiders.
In particular, I include Arab Muslim women’s narratives that counter Orientalist
perceptions of Muslim women as passive victims of their faith.
