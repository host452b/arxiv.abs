Title: Transmedia Storytelling: Implicit Consumers, Narrative Worlds, and Branding in Contemporary Media Production
Authors: Carlos Alberto Scolari
ID: openalex.W2152274359
URL: http://hdl.handle.net/10854/2867

Many concepts have been developed to describe the convergence of media,
languages, and formats in contemporary media systems. This article is a
theoretical reflection on “transmedia storytelling” from a perspective that
integrates semiotics and narratology in the context of media studies. After
dealing with the conceptual chaos around transmedia storytelling, the article
analyzes how these new multimodal narrative structures create different implicit
consumers and construct a narrative world. The analysis includes a description
of the multimedia textual structure created around the Fox television series 24.
Finally, the article analyzes transmedia storytelling from the perspective of a
semiotics of branding.
