Title: Factores de riesgo y protectores en el suicidio. Un estudio de casos y controles mediante la autopsia psicológica
Authors: Jose M. Antón-San-Martín, Eduardo Sánchez-Guerrero, Lucía Pérez-Costillas, Ma-Teresa Labajos-Manzanares, Yolanda de Diego‐Otero
ID: openalex.W2100132616
URL: https://doi.org/10.6018/analesps.29.3.175701

Rates, methods and some of the risk factors of suicide seem to depend on
geographical and cultural differences, which involve conducting research in
specific high-risk areas facing the implementation of preventive strategies.
This study seeks to determine the risk and protective factors of suicide in the
region of Antequera-Málaga (Spain). Is performed on a case-control study using
the psychological autopsy of 40 cases of suicide and 80 population controls
matched on gender, age and area of residence. The multivariate model found is
composed of the following factors: the antecedents of suicide and mental
disorders aggregate in families, a diagnosis of personality disorder and family
conflict in the month prior to suicide. These factors discriminate risk
populations facing the implementation of preventive programs.
