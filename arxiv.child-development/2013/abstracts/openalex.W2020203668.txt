Title: Cultural features of performance in Norwegian children’s narratives
Authors: Trude Hoel
ID: openalex.W2020203668
URL: https://doi.org/10.7577/nbf.473

This article presents part of a research project whose aim is to investigate
Norwegian 6–7-year-old children’s language use in story-telling. The data
consist of the children’s oral texts based on a wordless picture book. By
studying creative and stylistic features of the children’s narratives, I attempt
to shed light on cultural interaction and socio-cultural factors as fundamental
contributors to those narratives. The aim of this article is to contribute to
the exploration of a culturally anchored perspective on narratives in light of
the reading culture prevailing at Norwegian kindergartens.
