Title: The Role of Institutional Agents in Promoting Transfer Access
Authors: Alicia C. Dowd, Jenny Pak, Estela Mara Bensimon
ID: openalex.W1671612568
URL: https://doi.org/10.14507/epaa.v21n15.2013

A growing body of research points to the important role played by “institutional
agents” in facilitating college access and success for students from non-
dominant racial-ethnic and low socioeconomic status groups. Applying attachment
theory, this study adds to that literature by demonstrating how institutional
agents can provide a secure base, in a psychological sense, for such low-status
college students in the United States to make successful postsecondary
transitions and develop collegiate identities. Based on the life stories of 10
low-status students who successfully transferred from a community college to a
selective college or university, our narrative analysis depicts the students’
collegiate identity development and how college practitioners in positions of
authority were instrumental in raising their collegiate aspirations. The results
demonstrate the role of college practitioners in facilitating students’
postsecondary transitions and warrant the professional development of faculty
and administrators as institutional agents. We conclude by providing resources
for practitioners to develop their professional knowledge and capacity to act as
institutional agents.
