Title: Cultivation Effects of Media on Perceptions of Ideal Masculinity and Identity
Authors: Christopher F Clemens
ID: openalex.W2148971426
URL: https://opencommons.uconn.edu/dissertations/70

The current study utilized a survey approach using a convenience. Participants
were asked to answer a battery of questions about media use, masculine ideal
perceptions, and identification with masculine norms. Results showed that
general media use positively predicted the perceptions of the ideal man’s drive
for success, power, and competition. Contrary to cultivation research,
television use found no significant relationships. Results also indicated that
the ideal man’s conformity to masculine norms significantly predicted one’s own
conformity to masculine norms. Many gender differences were present for media
use, ideal perceptions, and identification with masculine norms. A structural
equation model is presented with all findings. Implications, limitations, and
future research directions are discussed.
