Title: Uxoricide in Pregnancy: Ancient Greek Domestic Violence in Evolutionary Perspective
Authors: Susan Deacy, Fiona McHardy
ID: openalex.W2146391083
URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/147470491301100505

Previous studies of ancient Greek examples of uxoricide in pregnancy have
concluded that the theme is used to suggest tyrannical abuse of power and that
the violence is a product of the patriarchal nature of ancient society. This
article uses evolutionary analyses of violence during pregnancy to argue that
the themes of sexual jealousy and uncertainty over paternity are as crucial as
the theme of power to an understanding of these examples and that the examples
can be seen as typical instances of spousal abuse as it occurs in all types of
society.
