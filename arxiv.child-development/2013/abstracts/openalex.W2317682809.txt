Title: Marginal but Modern: Young Nepali Labour Migrants in India
Authors: Jeevan Sharma
ID: openalex.W2317682809
URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/1103308813506307

Migration to India has been one of the key livelihood strategies amongst
marginal households in the middle hills of Nepal. Based on ethnographic
fieldwork in western and far-western hill villages in Nepal, including tracing
the experience of young men who take on adult responsibility to travel to find
work opportunities in Mumbai and Nainital in India, this article discusses how
the steady flow of marginal migrants from the middle hills is shaped and
sustained by gendered and generational considerations. An intriguing aspect of
young migrants’ lives in India is their participation in consumption of modern
commodities despite their marginal position and difficult working condition.
