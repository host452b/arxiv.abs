Title: Poor Adherence to National and International Breastfeeding Duration Targets in an Australian Longitudinal Cohort
Authors: Alexis Hure, Jennifer R. Powers, Catherine Chojenta, Julie Byles, Deborah Loxton
ID: openalex.W2037771257
URL: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0054409

While national breastfeeding rates are typically evaluated in relation to the
infant, a novel component of our study is that we have assessed maternal
adherence to breastfeeding duration targets and the longitudinal feeding
practices of women with more than one child. Separate evaluations of maternal
and infant breastfeeding rates are important as they differ in their
implications for public health policy and practice.
