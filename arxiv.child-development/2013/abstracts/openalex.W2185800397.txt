Title: Emotion, Embodied Mind and the Therapeutic Aspects of Musical Experience in Everyday Life
Authors: Dylan van der Schyff
ID: openalex.W2185800397
URL: http://dx.doi.org/10.56883/aijmt.2013.470

The capacity for music to function as a force for bio-cognitive organisation is
considered in clinical and everyday contexts. Given the deeply embodied nature
of such therapeutic responses to music, it is argued that cognitivist approaches
may be insufficient to fully explain music’s affective power. Following this, an
embodied approach is considered, where the emotional-affective response to music
is discussed in terms of primary bodily systems and the innate cross-modal
perceptive capacities of the embodied human mind. It is suggested that such an
approach may extend the largely cognitivist view taken by much of contemporary
music psychology and philosophy of music by pointing the way towards a
conception of musical meaning that begins with our most primordial interactions
with the world.
