Title: Organizational Identity and Organizational Identification
Authors: Hongwei He, Andrew D. Brown
ID: openalex.W2005421370
URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601112473815

In this article, we present an overview of the literatures on organizational
identity and organizational identification. We provide overviews of four major
approaches to organizational identity: functionalist, social constructionist,
psychodynamic, and postmodern. The literature on organizational identification,
by contrast, exhibits greater consensus due to the hegemonic power of social
identity theory, and is predominantly functionalist. We review recent research
on organizational identification regarding performance outcomes and antecedents
(mainly focusing on leadership and the social exchange perspective), and in
relation to change and virtual contexts. Some suggestions for further research
are then offered. Finally an overview of the articles in this special issue is
presented.
