Title: Understanding the Journey Through Homelessness: A Hermeneutic Study of Women Without Children
Authors: Joseph C. Osuji, Sandra P. Hirst
ID: openalex.W1820196453
URL: https://doi.org/10.7870/cjcmh-2013-017

This study explored the meaning of the experience of homelessness and exiting
homelessness among women without children. Convenience and snowball sampling
techniques were used to recruit 12 women in an urban centre in Canada. Texts
resulting from audiotaped interviews, participant observations, and reflective
journal entries constituted data for analysis. Gadamerian hermeneutics informed
the interpretive method used for analysis. The analysis yielded 5 subthemes that
described the journey: (a) loss of self at home: the trigger; (b) non-feeling of
“at-homeness”: dissociation; (c) disconnection and aloneness: homelessness; (d)
simulating home: transitional shelter living; and (e) finding oneself:
hopefulness. Findings suggest that exiting homelessness for women was a journey
in search of hope, and reconnection with the self and others. This perspective
suggests a new approach for policy and practice.
