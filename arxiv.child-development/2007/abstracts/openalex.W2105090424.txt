Title: From Practice to Theory, Or What Is a White Woman Anyway?
Authors: Catharine A. MacKinnon
ID: openalex.W2105090424
URL: https://doi.org/10.2307/j.ctv1pdrpw3.6

It is common to say that something is good in theory but not in practice. I
always want to say, then it is not such a good theory, is it? To be good in
theory but not in practice posits a relation between theory and practice that
places theory prior to practice, both methodologically and normatively, as if
theory is a terrain unto itself. The conventional image of the relation between
the two is first theory, then practice. You have an idea, then act on it. In
legal academia you theorize, then try to get some practitioner to put it into
practice. To be more exact, you 'read law review articles, then write more law
review articles. The closest most legal academics come to practice is teaching-
their students, most of whom will practice, being regarded by many as an
occupational hazard to their theorizing.
