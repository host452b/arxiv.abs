Title: Personal and lifestyle characteristics predictive of the consumption of fast foods in Australia
Authors: Philip Mohr, Carlene Wilson, Kirsten I. Dunn, Emily Brindal, Gary Wittert
ID: openalex.W2170332957
URL: https://doi.org/10.1017/s1368980007000109

The effects for age suggest that fast-food take-away consumption is associated
with a general cultural shift in eating practices; individual differences in
attitudinal and lifestyle characteristics constitute additional, cumulative,
predictive factors. The role of advertising and the reasons for the lesser
explanatory value of the eat-in models are important targets for further
research.
