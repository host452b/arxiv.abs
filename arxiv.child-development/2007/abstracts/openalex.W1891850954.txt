Title: An Exploratory Analysis of Children's Daily Time-Use and Activity Patterns Using the Child Development Supplement (CDS) to the US Panel Study of Income Dynamics (PSID)
Authors: Rachel B. Copperman, Chandra R. Bhat
ID: openalex.W1891850954
URL: http://hdl.handle.net/2152/23831

This research paper examines the weekday and weekend activity participation
characteristics of school-going children. Specifically, the research focuses on
the overall time-use of children in different types of activities, as well as on
several dimensions characterizing the context of participation in activities.
These include the temporal (day of week and participation duration), spatial
(location), with-whom (i.e., accompanying individuals), and episode sequencing
dimensions. The data for our analysis is drawn from the 2002 Child Development
Supplement to the Panel Study of Income Dynamics.
