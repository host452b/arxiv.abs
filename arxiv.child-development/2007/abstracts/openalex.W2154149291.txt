Title: Looking Back to Look Forward: Understanding the Present by Revisiting the Past: An Australian Perspective
Authors: Jan Turbill, Brian Cambourne
ID: openalex.W2154149291
URL: https://ro.uow.edu.au/edupapers/653

Cambourne and Turbill trace the growth, change and finally marginalisation of
progressive approaches to literacy education by examining whole language
philosophy in Australia from the 1960s to the present. Using a critical lens,
Cambourne and Turbill describe how whole language has been positioned throughout
the last nearly 50 years in terms of curriculum, pedagogy, and assessment.
Cambourne and Turbill offer a personal history of whole language in Australia
and draw connections of the educational changes occurring in their country to
other western democracies. Their insights are valuable in order to examine other
grass roots programs and to better understand how politics impact educational
movements
