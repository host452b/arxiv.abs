Title: Virtual Consumption: A Second Life for Earth?
Authors: Albert Lin
ID: openalex.W1484367797
URL: https://digitalcommons.law.byu.edu/lawreview/vol2008/iss1/2

Consumption is at the root of many of the world's greatest environmental
challenges, yet laws or policies that directly address are rare. Virtual worlds
such as Second Life offer the intriguing prospect of displacing a substantial
amount of real-world without running afoul of the political and economic
obstacles that proposals to reduce often face. In the interactive online reality
of worlds, players adopt an avatar and participate in an electronic world that
mirrors the real world in striking ways. As this Article explains, worlds offer
opportunities, experiences, and pleasures that satisfy many of the basic
motivations that drive modern consumption. Yet while virtual consumption may be
a promising substitute for real consumption, worlds also present dangers that
require careful reflection before we wholeheartedly embrace them as a tool for
protecting the environment.
