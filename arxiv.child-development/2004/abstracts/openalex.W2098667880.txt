Title: WIC Participation, Breastfeeding Practices, and Well-Child Care Among Unmarried, Low-Income Mothers
Authors: Pinka Chatterji, Jeanne Brooks‐Gunn
ID: openalex.W2098667880
URL: https://doi.org/10.2105/ajph.94.8.1324

We estimated the effect of Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women,
Infants, and Children (WIC) participation in 1999 to 2000 on breastfeeding
initiation and duration and well-child care. We applied multivariate regression
to a sample of 2136 unmarried, low-income, urban mothers from the Fragile
Families and Child Wellbeing Study. WIC participation was associated with small
increases in the probabilities of initiating breastfeeding and having had at
least 4 well-child visits since birth-behaviors that benefit infants beyond the
newborn period-but not with breastfeeding duration.
