Title: Paradoxes of Health and Equality: When a Boy Becomes a Girl
Authors: Noa Ben-Asher
ID: openalex.W2127636178
URL: https://digitalcommons.law.yale.edu/yjlf/vol16/iss2/5

In the fall of 2000, six-year-old male Zachary from a small town in Ohio,
claimed that s/he was a girl and requested, from now on, to be called Aurora.
When the child's parents honored this unusual wish and made efforts to make
official the child's feminine identity, the case turned into a custody battle
between the parents and the state of Ohio. Although the child was occasionally
treated as a girl at home from the age of two, the attempt to register the child
in public school as a girl motivated the state dissolution of this family. At
the conclusion of this legal dispute, the child was removed to foster care, with
the expressed hope that the child would enjoy future prosperity as a normal male
boy. Can a parent be legally required to control the gender of a child? What is
the legal justification of a forced disintegration of a family? This Essay will
try to address these issues, raised by this unusual legal dispute between
parents who believed that they should raise their child as a girl, and a state
agency that insisted on a masculine upbringing as a boy..
