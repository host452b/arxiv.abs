Title: Bone stable isotope evidence for infant feeding in Mediaeval England
Authors: Simon Mays, Michael P. Richards, Benjamin T. Fuller
ID: openalex.W2121763387
URL: https://doi.org/10.1017/s0003598x00091067

This paper is a first study of duration of breastfeeding using bone stable
isotopes in infants in a British palaeopopulation, from the deserted Mediaeval
village of Wharram Percy, England. Nitrogen stable isotope analysis suggests
cessation of breastfeeding between 1 and 2 years of age. Comparison with
Mediaeval documentary sources suggests that recommendations of physicians
regarding infant feeding may have influenced common practice in this period.
