Title: Drum and voice: aesthetics and social process on the Rai Coast of Papua New Guinea
Authors: James Leach
ID: openalex.W2048096412
URL: https://doi.org/10.1111/1467-9655.00130

Reite people on the Rai Coast of Papua New Guinea describe a large ceremonial
drum (a garamut ) as a man. In its construction, a garamut is the focus of a
process which brings forth a form of social relations, as well as the object
itself. I ask, ‘What language might we use to describe such a creation?’. In
recent discussions of art, the concepts of aesthetics and technology have been
central. Drawing briefly on this literature, I approach an ethnographic
description of garamut construction as revealing the particular way in which
Reite people generate their social world. The construction is based upon mythic
knowledge. This shapes the mode in which persons as gendered agents, and with
particular identities, are made to appear. A specific ‘aesthetic’ scheme is thus
apparent. The emergence of the garamut cannot be seen as the end of the process.
The object has effect within and upon the relations given form by its emergence.
Formation is ongoing, with becoming built in.
