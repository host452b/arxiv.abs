Title: What nourishes the spirit of adolescents in the classroom? : a qualitative study into the gateways of adolescents' souls /
Authors: Hilary Brown
ID: openalex.W2196678551
URL: http://hdl.handle.net/10464/1358

This thesis investigated the question, "What nourishes the&#13;\nadolescent
spirit in the classroom?" Action research was conducted&#13;\nby the teacher of
16 grade 8 participants. By undergoing Kessler's&#13;\n(2000) "Passages
Program," the students participated in 6 sessions&#13;\nwhich exposed them to a
holistic intrapersonal curriculum. Student&#13;\njournal responses were
documented after each session. The action&#13;\nresearcher also kept journal
reflections after each session. Upon&#13;\ncompletion of the 6 sessions, a
postinterview was conducted which&#13;\nposed the research question.&#13;\nThe
research found that the adolescent spirit gets nourished&#13;\nthrough
encouragement by the teacher, peers, and parents. This&#13;\nincreases their
competency, which increases their confidence, and&#13;\nultimately their self-
esteem goes up, which affects their selfconcept.&#13;\nIn addition, the role of
the teacher permeates every aspect&#13;\nof what nourishes the adolescent spirit
in the classroom. In&#13;\naddition to the encouragement of the student, how a
teacher teaches&#13;\nplays a vital role. A holistic approach to teaching
provided the best&#13;\natmosphere for the adolescent. It promoted creativity
and choice,&#13;\nwhich stimulated the spirit of the adolescent. By working from
a&#13;\nholistic philosophy, the teacher/action researcher created
an&#13;\nenvironment conducive to teaching the whole person,
which&#13;\nultimately nourished the participants' spirit. The research
highlighted that in order for this type of environment to exist
the&#13;\nteacher needs to make a conscious and deliberate effort to
look&#13;\nwithin and develop their inner self before they can begin to
promote&#13;\nthis type of classroom for the adolescents they teach.
When&#13;\nteachers and students develop an inner life together, they can
begin&#13;\nto work in harmony to achieve an atmosphere where the teaching
and&#13;\nlearning environment becomes one seamless transaction. Only
then&#13;\ncan one's whole potential be realized.
