Title: The role of initiation schools in the identity formation of Southern Ndebele adolescent boys
Authors: David Khuwa Mabena
ID: openalex.W1694258646
URL: http://hdl.handle.net/10500/17271

This study investigates the role of initiation schools in the identity
formation&#13;\nof Southern Ndebele adolescent boys. The subsequent influence of
initiation&#13;\nschools on scholastic achievement was also researched.&#13;\nA
qualitative research method was used to give a scientific support to
the&#13;\nfindings made in literature. Interviewing and interview schedules
were&#13;\nemployed as data-collecting techniques. Interviews were conducted
with 10&#13;\ninitiates from the Ndzundza and the Manala initiation
schools.&#13;\nThe positive outcomes of this research can, however, not be
generalised, but&#13;\nrather indicate possible tendencies that initiation
schools play a significant&#13;\nrole in the identity formation of Southern
Ndebele adolescent boys.
