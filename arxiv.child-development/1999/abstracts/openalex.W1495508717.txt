Title: Bodies of vital matter : notions of life force and transcendence in traditional southern Italy
Authors: Per Binde
ID: openalex.W1495508717
URL: http://hdl.handle.net/2077/29000

The aim of this study is to investigate beliefs and practices relating to
vitality, illness and death in traditional Southern Italy. My prime \nargument
is that many of these beliefs and practices relate to just a few interconnected
sets of notions. A basic presumption for the \nanalysis of the material is that
vital force is construed as a quality or substance which can be lost as well as
gained. A first set of notions \nconcerns losses leading to weakness, illness or
death, caused by another person’s appropriation of vitality. A second set
includes ideas of how force of life might be gained from external sources,
thereby reinvigorating the body. A third set concerns the inevitable situation
in which physical life can no longer be sustained and death occurs.
Transcendence beyond the carnal realm is symbolically achieved; a new and
incorruptible body is created, or death is construed as giving new life. The
study covers such topics as the occult transfer of mother’s milk, the evil eye,
beliefs about menstruation and witches, the cult of saints, Easter celebrations,
death rituals, burial customs and the celebration of All Souls Day.
