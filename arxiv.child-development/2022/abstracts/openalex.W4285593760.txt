Title: Women Entrepreneurs Negotiating Identities in Liminal Digital Spaces
Authors: Gráinne Kelly, Maura McAdam
ID: openalex.W4285593760
URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/10422587221115363

Our article conceptualizes the experiences of women entrepreneurs through
exploring how they negotiate an entrepreneurial identity in liminal digital
spaces. Providing empirically textured narrative portraits of women’s experience
of transitioning from employment to a digital entrepreneurial career, this
article counters the ascendant rhetoric celebrating the democratizing promise of
digital technologies. We present a more critical analysis of the experience of
self-doubt and existential precarity including the ways in which gender norms
permeate the intimate structures of women entrepreneurs’ everyday lives and
selves. We also develop the concept of liminality by illustrating how women
digital entrepreneurs cope with liminality through identity play and identity
work.
