Title: Mental images of 48–72-month-old children about the concept of mathematics
Authors: Berrin Akman, Esra Akgül, Fulya Ezmeci
ID: openalex.W4308878217
URL: https://doi.org/10.24130/eccdjecs.1967202262455

The aim of this study was to find out mental images (metaphors) of 48–72 month
old children were receiving preschool education, about the concept of
mathematics by using the metaphor technique. This study was a qualitative
research study. The following two sets of questions were asked to the children
participating in the study and their answers were noted in order to reveal their
metaphors about the concept of “mathematics:”, “What do you think mathematics is
like? Why?” and “If mathematics were a color, which color do you think it would
be? Why?”. The data were analyzed by creating codes and categories. As a result
of the research; It was seen that children compared mathematics to paper and
pencil even in early childhood education period, when everything needs to be
presented by gamification. It was also observed that children formed metaphors
for mathematics that contain elements of fear and hate.
