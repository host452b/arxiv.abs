Title: JOGOS INTERDISCIPLINARES EM AULAS VIRTUAIS NOS ANOS INICIAIS
Authors: MARIA DE FATIMA VILHENA DA SILVA, Amanda Lohanna da Mota Costa, Yasmim Nazaré Moura Sarmanho, Francisco Hermes Santos da Silva, Rudinei Alves dos Santos
ID: openalex.W4392838027
URL: https://doi.org/10.29327/1137060.14-77

The study takes place from a supervised training internship for teachers in the
early years with digital technologies and also on use of games and recreations
to learn mathematics and language.Its objective is to describe how remote gaming
can contribute to interdisciplinary learning and inclusion of children in the
early grades of elementary school.This text is based on narrative research about
observations of classes on the Google Meet platform, in two 1 st grade
classrooms of elementary school in a public school in Belém-PA, Brazil.The
observations are analyzed through descriptive-interpretative means.The results
indicate that games can promote learning in different areas such as mathematics,
language and communication, keeping the child's interest.We conclude that remote
teaching, with the use of games and recreations in the early grades, can
significantly contribute to interdisciplinary learning when the challenges for
children highlight less content-oriented classes and greater stimulus of
imagination, creativity and memory in an educational context of interaction and
inclusion.
