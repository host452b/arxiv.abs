Title: Look and Read: BBC School Television and Literacy Teaching 1957-1979
Authors: Steven Barclay
ID: openalex.W4289836938
URL: https://doi.org/10.18146/view.280

The BBC began broadcasting school television in 1957. As school television
developed in the 1960s, the BBC engaged with educational research and addressed
national educational problems. Pedagogy in UK schools was becoming more
progressive, and literacy was one of its most important and complex elements. UK
Schools had struggled to achieve universal literacy among 7–9-year-olds. The
series <em>Look and Read</em> and <em>Words and Pictures</em> used literacy
research, adapted to the best method of presentation by television. The series
took advantage of the developing televisual genres of children’s drama and
animation.
