Title: Disconnectivity synced with identity cultivation: adolescent narratives of digital disconnection
Authors: Mehri Agai
ID: openalex.W4307810823
URL: https://doi.org/10.1093/jcmc/zmac025

Abstract This article elucidates adolescent digital disconnection through the
lens of narrative identity development to answer the research question: “How do
adolescent disconnection experiences play into ongoing identity development
processes?” The study draws from qualitative interviews with 17 Norwegian
adolescents. The findings indicate that adolescents are ambivalent about their
relationships with social networking sites, producing ripple effects on their
motivation and disconnection practices. The findings are explained through the
cultivation of narrative identity. Thus, this article proposes that adolescent
digital disconnection is aligned with identity formation, reflected in narrative
themes and corresponding features. By detailing how ongoing identity
construction processes underpin the need and ability to disconnect, this article
contributes a developmental perspective to the digital disconnection literature.
