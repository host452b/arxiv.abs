Title: Perceived stress, recent stressors, and distress in relation to sleep disturbance and duration among middle-aged and older Asian immigrants
Authors: Ruijia Chen, Natalie Slopen, Sunmin Lee
ID: openalex.W4312119681
URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.sleh.2022.11.005

Perceived stress, stressors, and distress were associated with a higher
prevalence of sleep disturbance. Moreover, perceived stress and distress had
stronger associations with times in bed among recent immigrants. Future sleep
health research in Asian Americans should consider the important role of stress
and distress, especially among recent immigrants.
