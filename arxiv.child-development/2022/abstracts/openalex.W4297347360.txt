Title: Physical Activity and Sleeping Duration Among Adolescents in the US
Authors: Marc Ganz, Menachem Jacobs, Christopher Alessandro, Samuel Sabzanov, Avrohom Karp
ID: openalex.W4297347360
URL: https://doi.org/10.7759/cureus.29669

Sleep plays a critical role in the development of adolescents. Identifying the
factors influencing adolescent sleep duration is a critical public health
concern. Our study was designed to look for associations that may affect sleep
duration in adolescent students. A cross-sectional research method was used to
evaluate a dataset of thousands of adolescent students who were polled. The
study assessed whether there was a correlation between exercise and length of
sleep. The research showed a statistically significant positive correlation
between the studied variables. We found that while demographic variables modify
this association, a positive correlation exists even after controlling for these
confounders. Our findings suggest that promoting physical activity can increase
the quantity of sleep among adolescents.
