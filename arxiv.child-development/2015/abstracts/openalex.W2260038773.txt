Title: (Re)Claiming Voices: Digital Storytelling and Second Language Learners
Authors: Yurimi Grigsby, Carolyn Theard-Griggs, Christopher Lilly
ID: openalex.W2260038773
URL: https://doi.org/10.1515/atd-2015-0034

Abstract With almost five million English language learners in the United
States, digital storytelling is increasingly being used in second language
learning classrooms. As a teaching and learning strategy, digital storytelling
can promote critical thinking, connect new content with prior knowledge, enhance
memory, and foster confidence and motivation for learning. Digital stories
possess unique narrative qualities that often center on identity negotiation and
the ways culturally and linguistically diverse students make meaning out of
their lives. Fostering hands-on, active learning, digital storytelling is an
interactive way to include culturally and linguistically diverse students’
voices in a curriculum that may not easily represent them. Practical
implementation of digital storytelling is included.
