Title: TV Got Better: Netflix’s Original Programming Strategies and the On-Demand Television Transition
Authors: Chuck Tryon
ID: openalex.W2178209173
URL: https://doi.org/10.3998/mij.15031809.0002.206

This paper analyzes the promotional strategies of Netflix, arguing that the
company reinforces what Pierre Bourdieu has called the discourses of
distinction. In particular, the streaming service highlights what Tryon calls
the promises of plenitude, participation, prestige, and personalization. Netflix
highlights these discourses in part through its ongoing engagement with
subscription cable channel HBO, and in part through promotional materials such
as its TV Got Better campaign, which sought to naturalize viewing practices such
as binge watching as being part of a technological and narrative cutting edge.
