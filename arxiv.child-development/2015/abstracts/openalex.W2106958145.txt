Title: The Influence of Informal Music Education in Teacher Formation: An Autoethnography
Authors: Rohan Nethsinghe
ID: openalex.W2106958145
URL: https://doi.org/10.46743/2160-3715/2012.1701

In this paper I explore how my musical background, teaching skills,
understanding and knowledge as well as music-making abilities and skills, have
formed my current self as musician, teacher and researcher. An
autoethnographical method is used to investigate my background, including the
different modes of music education I received. From this qualitative study, it
was possible to find that my interests along with the methods of interpretations
I practice in the field of multicultural music are influenced by and formulated
through my appreciation and understandings of and beliefs gained from education.
Most importantly, they are shaped by the social context, cultural placing, and
life experiences.
