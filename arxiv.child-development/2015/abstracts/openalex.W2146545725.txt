Title: Visual Evidence in Qualitative Research: The Role of Videorecording
Authors: Sorrel Penn‐Edwards
ID: openalex.W2146545725
URL: https://doi.org/10.46743/2160-3715/2004.1928

Videorecording allows the researcher to record and replay the pictures and sound
of an event. As such, it can be a valuable research tool. Nevertheless, it is
not just a simple measuring instrument. As a qualitative research data gathering
tool, video recordings should be authenticated. Researchers should indicate
clearly the role of this tool in their work and discuss the factors that may
have an influence on the way it is used or on the data analyzed. The substance
of these factors is shown in inventoried form. This paper discusses these and
advises how researchers may address the validity of video recording as a
qualitative research tool.
