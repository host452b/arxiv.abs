Title: Co-constructing identity in virtual worlds for children
Authors: Christoph A. Hafner
ID: openalex.W2794500669
URL: https://doi.org/10.4324/9781315726465-7

This chapter considers how such discursive interaction and joint construction
can be analysed, in the context of the co-construction of identity in online
virtual worlds for children. An important insight into the nature of discourse
in digital contexts is that such discourse is frequently co-constructed. Online
virtual worlds for children are graphically represented online spaces that
children navigate through using ‘avatar’ and where they can interact, play games
with large numbers. In particular, Zoe’s attempts to represent herself as
African American with dark brown skin colour were often frustrated as it was
difficult to trade items for her avatar that came in that skin colour. One way
to analyse the discourse of a digital environment like a virtual world would be
to treat it as a text or set of texts. However, such an approach risks
overlooking the essentially fluid and dynamic nature of the virtual world, which
is to a large extent constituted of the actions of its users.
