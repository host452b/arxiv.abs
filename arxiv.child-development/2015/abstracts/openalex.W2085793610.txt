Title: Intervention in Autism: Social Engagement Implemented by Caregivers
Authors: Adrine Carvalho dos Santos, Marilice Fernandes Garotti, Ivete Furtado Ribeiro Caldas, Cleonice Alves Bosa
ID: openalex.W2085793610
URL: https://doi.org/10.1590/1982-43272560201509

To become involved in states of social engagement with their caregivers is a
core deficit of children with autism. This study verified the effectiveness of
an intervention with caregivers for the promotion of social engagement in their
children with autism. The interactions of seven dyads were video recorded
before, during and after the intervention in order to record the duration of
eleven states of the children’ attention engagement, coded as social engagement
(SE) or non-social engagement (NSE). Eight hours of intervention were divided
into meetings with all the caregivers and into individualized monitoring. After
the intervention, significant differences were observed regarding the increase
of SE and in the decrease of NSE and these gains were maintained at follow-up.
The increase in SE was positively associated with the degree of caregivers’
adherence to the treatment. We conclude that short duration interventions can
qualify caregivers as competent mediators to increase social engagements.
