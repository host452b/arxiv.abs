Title: Unemployment and household food hardship in the economic recession
Authors: Jin Huang, Youngmi Kim, Julie Birkenmaier
ID: openalex.W2257250526
URL: https://doi.org/10.1017/s1368980015001603

More in-depth understanding of the relationship between unemployment and food
insecurity is useful to better identify and serve the at-risk population.
Connecting unemployment assistance closely to nutrition assistance could lower
the prevalence of food insecurity among unemployed households. Public policy
should better account for both episodes and duration of unemployment to reduce
food insecurity.
