Title: Reconceptualising the Gender of Fitness Doping: Performing and Negotiating Masculinity through Drug-Use Practices
Authors: Jesper Andreasson
ID: openalex.W1898261137
URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/socsci4030546

This article analyses self-portrayals and gender constructions among Swedish
male bodybuilders who are engaged in fitness doping. The empirical material
comes from a larger ethnographic investigation into gym culture. The results
show that there is a strong propensity to conform with particular gender
fantasies that rests heavily on a binary understanding of gendered, doped
bodies. However, this storyline does not apprehend the entire self-presentation
of the analysed drug users. Negotiations and inclusive subversions of
traditional gender norms are also expressed. For example, the narratives show
how the use of performance-enhancing substances makes it possible for
(heterosexual) men to approach, touch and express feelings of desire towards
other men and their bodies. As such, this practice can be viewed as a
contestation of hegemonic gender values, in which masculinity and fitness doping
are detached from a quite heterosexist understanding, and turned into a symbolic
world of homoerotic pleasure.
