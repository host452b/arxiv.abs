Title: “If I was going to die I should at least be having fun”: Travel blogs, meaning and tourist experience
Authors: Carmela Bosangit, Sally Hibbert, Scott McCabe
ID: openalex.W1247899325
URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.annals.2015.08.001

Travel blogs are an under-utilised resource for researchers of tourism
experiences. They can provide rich insights on how tourists express the
transformational effects of their experiences for the self. This study of travel
blogs by nineteen British bloggers reveals how elements of the narrative
relating to self-reflection and emotions are central to the process of
transforming their travel experiences into personally meaningful experiences.
Bloggers implicitly and explicitly express how travel contributes to self-
identity, signalling self-development. The study contributes to knowledge about
the lasting impact of long term travel on people, adding meaning symbolic of an
evolving self.
