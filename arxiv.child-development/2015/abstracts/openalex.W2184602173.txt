Title: Sexual behaviour, debut and identity among Swedish Schoolchildren
Authors: Åsa Kastbom
ID: openalex.W2184602173
URL: https://doi.org/10.3384/diss.diva-123029

Background: Sexual behaviour among schoolchildren and adolescents is a sparsely
researched area and there are delicate methodological obstacles and ethical
concerns when conducting such research. Still it is a subject that engages both
parents and professionals. A sexualized behaviour or an early sexual debut
(younger than 14 years) can be a sign of sexual abuse. It is therefore of
importance to describe what is common and what is uncommon sexual behaviour
among children and what the consequences of an early or a late sexual debut may
be for the individual upon reaching late adolescence. Adolescents who identify
themselves as lesbian, gay or bisexual (LGB) are also a group that needs further
attention and research since they are often described as having a lower quality
of life and more often experience child abuse than heterosexual teens.
