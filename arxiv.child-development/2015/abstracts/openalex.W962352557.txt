Title: LANGUAGE OF SILENCE: AN ETHNOGRAPHIC CASE STUDY OF THE EXPRESSIVE LANGUAGE SKILLS OF PRESCHOOL NATIVE AMERICAN GIRLS
Authors: Arlene Marie Hett
ID: openalex.W962352557
URL: https://scholarworks.montana.edu/xmlui/handle/1/7108

The purpose of this descriptive case study was to describe the expressive
language characteristics of the preschool Native American girl. Additionally,
the study investigated whether (a) these characteristics are specific to this
population as compared to the Anglo children in the Head Start program, (b)
these characteristics are gender specific, and (c) these characteristics
handicap the preschool Native American girl when she enters a formal education
program.
