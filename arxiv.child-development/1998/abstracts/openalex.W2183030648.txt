Title: To be Indian (hyphen) American: Communicating diaspora, identity and home.
Authors: Archana A. Pathak
ID: openalex.W2183030648
URL: http://hdl.handle.net/11244/5669

This idea of cultural fusion is best examined utilizing Jean Gebser's theory of
civilizational expression. In his text, The Ever-Present Origin (1949/1985),
Gebser explains the structures of consciousness as ways of being and structuring
perception. Utilizing Gebser's work, this dissertation presents a historical
hermeneutics of Indian-American identity, examining issues such as the concept
of model minority, frozen-in-time memory, religion as a system of cultural
preservation and the westernization of the world.
