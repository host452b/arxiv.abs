Title: Duration of child home care allowance period and school success: Differences by parental education level and ethnic origins
Authors: Markus Laaninen
ID: openalex.W4410421669
URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rssm.2025.101063

Early childhood education and care (ECEC) participation is a key mechanism for
narrowing the achievement gap between children from different family
backgrounds. ECEC particularly benefits children with immigrant backgrounds by
providing earlier exposure to the host country language, which boosts later
school performance. We employ family fixed-effects regression models and high-
quality Finnish register data to examine the association between the duration of
the child home care allowance (HCA)—a special feature of Finnish family policy
and the main counterfactual for child care services—and school success (as
measured by literacy grade at the end of elementary education), parental
education, and ethnic origins. In addition to showing that the duration of the
HCA period is negatively linked to the school success of children of less
educated mothers, this study shows that this duration is negatively associated
with the school success of children of immigrants in universal ECEC. •
Association between child home care allowance (HCA) period and school success is
studied. • HCA duration is negatively associated with school success for
children of low educated mothers and children of immigrants. • The strongest
negative association is observed for children of former-Yugoslavian ancestry. •
HCA can undermine the realization of equality of opportunity.
