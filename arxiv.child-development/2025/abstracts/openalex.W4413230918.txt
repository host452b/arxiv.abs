Title: Intervenções sobre Estratégias de Mediação de Tecnologia para Pais de Crianças Pré-Escolares: Revisão Integrativa de Literatura
Authors: António Silva, Andréia Schmidt
ID: openalex.W4413230918
URL: https://doi.org/10.9788/cp2025.2-07

The increased use of digital technologies by children has raised concerns in
diff erent sectors of society. Studies suggest that parental mediation can
mitigate negative eff ects, making it crucial to prepare parents for this role.
The aim of this study was to identify the characteristics and eff ects of
interventions targeted at parents of preschool children to promote digital
technology mediation. An integrative literature review was conducted using
empirical articles extracted from the SciELO, Web of Science, Scopus, PubMed and
PsycINFO databases on interventions with parents of children aged 0-6 years. Of
the 506 studies found, 10 were analyzed. The strategies were classifi ed into
two categories: “Strategies used by researchers” (e.g., providing support
materials, expository instruction, support/counseling, group activity) and
“Mediation strategies taught to parents” (e.g., setting rules to reduce screen
time, including alternative activities, shared reading, prohibiting meals in
front of the TV, removing electronics from children’s bedrooms). Based on these
results, it was possible to conclude that the strategies taught to parents
mainly aim at reducing screen time and that the strategies used by researchers
tend to be applied in a combined manner.
