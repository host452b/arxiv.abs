Title: Pengaruh Media Sosial Terhadap Tingkat Stres Remaja
Authors: Azzura Aidenia Ardatien Najwa, Zidni Fauziyyah Al Ghifari, Naiima Khairiya Ismah, Ati Kusmawati
ID: openalex.W4412071729
URL: https://doi.org/10.61722/jmia.v2i4.5778

Social media has become an essential aspect of modern teenagers' lives, offering
communication convenience but also introducing new mental health challenges.
This study aims to examine the influence of social media on adolescent stress
levels using a literature review approach. The findings indicate that excessive
social media use can lead to stress due to social pressure, self-comparison,
cyberbullying, and the fear of missing out (FOMO). While social media can
support self-expression and creativity, its negative effects cannot be
overlooked. Therefore, preventive strategies such as limiting screen time,
strengthening social support systems, and increasing parental involvement are
crucial in guiding teens toward healthier and more balanced digital media use.
These efforts are expected to promote better psychological well-being among
adolescents in the digital era.
