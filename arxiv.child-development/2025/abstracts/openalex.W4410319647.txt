Title: Music Education from the Perspective of the Cognitive and Communicative Dimensions as Components of the Integral Formation of Secondary School Students: A Documentary Analysis
Authors: B. Domínguez Fuentes, Nubia Neydy Hernández-Hernández, Olena Klimenko
ID: openalex.W4410319647
URL: https://doi.org/10.70747/cr.v4i1.184

Music education is recognized as a key component in the comprehensive
development of secondary school students, as it enhances cognitive and
communicative dimensions while fostering socio-emotional skills in educational
contexts. This study examines the impact of music education on the development
of socio-communicative and cognitive skills in secondary school students, aiming
to identify the implications and benefits of music as a pedagogical tool. Using
a qualitative approach with a bibliographic design, sources, and curricular
documents were reviewed to explore the relationship between music education and
students’ holistic development. The findings reveal that music education not
only promotes emotional expression and inclusion in the classroom but also that
sensory-perceptual stimulation through music contributes to holistic learning,
enhancing competencies and critical thinking skills. Integrating music into the
school curriculum is essential for developing socio-emotional skills and socio-
cognitive competencies that are crucial for academic success, supporting a well-
rounded education.
