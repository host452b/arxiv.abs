Title: Cultivating careers in agriculture: Exploring the life experiences, career motivations, and vocational identity of young Australians
Authors: Lorraine De Guzman, Melissa Ann Sullivan, Nicole McDonald
ID: openalex.W4408971156
URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/10384162251326167

Despite the clear importance of agriculture in Australia, and a diverse range of
career opportunities in the sector, knowledge of agriculture and farming
processes is declining. This study explores the vocational development
experiences of young people within the Australian agricultural industry,
highlighting the influences of life experiences, career motivations, and
vocational identity on their career decision-making process. Drawing on the
developmental-contextual model of career development, the research investigates
how individual, social, and contextual factors shape young people's motivations
to pursue agricultural careers. Through qualitative analysis of semi-structured
interviews with young people working in the cotton industry, the study
emphasises the significant role of early exposure to agriculture, family
support, and workplace culture in shaping vocational identity and career
aspirations. The findings contribute insights into the dynamic interplay between
individual and contextual factors in career development and offer practical
implications for stakeholders to nurture the next generation of agricultural
leaders.
