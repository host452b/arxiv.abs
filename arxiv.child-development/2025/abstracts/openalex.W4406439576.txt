Title: Body Composition Changes and Associations in Infants and Mothers During the First Year: Insights from a Pilot Study of the Baby-bod Project
Authors: Manoja P. Herath, Kdk Ahuja, Jeffrey M. Beckett, Sisitha Jayasinghe, Nuala M. Byrne
ID: openalex.W4406439576
URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/children12010097

Infants born to OW/OB mothers have increased %FM at birth and 3 months, which
may have consequences for their health throughout childhood and into adulthood.
Moreover, maternal prepregnancy BMI is a significant predictor of maternal
postpartum weight status and body composition and impacts the relationship
between maternal and infant body composition at 12 months postpartum. While the
findings of our pilot study underscore the importance of encouraging women of
childbearing age to maintain a healthy BMI before conception, further research
is needed to substantiate these results.
