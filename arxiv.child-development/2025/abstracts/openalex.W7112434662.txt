Title: INTERNALISASI LITERASI SOAL CERITADALAM KEMAMPUAN PEMECAHAN MASALAH MATEMATISPESERTA DIDIK DI SEKOLAH DASAR KOTA CIREBON: studi fenomenologi literasi soal cerita dalam memecahkan masalah matematis di kelas iv sekolah dasar Kota Cirebon
Authors: WIDIA NUR JANNAH, -, Tatang Herman, -, Mubiar Agustin, -, Vismaia S. Damaianti, -
ID: openalex.W7112434662
URL: 

Penelitian ini menganalisis bagaimana literasi soal cerita terinternalisasi
dalam cara berpikir siswa serta implikasinya terhadap kemampuan pemecahan
masalah matematika di kelas IV Sekolah Dasar Kota Cirebon. Penelitian
kualitatif, pendekatan fenomenologi ini dilaksanakan di tiga Sekolah Penggerak
(SDN SM, KM, PL) dengan partisipan guru kelas IV dan siswa terpilih secara
purposif. Data dikumpulkan melalui observasi pembelajaran, wawancara mendalam,
dan dokumentasi (soal cerita buatan guru, lembar jawab siswa), kemudian
dianalisis dengan reduksi, penyajian, dan penarikan kesimpulan induktif disertai
triangulasi sumber dan teknik. Hasil penelitian: (1) siswa mengalami kesulitan
pada semua tahapan Newman, siswa kerap keliru menangkap kata kunci seperti
“masingmasing”, “beberapa”, dan “dibagikan habis”; tidak menulis Diketahui,
Ditanyakan, Model; belum menghubungkan representasi ke persamaan; memilih
operasi tanpa alasan; serta menutup jawaban tanpa satuan dan tanpa pemeriksaan
kewajaran; (2) hambatan didaktis muncul dari bahasa soal yang bertele-tele,
ilustrasi yang dekoratif, asumsi yang tidak ditanyakan, konteks yang tidak
familier, serta minimnya scaffolding; (3) praktik pedagogis guru belum
sepenuhnya menautkan aspek linguistik, kontekstual, dan numerik sehingga
internalisasi bersifat parsial; (4) kemampuan pemecahan masalah siswa bervariasi
pada tingkat rendah, sedang, dan tinggi, dipengaruhi oleh kualitas internalisasi
literasi. Grand theory penelitian mengacu pada sosiokultural Vygotsky
(scaffolding dan mediasi bahasa). Teori Situasi Didaktis Brousseau untuk membaca
pengalaman belajar siswa. Pada tingkat operasional, penelitian memakai langkah
Polya, memetakan kesulitan dengan tahapan Newman. Kebaruan riset ini ada pada
model analisis terpadu yang memadukan empat teori: Vygotsky (peran guru/ZPD),
Polya (langkah kerja), Newman (titik gagal), dan Teori Situasi Didaktis
(dinamika kelas). Melalui gabungan ini, mutu bahasa soal dan cara guru memberi
dukungan dihubungkan dengan jejak internalisasi pada karya siswa kelas IV. Jejak
yang ditelaah meliputi kejelasan Diketahui, Ditanyakan, Model, ketepatan
representasi dan persamaan, alasan memilih operasi, konsistensi satuan, serta
uji kewajaran. This study analyzes how story problem literacy is internalized in
students' thinking and its implications for mathematical problem-solving
abilities in fourth-grade elementary schools in Cirebon City. This qualitative
research, with a phenomenological approach, was conducted in three Driving
Schools (SDN SM, KM, PL) with fourth-grade teachers and students selected
purposively as participants. Data were collected through learning observations,
in-depth interviews, and documentation (teacher-made story problems, student
answer sheets), then analyzed by reduction, presentation, and inductive
conclusion drawing accompanied by triangulation of sources and techniques. The
results of the study: (1) students experienced difficulties at all Newman
stages, students often misunderstood keywords such as "each", "some", and
"divided evenly"; did not write Known, Asked, Model; had not connected
representations to equations; chose operations without reason; and closed
answers without units and without checking reasonableness; (2) didactic
obstacles arose from wordy question language, decorative illustrations,
unquestioned assumptions, unfamiliar contexts, and minimal scaffolding; (3)
teachers' pedagogical practices have not fully linked linguistic, contextual,
and numerical aspects so that internalization is partial; (4) students' problem-
solving abilities vary at low, medium, and high levels, influenced by the
quality of literacy internalization. The research grand theory refers to
Vygotsky's sociocultural (scaffolding and language mediation). Brousseau's
Didactic Situation Theory to read students' learning experiences. At the
operational level, the research uses Polya's steps, mapping difficulties with
Newman's stages. The novelty of this research lies in the integrated analysis
model that combines four theories: Vygotsky (teacher role/ZPD), Polya (work
steps), Newman (failure point), and Didactic Situation Theory (class dynamics).
Through this combination, the quality of the question language and the teacher's
way of providing support are linked to traces of internalization in the work of
fourth-grade students. The traces examined include the clarity of Known, Asked,
Model, accuracy of representation and equations, reasons for choosing
operations, consistency of units, and reasonableness tests.
