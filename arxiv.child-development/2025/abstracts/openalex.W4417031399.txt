In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, children encounter unprecedented
opportunities for learning, creativity, and connection, but also face challenges
and risks that demand careful and informed navigation.As children increasingly
engage with online environments, it is imperative to empower healthcare
professionals, teachers and parents with the knowledge and skills needed to
confidently support children in managing risks safely and effectively.Amid this
complexity, the enduring power of movement and physical activity remains a vital
cornerstone of healthy child development.This talk highlights potential negative
consequences of digital engagement and underscores the importance of nurturing
movement as a universal language through which children explore, learn, and
grow.In an age dominated by digital noise, physical activity offers children
essential opportunities to develop cognitive, emotional, and social skills in an
engaging, natural, and holistic manner.Prioritising physical activity and skill-
building through movement goes far beyond enhancing physical strength, it
develops intelligence, courage, kindness, and self-confidence.These qualities
enable children to process experiences, build resilience, and cultivate a
grounded sense of self, all of which are critical not only for success offline
but also for navigating the digital world with awareness and safety.This
presentation will highlight the need to integrate movement and physical activity
into contemporary strategies aimed at supporting children's holistic development
in the digital age.By embracing both digital literacy and the irreplaceable
benefits of active play, we can equip children to thrive as well-rounded,
empowered individuals prepared to meet the challenges and possibilities of our
interconnected world.
