Title: Exploring Current Arts Practice in Kindergartens and Preparatory Classrooms
Authors: Susanne Garvis
ID: openalex.W2124142135
URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/183693911203700412

THE ARTS ARE AN IMPORTANT AREA of development for young children in their early
years. By engaging with arts activities, young children are able to use their
senses to explore the world. This paper reports on current arts practice in two
kindergartens and two preparatory classrooms in Queensland, Australia. All sites
are located in neighbouring suburbs. Findings reveal children in kindergarten
are exposed to an arts-rich environment. In preparatory classrooms, few examples
of arts activities could be observed, with children mostly accessing only one
weekly 30-minute music lesson with a specialist teacher. Arts practice in
classrooms was related to each teacher's philosophy of arts education, and the
amount of arts education exposure they received during teacher education.
Findings bring to the surface the tensions between kindergarten and preparatory
education, and highlight the importance of adequate arts education in early
childhood teacher education programs.
