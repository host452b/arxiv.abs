Title: DEPRESSION AMONG CHINESE CHILDREN AND ADOLESCENTS: A REVIEW OF THE LITERATURE
Authors: Maggie Zgambo, Fatch Welcome Kalembo, Guoping He, Honghong Wang
ID: openalex.W1594774770
URL: https://doi.org/10.18357/ijcyfs34.1201211543

&lt;p&gt;&lt;span style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: small;"&gt;The
purpose of this review is to explore, identify, and discuss the predisposing
factors and associated outcomes of depression in Chinese children and
adolescents. &lt;span style="font-family: Times New Roman; color:
#262626;"&gt;For inclusion in the review, studies had to meet our objectives, be
original peer-reviewed articles, conducted among Chinese children and
adolescents in China. Articles were sourced through MEDLINE, EMBASE,
&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span style="font-family: Times New Roman; color:
#262626; font-size: small;"&gt;Wan Fang Data&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span style="font-
family: Times New Roman; color: #262626; font-size:
small;"&gt;,&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span style="font-family: Times New Roman; color:
#262626; font-size: small;"&gt; &lt;/span&gt;&lt;span style="font-family: Times
New Roman; font-size: small;"&gt;PsycINFO,&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span style="font-
family: Times New Roman; color: #262626; font-size: small;"&gt; and DOAJ
databases. The results of the review indicate that the prevalence of depression
symptoms in Chinese children and adolescents is high. The following factors are
related to depression in Chinese children and adolescents: family, social
factors, peer relations, gender, age, obesity, body image, and ethnicity. The
outcomes of depression are poor academic performance, psychosocial retardation,
conduct problems, cognitive distortion, and suicide. Depression is a major
mental health problem among Chinese children and adolescents. This points to the
need for longitudinally designed and controlled studies to establish effective
preventive strategies.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
