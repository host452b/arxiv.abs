Title: Narrative Inquiry Into Two Worlds of Curriculum Making
Authors: Maureen Murphy, Janice Huber, D. Jean Clandinin
ID: openalex.W2772756424
URL: https://doi.org/10.36510/learnland.v5i2.562

This paper draws on a long-term multi-site narrative inquiry into the
curriculummaking experiences of children, families, and teachers. We draw upon
our earlier understandings of two worlds of curriculum making, the familial and
the school, to inquire into tensions shaped for one family, in a place of
school, as they experienced the meeting of their familial curriculum-making
world with the school curriculummaking world. Familial curriculum making is
curriculum making in which children are engaged as they interact with family and
community members. We wonder how we might move forward as we create situations
with children in both curriculum-making worlds, situations in which they can
find ways of making sense of the two constructions of themselves in these two
worlds.
