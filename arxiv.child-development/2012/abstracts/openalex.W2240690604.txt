Many of us who work in basic writing (BW), and especially those of us who work
in BW programs within the context of predominantly white institutions (or PWIs
for short), consider our programs to be "race-conscious" spaces-that is, spaces

literacy can be openly interrogated, challenged, and reformed when students
learn how to write.In this sense, many of us are inspired by assertions like
