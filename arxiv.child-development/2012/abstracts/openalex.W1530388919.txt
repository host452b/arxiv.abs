Title: Art education as multiprofessional collaboration.
Authors: Saila Nevanen, Antti Juvonen, Heikki Ruismäki
ID: openalex.W1530388919
URL: http://hdl.handle.net/10138/233052

In this article we explore the realisation of an art education project as
multiprofessional cooperation.The multiprofessional collaboration pair in this
study consisted of an artist working together with a teacher.This resulted in
activities, which all actors, artists, teachers and administrators saw to be at
an especially high level, both artistically and to the practice of
teaching.Actually they all thought that the targets, which were set to the
project, were clearly surpassed.At its best this working method connected
artistic work with the pedagogic knowledge and experience of the children's
group work.The work required common planning, flexibility from the traditional
methods and culture together with a long-lasting timeframe, (1-2.5 year per each
of the sub projects), which made it possible to try to develop new methods.In
