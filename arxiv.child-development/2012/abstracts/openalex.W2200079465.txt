Title: A conceptualisation of the post-museum as pedagogical space
Authors: Richard Watermeyer
ID: openalex.W2200079465
URL: https://doi.org/10.22323/2.11010202

This paper explores the idea of the post-museum as an immersive knowledge
experience facilitating conceptual and strategic directions in public engagement
with science and technology. It considers the extent to which the museum has
evolved from repository of cultural artefacts to experience-based process of
knowledge acquisition and production. The post-museum is invoked as a model of
participatory pedagogy that moves beyond traditional forms of learning,
knowledge acquisition and knowledge interface, and conceptualisations of the
learner in science. It is presented as an educational and recreational
experience, which locates and translates knowledge to the novice or non-
traditional patron using rich social narratives that ground scientific expertise
in the practice of everyday life. The experience of science is thus made
familiar and relevant and concurrently regulated and owned by the visitor. The
learner is consequently recast from passive recipient of information-bites to
choreographer, translator and innovator within a scientific knowledge continuum.
