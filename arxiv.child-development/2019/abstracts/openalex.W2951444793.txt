Title: Short Sleep Duration and Insomnia Symptoms were Associated with Lower Happiness Levels in Chinese Adults in Hong Kong
Authors: Sheng Zhi Zhao, Man Ping Wang, Kasisomayajula Viswanath, Agnes Yuen Kwan Lai, Dyt Fong
ID: openalex.W2951444793
URL: https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph16122079

<i>Study objective</i>: To examine the association of sleep duration and
insomnia symptoms with happiness. <i>Methods</i>: A random sample of 1691
Chinese adult (mean age 54 ± 20.1, male 51%) were interviewed in a population-
based telephone survey. Happiness was measured by the subjective happiness scale
(SHS) and the one-item global happiness index (GHI). Information on sleep
included mean past seven-day sleep duration (<6 h, ≥6 to <8 h and ≥8 h) and
insomnia symptoms: Difficulty in initiating sleep (DIS), difficulty in
maintaining sleep (DMS), and early morning awakening (EMA). Adjusted beta-
coefficient (β) of SHS and adjusted odds ratio (aOR) of GHI in relation to sleep
problems were calculated. Interaction effects by age (18-65 vs. ≥65) and by sex
were assessed. <i>Results</i>: Compared to ≥8 h of sleep, having <6 h of sleep
had lower SHS (adjusted β -0.32, 95% CI -0.46 to -0.17) and GHI (aOR 0.54, 95%
CI 0.38 to 0.78). The associations were stronger in younger adults and in women
(<i>p</i> < 0.05). DIS, DMS, and EMA were associated with lower SHS (adjusted β
ranged from -0.20 to -0.06) and GHI (aOR ranged from 0.57 to 0.89). Dose-
response association between the number of insomnia symptoms and lower SHS was
observed (<i>p</i> < 0.001). These associations were generally stronger in older
adults and among women. <i>Conclusions</i>: Lower levels of happiness were
observed, particularly in younger adults and females with short sleep duration
and older adults and females with insomnia symptoms. Prospective studies are
needed to confirm the findings and understand the mechanisms between sleep and
happiness.
