Title: Spiralling Back To The Spark: A Spiritual Journey Towards Authentic Creativity
Authors: Amy Rebecca Selina Pitt
ID: openalex.W3012296780
URL: http://hdl.handle.net/1807/98293

Grounded in the philosophical assumptions associated with holistic education and
transformative teacher development, this narrative inquiry is a collaborative
self-study that explores how spiritual practices contribute to the creative
process through the primary research question; How do I connect with my soul
through spiritual practices? Core themes emerging from this study include
Intention, Identity, Intuition, Integration, Paradox, Voice, Embodied Teaching,
Social Justice, and Death/Pain as Teacher. Its findings include the inextricably
entangled nature of soul, authenticity, and creativity and demonstrates the
imperative role spiritual practices play in spiralling back along the path
through and beyond the layers we’ve built up, made up of false identities, fear,
and perfectionism towards our own true essence—our souls in creating a more
loving and unified way forward for all living things both inside and outside of
the classroom.
