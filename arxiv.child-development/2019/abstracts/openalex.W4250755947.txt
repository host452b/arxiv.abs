Title: Transformation of Tolerance Values (in Religion) in Early Childhood Education
Authors: Tjipto Sumadi, Elindra Yetti, Yufiarti Yufiarti, Wuryani Wuryani
ID: openalex.W4250755947
URL: https://doi.org/10.21009/jpud.132.13

Religious tolerance is a supporter of social harmony and brings a country to a
better life. Instilling tolerance in early childhood is a challenge for early
childhood educators. This study aims to describe the transformation of religious
tolerance values ​​by teachers in early childhood education. This research is a
type of qualitative case study research model with researchers as observer
participants. This research produces the following findings, that (1)
transformation of tolerance values ​​among religious communities, is explicitly
not taught in Early Childhood Education (ECE) on the grounds that all students
are of the same religion, (2) transformation of tolerance of values among
religious students taught through learning integrated with other lessons, (3)
although explicitly the values ​​of tolerance among religious students are not
taught, but the values ​​of togetherness such as greeting, sharing something
that is owned, and helping the needs of other students are taught by practicing
at the same time. Keywords: Early Childhood Education, Tolerance Values in
