Title: Large-scale data from wearables reveal regional disparities in sleep patterns that persist across age and sex
Authors: Ju Lynn Ong, Jesisca Tandi, Amiya Patanaik, June C. Lo, Michael W.L. Chee
ID: openalex.W2918828451
URL: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-019-40156-x

Prior reports on geographical differences in sleep duration have relied on
samples collected at different time points with a variety of subjective
instruments. Using sleep data from a total of 553,559 nights from 23,680 Fitbit
users (aged 15-80y), we found objective evidence for regional disparities in
sleep duration of 32-43 min between Oceanian and East Asian users on weekdays.
This was primarily driven by later bedtimes in East Asians. Although users in
all countries extended sleep on weekends, East Asians continued to sleep less
than their Oceanian counterparts. Women generally slept more than men, and older
users slept less than younger users. Reasons for shorter sleep duration in East
Asians on both weekdays and weekends, across the lifespan and in both sexes
remain to be investigated.
