Title: The Interplay Between Family and Media as Socialisation Contexts: Parents’ Mediation Practices
Authors: Ingrid Paus-Hasebrink, Jasmin Kulterer, Philip Sinner
ID: openalex.W2909150585
URL: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-02653-0_7

Abstract This chapter sheds light on how, over an extended period, the parents
of the longitudinal study mediated the children’s use of the media. We discuss
this from two angles: on the one hand, with respect to both the children’s age
and their media usage and, on the other hand, with respect to changing
conditions in the families’ everyday lives. After that, we discuss what we term
the “practices of mediation” observed in the study, how they differ and how they
are related to options for action , outlines for action and competences for
action .
