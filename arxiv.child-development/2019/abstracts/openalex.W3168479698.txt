Title: Megachurches and Popular Culture: On Enclaving and Encroaching
Authors: Simon Coleman, Saliha Chattoo
ID: openalex.W3168479698
URL: https://doi.org/10.1163/9789004412927_006

In discussing examples of megachurches from around the world, this chapter
explores the close connections between such churches’ engagement in popular
culture and their strategies for expansion. The chapter argues that two ethical
approaches to ‘performing the mega’ can be discerned: ‘enclaving’, which
involves drawing, social, and ritual boundaries around behaviours of those who
are already members; and ‘encroaching’, which involves explicit and often
aggressive attempts to move into and appropriate secular realms of discourse and
culture. Overall, this chapter indicates the subtleties of engagement and
motivation among believers who are committed to inhabiting a space of agency
that lies on the border of redeemed and unredeemed arenas of action.
