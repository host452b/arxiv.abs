Title: The humanities in medical education: context, outcomes and structures: Figure 1
Authors: Jane Macnaughton
ID: openalex.W2152049931
URL: https://doi.org/10.1136/mh.26.1.23

There is now a context for teaching humanities in undergraduate medical
education via special study modules (SSMs). This paper discusses the
instrumental and non-instrumental role of the humanities in the education of
doctors. Three courses are then described and compared. The most successful of
the three is a SSM which had the following characteristics: it was voluntary, it
was an integral part of the curriculum, and it was examinable.
