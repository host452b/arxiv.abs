Title: To Wonder, Wander, and Linger in the World of Standardized Testing
Authors: Randall Wright, Alayne Sullivan
ID: openalex.W835753601
URL: https://scholarworks.lib.csusb.edu/wie/vol1/iss1/2

The standards movement began as a nobly-intended effort to establish a core
curriculum—a template of knowledge and skills that would guide teaching and
learning across the K-12 curriculum. Our attempts to standardize curriculum may
have unintended and deleterious side-effect: The atrophying of the mind’s
natural tendencies for exploratory play and inherently imaginative dimensions.
This paper engages us in a critical remembering of our pedagogical relationships
with children. It reminds us of children’s ways of being and asks how we might
engage them in a rigorous appreciation of curricular literacies without
thwarting their wonderful wanderings. Ultimately, we worry about the place of
standardized testing in this picture which circulates and perpetuates prevailing
risk management discourses and colonizes pedagogical relations.
