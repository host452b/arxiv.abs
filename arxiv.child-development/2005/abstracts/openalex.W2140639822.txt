Title: The Architecture of Racial Segregation: The Challenges of Preserving the Problematical Past
Authors: Robert R. Weyeneth
ID: openalex.W2140639822
URL: https://doi.org/10.1525/tph.2005.27.4.11

The article examines racial segregation as a spatial system and proposes a
conceptual framework for assessing its significance. It analyzes how the
ideology of white supremacy influenced design form in the United States and how
Jim Crow architecture appeared on the landscape. For African Americans, the
settings for everyday life were not simply the confines of this imposed
architecture; the article analyzes responses such as the construction of
alternative spaces. The discussion concludes by considering the architecture of
segregation from the perspective of historic preservation.
