Title: Becoming a musician : a longitudinal study investigating the career transitions of undergraduate music students.
Authors: Karen Burland
ID: openalex.W2550322162
URL: 

This thesis examines the nature of the transition between training as an
undergraduate&#13;\nmusician and choosing whether or not to pursue a career as a
professional performer.&#13;\nPrevious studies of musical development have
focused on children's skill acquisition, but&#13;\nfew have considered the roles
of motivation, practice, and the social environment in the&#13;\ntransition into
the music profession. Musicians making early career choices are
also&#13;\nprogressing through one of the most critical life-span changes - from
adolescence to&#13;\nyoung adulthood - and little is known about how the
psychological changes occurring&#13;\nduring this time influence a musician's
development.&#13;\nA two-year longitudinal study was conducted with a group of
32 musicians who, at the&#13;\nbeginning of the study, were undergraduate music
students attending either a British&#13;\nmusic college or university. Eight
interviews were conducted with each of the&#13;\nparticipants. These were
primarily qualitative in design, being either structured or
semi&#13;\nstructured and the data were analysed using qualitative and
quantitative analysis&#13;\ntechniques. The findings indicate that distinct
characteristics defined the musicians who&#13;\nchose to pursue a professional
performance career which differed from those for whom&#13;\nmusic became an
amateur interest. The results suggest that the four factors of
motivation,&#13;\nmusical identity, learning styles and coping strategies
interact and influence the career&#13;\nchoices of the musicians. It is
suggested that an individual's musical identity and his/her&#13;\ncoping
strategies play an integral role in the process of becoming a professional
or&#13;\namateur musician. A Dynamic Model of Musical Identity Formation and
Career Choice&#13;\nis proposed in order to depict and explain the complex
process of becoming a&#13;\nprofessional or amateur musician in adulthood.
