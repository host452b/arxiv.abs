Title: Canadian Children and Race: Toward an Antiracism Analysis
Authors: Kerry‐Ann Escayg, Rachel Berman, Natalie Royer
ID: openalex.W2762893934
URL: https://doi.org/10.18357/jcs.v42i2.17838

&lt;div class="page" title="Page 1"&gt;&lt;div class="section"&gt;&lt;div
class="layoutArea"&gt;&lt;div
class="column"&gt;&lt;p&gt;&lt;span&gt;Psychological research on Canadian
children and race has shown that young White and racialized children generally
have a pro-White bias. While scholars have utilized developmental
&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span&gt;or social psychological explanations for this finding,
none &lt;/span&gt;&lt;span&gt;have used an antiracism lens to interpret
children’s racial attitudes or to develop an antiracism pedagogy. To address
this research gap, this article uses antiracism theory as an
&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span&gt;analytical tool to explore the social-historical
processes that &lt;/span&gt;&lt;span&gt;have affected how children evaluate
racial differences and &lt;/span&gt;&lt;span&gt;White identity. It also briefly
proposes antiracism teaching practices specific to early childhood education
settings.
