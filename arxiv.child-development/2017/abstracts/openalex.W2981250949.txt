Title: Bastardly Duppies and Dastardly Dykes: Queer sexuality and the supernatural in Michelle Cliff’s Abeng (1984) and Shani Mootoo’s Cereus Blooms at Night (1996)
Authors: R.K. Gairola
ID: openalex.W2981250949
URL: https://researchrepository.murdoch.edu.au/id/eprint/51959/

This paper explores the ways in which the "duppy," or malevolent spirit,
circulates the fictive landscape of the queer novels of Michelle Cliff and Shani
Mootoo. I explore the ways in which the unhappy ghost is a figure which comments
on the sexual pathology of postcolonial queerness in the Caribbean. I focus on
the characters of Clare in "Abeng" and Mala in "Cereus Blooms at Night" in a bid
to elucidate the ways that Caribbean lesbianism invokes, on the one hand, what
M. Jacqui Alexander calls "erotic autonomy as a form of decolonization politics"
in the material eroticism of women characters. On the other hand, and at the
same time, however, these practices resurrect spectres of dissent that index
queer phobia in the Caribbean that is a direct result of exploitative economic
strangulation, past and present.
