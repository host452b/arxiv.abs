Title: When trust is threatened: Qualitative study of parents' perspectives on problematic clinical relationships in child cancer care
Authors: Sarah Davies, Peter Salmon, Bridget Young
ID: openalex.W2612563236
URL: https://doi.org/10.1002/pon.4454

Given the complexity of childhood cancer care, problems with clinical care are
inevitable. By engaging in containment work, parents met their needs to feel
secure in the face of these problems, but the extent to which parents should
have to do this work is debatable. Parents could benefit from support to seek
help when problems arise which threaten their trust in clinicians. Attachment
theory can guide clinicians in giving this support.
