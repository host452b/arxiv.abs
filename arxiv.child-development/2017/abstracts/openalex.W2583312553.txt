Title: Student musicians’ experiences of reflexivity during internships: Personal narratives and complex modalities
Authors: Dawn Bennett, Anna Reid, Jennifer Rowley
ID: openalex.W2583312553
URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/0255761416689843

A presumption behind work-integrated learning activities such as internship
programmes is that student thinking will shift as a result of exposure to
industry practice. We wondered if all students experience this change in the
positive sense that teachers expect. To examine this presumption we asked to
what extent and in what ways students reorient their thinking about self and
identity as a result of an internship experience. Analysis of student
reflections following a structured internship programme leads us to believe that
not all students experience a shift in thinking, and that their personal
narratives speak instead to a complex relation of modalities.
