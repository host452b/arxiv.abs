Title: Much more than talking: An emergent bilingual learner’s interactions in a New Zealand early childhood centre
Authors: Madoka Takemoto, Carolyn Tait, Margaret Gleeson
ID: openalex.W2784093546
URL: https://doi.org/10.26686/nzaroe.v22i0.4147

This qualitative study uses a sociocultural perspective to examine the
interactions of a child from a Chinese speaking home with his peers in a New
Zealand early childhood centre. He was 3 years 3 months at the time of data
collection. Data were gathered in three five-minute video recordings taken over
a period of three weeks during free play in a group of children. Deductive
coding revealed themes of active interaction, emotion, influence, and verbal
interaction. The analysis, using Hawkins’ (2004) perspectives of learning
environments, indicated that the use of culturally inclusive resources during
free play allowed the children to create a community of learners and scaffold
social and linguistic learning. The teacher chose to observe these interactions.
One implication is that free play in an early childhood education environment
without direct teacher guidance can be an important part of facilitating the
learning of emergent bilingual children.
