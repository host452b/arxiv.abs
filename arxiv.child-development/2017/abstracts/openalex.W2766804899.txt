Title: Experiments in Life-Writing: Introduction
Authors: Julia Novak
ID: openalex.W2766804899
URL: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-55414-3_1

Abstract The introduction outlines how writers of the twentieth and twenty-first
centuries have extended the range of the biographical through formal innovations
commonly associated with the fictional mode. It discusses the centrality of the
fact/fiction dichotomy for an understanding of experimentation with
auto/biographical form and its ethical implications. Distinguishing between
experiments that negate and those that expand the premises of auto/biography, it
surveys a range of generic terms from the field of life-writing research and
examines the textual levels on which experiments have been conducted in modern
auto/biographical narratives, also addressing the historicity of
auto/biographical experimentation. The introduction concludes by outlining the
individual chapters in the collection.
