Title: ‘Very Little Heroes’ History and Roots of the Eco-Village Movement
Authors: Judit Farkas
ID: openalex.W2743448505
URL: https://doi.org/10.1556/022.2017.62.1.4

Although the ecovillage-movement is a relatively new phenomenon (it was first
formalised in the 1990s), the initiatives, which aim to integrate, do have a
much longer history. The author of this article has been studying ecovillages
since 2008. As a cultural anthropologist, her focus lies in the socio-cultural
dimensions of the ecovillage-movement. In the present paper the history and
roots of the movement are covered by positioning eco-villages within both the
history of the broader environmental movement and that of intentional
communities; the international and Hungarian history of the ecovillage-movement
is presented; and, last but not least, an interpretation is given of the
Hungarian ecovillages in the context of rural migration processes. The picture
drawn up here is not intended to be an exhaustive one, since the ecovillage-
phenomenon can be presented from numerous different perspectives. Yet, the paper
clearly demonstrates the multiple embeddedness of the movement, and provides an
insight into the aspirations of ecovillage inhabitants and the current
functioning of ecovillages.
