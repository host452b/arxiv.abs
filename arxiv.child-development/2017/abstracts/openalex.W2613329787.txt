Title: Enriching Aboriginal Engagement in Schools through Service-learning: The Biidaaban Experience
Authors: Blaine E. Hatt, Nancy Maynes
ID: openalex.W2613329787
URL: https://doi.org/10.5296/jse.v7i2.11194

This paper involves an inquiry into the effects and affects of service learning
in a Biidaaban Youth Group (BYG) programme under the auspices of the Biidaaban
Community Service Learning centre (BCSL) at a small northern Ontario university.
Phenomenological, hermeneutical, and narrative inquiry approaches were applied
to interviews with stakeholders in BYG including a First Nations’ parent, a
school-aged child, a First Nations’ grandparent and Elder, an education
community partner, and a university-student tutor. The concepts of pathic
teaching and liberatory service learning help to frame the findings of this
study. Analysis of the data evidenced authentic caring for self and other and
genuine reciprocity that is transformative and enabled participants to attain a
liberatory level of social change and social consciousness as key components of
the high quality of service learning that is perceived by those who serve and
those who receive service from this unit.
