Title: The Teaching of Recent and Violent Conflicts as Challenges for History Education
Authors: Mario Carretero
ID: openalex.W2753146239
URL: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-54681-0_14

This chapter deals with an interdisciplinary attempt to consider how the
teaching of violent and recent past implies a challenge for history education in
and out of the school. For this purpose three meanings of “historyhistory ”
(school historyschool history , popularhistory popular one and
historiographyhistoriography ) are considered as they imply different
representations of both the past and how to teach it. Firstly, a view on the
contributions and advances of history educationhistory education in the last
decades is presented. The specificity of teaching violent and recent events,
related to political conflictspolitical conflicts which are still running in
many cases is taken into account. I propose that it is essential to study both
what to teach and how to teach in history. I will argue that a meaningful
relation between these two important aspects should be developed in order to
improve history education in conflicting contexts. From this point of view
history education is seen from the point of view of both historical thinking and
historical consciousness. Also it is considered how the national character of
recent violent pasts is a very influential dimension for its teaching. In
relation to it these national master narratives are analyzed as possible
obstacles to fully understand troubled and recent past. Thus six dimensions of
these types of narratives are analyzed as they offer a framework to understand
this obstacle. These dimensions are also considered as providers of possible
inputs for improving recent history teaching and learning processes. Also the
contributions of social psychology to this improvement is analyzed in relation
to the chapters of this book.
