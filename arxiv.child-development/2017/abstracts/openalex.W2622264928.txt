Title: “Learning from real life and not books”: A gamified approach to Business English task design in transatlantic telecollaboration
Authors: Ana Sevilla‐Pavón, Julia Haba Osca
ID: openalex.W2622264928
URL: https://dialnet.unirioja.es/servlet/oaiart?codigo=5926086

This paper deals with task design in the context of a telecollaboration
project\nwhich was carried out in a Business English course among students from
Spain\nand the United States. The goal was to provide students with
opportunities to\ndevelop linguistic, intercultural and digital competences by
interacting and\ncollaborating online with native speakers of the target
language. A task-based\napproach was adopted and enriched by gamification, the
different tasks being\ndesigned with a view towards engaging students
intrinsically in the learning\nprocess. This was achieved by means of the
adoption of gamification strategies\nand techniques such as the use of points,
performance graphs, quests, avatars, a\nreward system, peer assessment and the
use of social media. Via technological\nimmersion, students from both sides of
the Atlantic Ocean were required to\nwork together online to complete different
tasks while exchanging peer feedback\nand assessment. The paper analyses and
discusses participants’ views and\nperceptions about the gamified
telecollaboration exchange. The quantitative and\nqualitative data were gathered
by means of pre- and post-treatment\nquestionnaires. Results indicate that
students found this way of learning\nbeneficial in terms of the development of
different skills and competences\n(namely linguistic, digital and intercultural)
and motivation
