Title: International organizations and the problem of child health, 1945-1960.
Authors: James Gillespie
ID: openalex.W1583836756
URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14626274

Faced with planning mass relief campaigns in liberated Europe in the aftermath
of the Second World War, the United Nations Relief and Rehabilitation
Administration relied heavily on cheap supplies of skimmed milk from surplus
production in northern Europe. The success of this approach swept aside more
detailed planning around restoring national maternal and children's health
services. This reliance on mass feeding continued into the post-war years.
UNICEF, the agency with the most substantial financial resources, continued this
supply-driven approach despite growing reservations by nutritionists in its
rival agencies -the UN Food and Agricultural Organization and the World Health
Organization.
