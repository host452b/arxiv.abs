Title: Attachment Styles in Older European American and African American Adults
Authors: Carol Magai, Carl I. Cohen, Norweeta G. Milburn, Bert D. Thorpe, Renee A. McPherson
ID: openalex.W2162655929
URL: https://doi.org/10.1093/geronb/56.1.s28

Age and ethnicity differences appear to reflect cohort effects related to the
impact of economic hardship on families earlier this century and racial
prejudice. The high rates of dismissing attachment and low rates of secure
attachment in this large urban population suggest that these individuals may be
at risk for social isolation and poor health as they become older and more
frail.
