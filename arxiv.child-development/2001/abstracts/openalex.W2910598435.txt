Title: Adolescents' Scholastic Field, Identity Frames, and Future Projects
Authors: Jean Guichard
ID: openalex.W2910598435
URL: https://doi.org/10.4324/9780203823033-11

The sociological model of frames and vicarious identity forms enables the
integration of different approaches relative to &quot;construction of self and
&quot;identity strategies,&quot; analysis of the complex processes involved in
the creation of future goals in adolescence and questioning of pertinent
guidance activities involving today's youths. The biographic transaction could
hence be said diachronic and the relational transaction to be synchronic.
Identity construction would hence be the fruit of a double transaction: a
negotiation relative to institutional identity offer and an intimate
deliberation. The identity frames system is a structure of structures underlying
the representation of self and of others and the construction of self. The
fields and habitus theory of Pierre Bourdieu leads to the following hypothesis:
attendance of a scholastic system by adolescents leads to the formation in their
minds of a system of representations of themselves, of others, of training, and
of occupations, organized according to the basic dimensions structuring the
system.
