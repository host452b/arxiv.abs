Title: The Finnish Five-String Kantele: Sustainably Designed for Musical Joy
Authors: Inkeri Ruókonen, Anu Sepp, Venla Moilanen, Ossi Autio, Heikki Ruismäki
ID: openalex.W1994764533
URL: https://doi.org/10.2478/jtes-2014-0004

Abstract This article discusses the five-string kantele as an example of the
Finnish national heritage, a school instrument and an example of sustainable
design. A qualitative case study was made by collecting the data from the
Finnish students – prospective teachers – and the sixth form pupils, who had
designed and carved their own five-string kanteles. The purpose of this research
was to find out which aspects of five-string kantele design are considered the
most important for sustainable principles and design among these youngsters. As
results, the elements and principles of designing the five-string kantele are
discussed and its relevance to five sustainable characteristics (creative,
ecological, economic, aesthetic and socio-environmental) is presented. The
sustainable values of the kantele and the purpose for which it is made are also
considered.
