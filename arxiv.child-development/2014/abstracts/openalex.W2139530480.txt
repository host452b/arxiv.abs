Title: Black Pete, “Smug Ignorance,” and the Value of the Black Body in Postcolonial Netherlands
Authors: Yvon van der Pijl, Karina Goulordava
ID: openalex.W2139530480
URL: https://doi.org/10.1163/22134360-08803062

This article discusses the controversies over the blackface figure Black Pete
(Zwarte Piet)—central to the popular Dutch Saint Nicholas holiday tradition—and
the public uproar surrounding the Saint Nicholas feast in 2013. It combines
history, social theory, and patchwork ethnography, and draws on theoretical
approaches discussing race, capitalism, and the commodification of cultural
difference to establish an understanding of the controversial character. In
doing so, it argues that Black Pete is an invented tradition that marks a “white
Dutch habitus” in which the historical context of colonialism and the legacy of
slavery is repeatedly ignored or denied.
