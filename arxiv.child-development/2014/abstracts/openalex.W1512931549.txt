Title: Cool, Creepy, Moé: Otaku Fictions, Discourses, and Policies
Authors: Thomas Lamarre
ID: openalex.W1512931549
URL: https://doi.org/10.7202/1024714ar

This essay looks at the otaku phenomenon in terms of the production of
consumption (what produces consumption, and what consumption produces) primarily
three registers: fictions of otaku that stage the repression of otaku desires
and identities in order to form erogenous zones; cultural discourses that
pathologize otaku consumption; and government policies related to Cool Japan
that strive to reconfigure otaku consumption in terms of markets and self-
satisfying subjects. Considering these different registers brings into a focus a
neoliberal socius or social being, which allows constant intervention into
everyday life in the name of the fragility of the global free market by
typologizing and pathologizing consumers.
