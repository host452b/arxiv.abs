Title: Drawing It Out
Authors: Haidy Geismar
ID: openalex.W1892637941
URL: https://doi.org/10.1111/var.12041

The fieldwork sketches of A rthur B ernard D eacon, made in V anuatu in 1926–27,
give us insight into the early methodologies of social anthropology and into the
role of images in anthropological ways of thinking. Here I develop a perspective
on field sketches that explores them not only as visual mediations of the
fieldworker's subjectivity, but also as genre pieces that indicate very
particular forms of training in “how to see.” I draw out the visual conventions,
ways of thinking and seeing, that underscore the different strategies that D
eacon used in his drawing.
