Title: Islam and Modern School Education in Journal Pengasuh: Review of the Kaum Muda - Kaum Tua Dichotomy
Authors: Hiroko Kushimoto
ID: openalex.W2086342472
URL: https://doi.org/10.15408/sdi.v19i2.361

This study analyzes the articles on education that appeared in a journal titled
Pengasuh to discover the ideas on education in early 20th century Malaya. It
explores the thinking on education that motivated Islamic leaders to establish
modern religious schools called madrasah. The example of the Pengasuh shows that
new ideas of Islamic learning that supported the spread of new madrasah had been
shared regardless kaum muda – kaum tua dichotomy, contrary to the assumption of
previous studies. The main difference between the kaum muda and their opponent
kaum tua is their attitude toward knowledge accumulated in the schools of
Islamic law. The discussions in the Pengasuh shows strong support for the
development of modern Islamic education, without denigrating the old style of
Islamic learning.DOI: 10.15408/sdi.v19i2.361
