Title: World literature: What gets lost in translation?
Authors: Nicholas Harrison
ID: openalex.W2313651088
URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/0021989414535420

In What is World Literature? (2003) and other influential works David Damrosch
suggests repeatedly that world literature “gains in translation”. This article
begins by showing that Damrosch gives no convincing account of what this phrase
means. It then develops a wider argument that, even if translations may be
accomplished literary works in their own right, the very notion of literature —
or at least, one important notion of literature — is associated with
untranslatability, or what is lost in translation. The losses, it is argued, may
be felt or imagined in various dimensions, and reach into the institutional
foundations of the study of literature and of foreign languages.
