Title: The U.S. car colossus and the production of inequality
Authors: Catherine Lutz
ID: openalex.W1907122820
URL: https://doi.org/10.1111/amet.12072

The contemporary world is one of restless mobilities, radically morphing
physical landscapes, baroque technologies, new forms of governance and
subjectivity, and onerous inequalities. The automobile provides vivid insight
into all five phenomena as well as into their relationship. I ask how the car-
dependent mobility system of the United States not only reflects but also
intensively generates the inequalities that characterize U.S. society. I propose
that “compulsory consumption” and the automobile's centrality to the current
regime of accumulation can help account for this. Theories of inequality and
mobility, I suggest, can be adapted to account for the automobile industry's
capture of contemporary life. [mobility, transportation, inequality, automobile,
regime of accumulation, political economy, United States]
