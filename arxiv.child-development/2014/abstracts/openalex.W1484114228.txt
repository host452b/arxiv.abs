Title: 7 Constructing the Landscape of Consciousness in News Stories
Authors: José Sanders, Hans Hoeken
ID: openalex.W1484114228

In every known culture, people tell each other narratives.2 Toolan (2001)
defines a narrative as "a perceived sequence of non-randomly connected events,
typically involving sentient beings as the experiencing agonist from whose
experience we can learn."3 The use of "learn" suggests that narratives may serve
an adaptive function. It has been argued that this function lies in narratives'
contribution to the smooth collaboration between individuals within a group.4
Since collaborating individuals were more successful in gathering food and
fighting enemies than individuals fending for themselves, their chances of
survival increased.
