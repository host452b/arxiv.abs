This study aimed to develop “Evaluation Instrument for the Early
Mathematical&#13;\nReasoning Skills” in order to determine the mathematical
reasoning skills of children&#13;\nin the area of measurement and data analysis-
probability as well as to perform its&#13;\nvalidity and reliability.
Furthermore, variables such as the effect of child gender,
age,&#13;\ninstitution attended and part/full time attendance to the
institution, total duration of&#13;\npreschool attendance as well as age and
educational background of their parents were&#13;\nanalyzed. The study group is
composed of randomly selected 204 children of normal&#13;\ndevelopment in Ankara
at the age of 60-74 months attending dependent and&#13;\nindependent
kindergarten, private preschools, daycare centers and kindergartens
of&#13;\nMinistry of National Education. Data were collected using “Evaluation
Instrument&#13;\nfor the Early Mathematical Reasoning Skills” that is type of
holistic rubric and&#13;\n“Child Information Form” that was developed by the
researcher. The instrument&#13;\ndeveloped was found to be reliable and valid.
No significant difference was found&#13;\nbetween the scores of reasoning areas
and types of boys and girls. In addition, there&#13;\nwas no significant
difference between the scores of age of parents and total duration&#13;\nof
preschool attendance for the reasoning areas and types. Older children
gained&#13;\nhigher scores from the instrument than attendees of preschool and
those attended&#13;\ninstitutions part-time for the measurement and induction
areas. It is observed that&#13;\nchildren of university graduate fathers got
higher scores as for the measurement and&#13;\ninduction areas; while, those of
university graduate mothers had higher scores from&#13;\nthe data analysis-
probability and induction areas. Moreover, no difference was found&#13;\namong
the variables for the deduction reasoning scores.
