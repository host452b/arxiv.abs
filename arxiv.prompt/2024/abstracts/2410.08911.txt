Title: Test-driven Software Experimentation with LASSO: an LLM Prompt
Benchmarking Example
Authors: Marcus Kessel
ID: 2410.08911
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.08911

Abstract:
Empirical software engineering faces a critical gap: the lack of standardized
tools for rapid development and execution of Test-Driven Software Experiments
(TDSEs) -- that is, experiments that involve the execution of software subjects
and the observation and analysis of their "de facto" run-time behavior. In this
paper we present a general-purpose analysis platform called LASSO that provides
a minimal set of domain-specific languages and data structures to conduct TDSEs.
By empowering users with an executable scripting language to design and execute
TDSEs, LASSO enables efficient evaluation of run-time semantics and execution
characteristics in addition to statically determined properties. We present an
example TDSE that demonstrates the practical benefits of LASSO's scripting
capabilities for assessing the reliability of LLMs for code generation by means
of a self-contained, reusable and extensible study script. The LASSO platform
and live pipeline examples are publicly available at:
https://softwareobservatorium.github.io/.
