Title: Soft Begging: Modular and Efficient Shielding of LLMs against Prompt
Injection and Jailbreaking based on Prompt Tuning
Authors: Simon Ostermann, Kevin Baum, Christoph Endres, Julia Masloh, Patrick
Schramowski
ID: 2407.03391
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.03391

Abstract:
Prompt injection (both direct and indirect) and jailbreaking are now recognized
as significant issues for large language models (LLMs), particularly due to
their potential for harm in application-integrated contexts. This extended
abstract explores a novel approach to protecting LLMs from such attacks, termed
"soft begging." This method involves training soft prompts to counteract the
effects of corrupted prompts on the LLM's output. We provide an overview of
prompt injections and jailbreaking, introduce the theoretical basis of the "soft
begging" technique, and discuss an evaluation of its effectiveness.
