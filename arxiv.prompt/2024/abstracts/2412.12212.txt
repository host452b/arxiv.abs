Title: Finding a Wolf in Sheep's Clothing: Combating Adversarial Text-To-Image
Prompts with Text Summarization
Authors: Portia Cooper, Harshita Narnoli, Mihai Surdeanu
ID: 2412.12212
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.12212

Abstract:
Text-to-image models are vulnerable to the stepwise "Divide-and-Conquer Attack"
(DACA) that utilize a large language model to obfuscate inappropriate content in
prompts by wrapping sensitive text in a benign narrative. To mitigate stepwise
DACA attacks, we propose a two-layer method involving text summarization
followed by binary classification. We assembled the Adversarial Text-to-Image
Prompt (ATTIP) dataset ($N=940$), which contained DACA-obfuscated and non-
obfuscated prompts. From the ATTIP dataset, we created two summarized versions:
one generated by a small encoder model and the other by a large language model.
Then, we used an encoder classifier and a GPT-4o classifier to perform content
moderation on the summarized and unsummarized prompts. When compared with a
classifier that operated over the unsummarized data, our method improved F1
score performance by 31%. Further, the highest recorded F1 score achieved (98%)
was produced by the encoder classifier on a summarized ATTIP variant. This study
indicates that pre-classification text summarization can inoculate content
detection models against stepwise DACA obfuscations.
