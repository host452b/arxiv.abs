Title: Enhancing LLM Instruction Following: An Evaluation-Driven Multi-Agentic
Workflow for Prompt Instructions Optimization
Authors: Alberto Purpura, Li Wang, Sahil Badyal, Eugenio Beaufrand, Adam
Faulkner
ID: 2601.03359
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03359

Abstract:
Large Language Models (LLMs) often generate substantively relevant content but
fail to adhere to formal constraints, leading to outputs that are conceptually
correct but procedurally flawed. Traditional prompt refinement approaches focus
on rephrasing the description of the primary task an LLM has to perform,
neglecting the granular constraints that function as acceptance criteria for its
response. We propose a novel multi-agentic workflow that decouples optimization
of the primary task description from its constraints, using quantitative scores
as feedback to iteratively rewrite and improve them. Our evaluation demonstrates
this method produces revised prompts that yield significantly higher compliance
scores from models like Llama 3.1 8B and Mixtral-8x 7B.
