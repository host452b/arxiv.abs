Title: Conceptual Metaphor Theory as a Prompting Paradigm for Large Language
Models
Authors: Oliver Kramer
ID: 2502.01901
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.01901

Abstract:
We introduce Conceptual Metaphor Theory (CMT) as a framework for enhancing large
language models (LLMs) through cognitive prompting in complex reasoning tasks.
CMT leverages metaphorical mappings to structure abstract reasoning, improving
models' ability to process and explain intricate concepts. By incorporating CMT-
based prompts, we guide LLMs toward more structured and human-like reasoning
patterns. To evaluate this approach, we compare four native models (Llama3.2,
Phi3, Gemma2, and Mistral) against their CMT-augmented counterparts on benchmark
tasks spanning domain-specific reasoning, creative insight, and metaphor
interpretation. Responses were automatically evaluated using the Llama3.3 70B
model. Experimental results indicate that CMT prompting significantly enhances
reasoning accuracy, clarity, and metaphorical coherence, outperforming baseline
models across all evaluated tasks.
