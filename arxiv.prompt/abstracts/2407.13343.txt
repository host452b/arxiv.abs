Title: Learning-From-Mistakes Prompting for Indigenous Language Translation
Authors: You-Cheng Liao, Chen-Jui Yu, Chi-Yi Lin, He-Feng Yun, Yen-Hsiang Wang,
Hsiao-Min Li, Yao-Chung Fan
ID: 2407.13343
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.13343

Abstract:
Using large language models, this paper presents techniques to improve extremely
low-resourced indigenous language translations. Our approaches are grounded in
the use of (1) the presence of a datastore consisting of a limited number of
parallel translation examples, (2) the inherent capabilities of LLMs like
GPT-3.5, and (3) a word-level translation dictionary. We harness the potential
of LLMs and in-context learning techniques in such a setting for using LLMs as
universal translators for extremely low-resourced languages. Our methodology
hinges on utilizing LLMs as language compilers for selected language pairs,
hypothesizing that they could internalize syntactic structures to facilitate
accurate translation. We introduce three techniques: KNNPrompting with Retrieved
Prompting Context, Chain-of-Thought Prompting and Learningfrom-Mistakes
Prompting, with the last method addressing past errors. The evaluation results
suggest that, even with limited corpora, LLMs can effectively translate
extremely low-resource languages when paired with proper prompting.
