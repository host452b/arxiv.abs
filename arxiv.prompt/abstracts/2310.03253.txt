Title: Molecule Design by Latent Prompt Transformer
Authors: Deqian Kong, Yuhao Huang, Jianwen Xie, Ying Nian Wu
ID: 2310.03253
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.03253

Abstract:
This paper proposes a latent prompt Transformer model for solving challenging
optimization problems such as molecule design, where the goal is to find
molecules with optimal values of a target chemical or biological property that
can be computed by an existing software. Our proposed model consists of three
components. (1) A latent vector whose prior distribution is modeled by a Unet
transformation of a Gaussian white noise vector. (2) A molecule generation model
that generates the string-based representation of molecule conditional on the
latent vector in (1). We adopt the causal Transformer model that takes the
latent vector in (1) as prompt. (3) A property prediction model that predicts
the value of the target property of a molecule based on a non-linear regression
on the latent vector in (1). We call the proposed model the latent prompt
Transformer model. After initial training of the model on existing molecules and
their property values, we then gradually shift the model distribution towards
the region that supports desired values of the target property for the purpose
of molecule design. Our experiments show that our proposed model achieves state
of the art performances on several benchmark molecule design tasks.
