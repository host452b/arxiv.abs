Title: Enhancing Summarization Performance through Transformer-Based Prompt
Engineering in Automated Medical Reporting
Authors: Daphne van Zandvoort, Laura Wiersema, Tom Huibers, Sandra van Dulmen,
Sjaak Brinkkemper
ID: 2311.13274
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.13274

Abstract:
Customized medical prompts enable Large Language Models (LLM) to effectively
address medical dialogue summarization. The process of medical reporting is
often time-consuming for healthcare professionals. Implementing medical dialogue
summarization techniques presents a viable solution to alleviate this time
constraint by generating automated medical reports. The effectiveness of LLMs in
this process is significantly influenced by the formulation of the prompt, which
plays a crucial role in determining the quality and relevance of the generated
reports. In this research, we used a combination of two distinct prompting
strategies, known as shot prompting and pattern prompting to enhance the
performance of automated medical reporting. The evaluation of the automated
medical reports is carried out using the ROUGE score and a human evaluation with
the help of an expert panel. The two-shot prompting approach in combination with
scope and domain context outperforms other methods and achieves the highest
score when compared to the human reference set by a general practitioner.
However, the automated reports are approximately twice as long as the human
references, due to the addition of both redundant and relevant statements that
are added to the report.
