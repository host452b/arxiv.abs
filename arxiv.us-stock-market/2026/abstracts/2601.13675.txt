Title: On the Anchoring Effect of Monetary Policy on the Labor Share of Income and the Rationality of Its Setting Mechanism
Authors: Li Tuobang
ID: 2601.13675
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.13675

Modern macroeconomic monetary theory suggests that the labor share of income has
effectively become a core macroe-conomic parameter anchored by top policymakers
through Open Market Operations (OMO). However, the setting of this parameter
remains a subject of intense economic debate. This paper provides a detailed
summary of these controversies, analyzes the scope of influence exerted by
market agents other than the top policymakers on the labor share, and explores
the rationality of its setting mechanism.
