Title: Optimal market completion through financial derivatives with applications to volatility risk
Authors: Matt Davison, Marcos Escobar-Anel, Yichen Zhu
ID: 2202.08148
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.08148

This paper investigates the optimal choices of financial derivatives to complete
a financial market in the framework of stochastic volatility (SV) models. We
introduce an efficient and accurate simulation-based method, applicable to
generalized diffusion models, to approximate the optimal derivatives-based
portfolio strategy. We build upon the double optimization approach (i.e.
expected utility maximization and risk exposure minimization) proposed in
Escobar-Anel et al. (2022); demonstrating that strangle options are the best
choices for market completion within equity options. Furthermore, we explore the
benefit of using volatility index derivatives and conclude that they could be
more convenient substitutes when only long-term maturity equity options are
available.
