Title: Dominant Drivers of National Inflation
Authors: Jan Ditzen, Francesco Ravazzolo
ID: 2212.05841
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.05841

For western economies a long-forgotten phenomenon is on the horizon: rising
inflation rates. We propose a novel approach christened D2ML to identify drivers
of national inflation. D2ML combines machine learning for model selection with
time dependent data and graphical models to estimate the inverse of the
covariance matrix, which is then used to identify dominant drivers. Using a
dataset of 33 countries, we find that the US inflation rate and oil prices are
dominant drivers of national inflation rates. For a more general framework, we
carry out Monte Carlo simulations to show that our estimator correctly
identifies dominant drivers.
