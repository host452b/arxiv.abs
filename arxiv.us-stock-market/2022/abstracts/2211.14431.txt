Title: Efficient and Accurate Calibration to FX Market Skew with Fully Parameterized Local Volatility Model
Authors: Dongli Wu, Bufan Zhang, Xiao Lin
ID: 2211.14431
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.14431

When trading American and Asian options in the FX derivatives market, banks must
calculate prices using a complex mathematical model. It is often observed that
different models produce varying prices for the same exotic option, which
violates the non-arbitrage requirement of derivative risk management. To address
this issue, we have studied a fully parameterized local volatility model for
pricing American/Asian options. This model, when implemented using a grid or
Monte-Carlo numerical method, can be efficiently and accurately calibrated to FX
market skew volatilities. As a result, the model can provide reliable prices for
exotic options during daily trading activities.
