Title: What does machine learning say about the drivers of inflation?
Authors: Emanuel Kohlscheen
ID: 2208.14653
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2208.14653

This paper examines the drivers of CPI inflation through the lens of a simple,
but computationally intensive machine learning technique. More specifically, it
predicts inflation across 20 advanced countries between 2000 and 2021, relying
on 1,000 regression trees that are constructed based on six key macroeconomic
variables. This agnostic, purely data driven method delivers (relatively) good
outcome prediction performance. Out of sample root mean square errors (RMSE)
systematically beat even the in-sample benchmark econometric models. Partial
effects of inflation expectations on CPI outcomes are also elicited in the
paper. Overall, the results highlight the role of expectations for inflation
outcomes in advanced economies, even though their importance appears to have
declined somewhat during the last 10 years.
