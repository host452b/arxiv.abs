Title: "Stiff" Field Theory of Interest Rates and Psychological Future Time
Authors: Belal Baaquie, Jean-Philippe Bouchaud
ID: cond-mat/0403713
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/cond-mat/0403713

The simplest field theory description of the multivariate statistics of forward
rate variations over time and maturities, involves a quadratic action containing
a gradient squared rigidity term. However, this choice leads to a spurious kink
(infinite curvature) of the normalized correlation function for coinciding
maturities. Motivated by empirical results, we consider an extended action that
contains a squared Laplacian term, which describes the bending stiffness of the
FRC. With the extra ingredient of a `psychological' future time, describing how
the perceived time between events depends on the time in the future, our theory
accounts extremely well for the phenomenology of interest rate dynamics.
