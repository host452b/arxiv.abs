Title: Zero Black-Derman-Toy interest rate model
Authors: Grzegorz Krzyżanowski, Ernesto Mordecki, Andrés Sosa
ID: 1908.04401
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1908.04401

We propose a modification of the classical Black-Derman-Toy (BDT) interest rate
tree model, which includes the possibility of a jump with small probability at
each step to a practically zero interest rate. The corresponding BDT algorithms
are consequently modified to calibrate the tree containing the zero interest
rate scenarios. This modification is motivated by the recent 2008-2009 crisis in
the United States and it quantifies the risk of a future crises in bond prices
and derivatives. The proposed model is useful to price derivatives. This
exercise also provides a tool to calibrate the probability of this event. A
comparison of option prices and implied volatilities on US Treasury bonds
computed with both the proposed and the classical tree model is provided, in six
different scenarios along the different periods comprising the years 2002-2017.
