Title: Long-range dependence in Interest Rates and Monetary Policy
Authors: Daniel O. Cajueiro, Benjamin M. Tabak
ID: physics/0607245
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/physics/0607245

This paper studies the dynamics of Brazilian interest rates for short-term
maturities. The paper employs developed techniques in the econophysics
literature and tests for long-range dependence in the term structure of these
interest rates for the last decade. Empirical results suggest that the degree of
long-range dependence has changed over time due to changes in monetary policy,
specially in the short-end of the term structure of interest rates. Therefore,
we show that it is possible to identify monetary arrangements using these
techniques from econophysics.
