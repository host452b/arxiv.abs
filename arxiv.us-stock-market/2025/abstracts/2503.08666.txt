Title: Modeling Stock Return Distributions and Pricing Options
Authors: Xinxin Jiang
ID: 2503.08666
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.08666

This paper provides evidence that stock returns, after truncation, might be
modeled by a special type of continuous mixtures or normals, so-called
$q$-Gaussians. Negative binomial distributions might model the counts for
extreme returns. A generalized jump-diffusion model is proposed, and an explicit
option pricing formula is obtained.
