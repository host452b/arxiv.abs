Title: Introducing the PIT-plot -- a new tool in the portfolio manager's toolkit
Authors: Stig-Johan Wiklund, Magnus Ytterstad
ID: 2506.12068
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.12068

Project portfolio management is an essential process for organizations aiming to
optimize the value of their R&D investments. In this article, we introduce a new
tool designed to support the prioritization of projects within project portfolio
management. We label this tool the PIT-plot, an acronym for Project Impact
Tornado plot, with reference to the similarity to the Tornado plot often used
for sensitivity analyses. Many traditional practices in portfolio management
focus on the properties of the projects available to the portfolio. We are with
the PIT-plot changing the perspective and focus not on the properties of the
projects themselves, but on the impact that the projects may have on the
portfolio. This enables the strategic portfolio management to identify and focus
on the projects of largest impact to the portfolio, either for the purpose of
risk mitigation or for the purpose of value-adding efforts.
