Title: Forecasting Nigerian Equity Stock Returns Using Long Short-Term Memory Technique
Authors: Adebola K. Ojo, Ifechukwude Jude Okafor
ID: 2507.01964
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.01964

Investors and stock market analysts face major challenges in predicting stock
returns and making wise investment decisions. The predictability of equity stock
returns can boost investor confidence, but it remains a difficult task. To
address this issue, a study was conducted using a Long Short-term Memory (LSTM)
model to predict future stock market movements. The study used a historical
dataset from the Nigerian Stock Exchange (NSE), which was cleaned and normalized
to design the LSTM model. The model was evaluated using performance metrics and
compared with other deep learning models like Artificial and Convolutional
Neural Networks (CNN). The experimental results showed that the LSTM model can
predict future stock market prices and returns with over 90% accuracy when
trained with a reliable dataset. The study concludes that LSTM models can be
useful in predicting financial time-series-related problems if well-trained.
Future studies should explore combining LSTM models with other deep learning
techniques like CNN to create hybrid models that mitigate the risks associated
with relying on a single model for future equity stock predictions.
