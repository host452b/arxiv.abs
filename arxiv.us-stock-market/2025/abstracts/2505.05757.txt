Title: Who's at Risk? Effects of Inflation on Unemployment Risk
Authors: Hie Joo Ahn, Lam Nguyen
ID: 2505.05757
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.05757

We empirically investigate the distributional effects of inflation on workers'
unemployment tail risks using instrumental variable quantile regression. We find
that supply-driven inflation disproportionately raises unemployment tail risks
for cyclically vulnerable workers in both the short and medium term, while
demand-driven inflation has differential effects -- limited to race and reason
for unemployment -- only in the medium term. Demand-boosting policies, including
monetary policy, can inadvertently widen those disparities through the inflation
channel, underscoring the importance of inflation stabilization in promoting
equitable growth in the labor market. Our findings could be explained
structurally by heterogeneity in experienced inflation and wage inflation
expectations.
