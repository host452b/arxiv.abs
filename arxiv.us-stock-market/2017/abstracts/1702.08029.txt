Title: The short-term price impact of trades is universal
Authors: Bence Toth, Zoltan Eisler, Jean-Philippe Bouchaud
ID: 1702.08029
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1702.08029

We analyze a proprietary dataset of trades by a single asset manager, comparing
their price impact with that of the trades of the rest of the market. In the
context of a linear propagator model we find no significant difference between
the two, suggesting that both the magnitude and time dependence of impact are
universal in anonymous, electronic markets. This result is important as optimal
execution policies often rely on propagators calibrated on anonymous data. We
also find evidence that in the wake of a trade the order flow of other market
participants first adds further copy-cat trades enhancing price impact on very
short time scales. The induced order flow then quickly inverts, thereby
contributing to impact decay.
