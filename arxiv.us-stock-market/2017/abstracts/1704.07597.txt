Title: Learning Agents in Black-Scholes Financial Markets: Consensus Dynamics and Volatility Smiles
Authors: Tushar Vaidya, Carlos Murguia, Georgios Piliouras
ID: 1704.07597
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1704.07597

Black-Scholes (BS) is the standard mathematical model for option pricing in
financial markets. Option prices are calculated using an analytical formula
whose main inputs are strike (at which price to exercise) and volatility. The BS
framework assumes that volatility remains constant across all strikes, however,
in practice it varies. How do traders come to learn these parameters? We
introduce natural models of learning agents, in which they update their beliefs
about the true implied volatility based on the opinions of other traders. We
prove convergence of these opinion dynamics using techniques from control theory
and leader-follower models, thus providing a resolution between theory and
market practices. We allow for two different models, one with feedback and one
with an unknown leader.
