Title: A large non-Gaussian structural VAR with application to Monetary Policy
Authors: Jan Prüser
ID: 2412.17598
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.17598

We propose a large structural VAR which is identified by higher moments without
the need to impose economically motivated restrictions. The model scales well to
higher dimensions, allowing the inclusion of a larger number of variables. We
develop an efficient Gibbs sampler to estimate the model. We also present an
estimator of the deviance information criterion to facilitate model comparison.
Finally, we discuss how economically motivated restrictions can be added to the
model. Experiments with artificial data show that the model possesses good
estimation properties. Using real data we highlight the benefits of including
more variables in the structural analysis. Specifically, we identify a monetary
policy shock and provide empirical evidence that prices and economic output
respond with a large delay to the monetary policy shock.
