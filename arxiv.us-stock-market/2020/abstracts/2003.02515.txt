Title: Time-varying neural network for stock return prediction
Authors: Steven Y. K. Wong, Jennifer Chan, Lamiae Azizi, Richard Y. D. Xu
ID: 2003.02515
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.02515

We consider the problem of neural network training in a time-varying context.
Machine learning algorithms have excelled in problems that do not change over
time. However, problems encountered in financial markets are often time-varying.
We propose the online early stopping algorithm and show that a neural network
trained using this algorithm can track a function changing with unknown
dynamics. We compare the proposed algorithm to current approaches on predicting
monthly U.S. stock returns and show its superiority. We also show that prominent
factors (such as the size and momentum effects) and industry indicators, exhibit
time varying stock return predictiveness. We find that during market distress,
industry indicators experience an increase in importance at the expense of firm
level features. This indicates that industries play a role in explaining stock
returns during periods of heightened risk.
