Title: Interest Rates and Inflation
Authors: Michael Coopersmith, Pascal J. Gambardella
ID: 1603.08311
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1603.08311

This article is an extension of the work of one of us (Coopersmith, 2011) in
deriving the relationship between certain interest rates and the inflation rate
of a two component economic system. We use the well-known Fisher relation
between the difference of the nominal interest rate and its inflation adjusted
value to eliminate the inflation rate and obtain a delay differential equation.
We provide computer simulated solutions for this equation over regimes of
interest. This paper could be of interest to three audiences: those in Economics
who are interested in interest and inflation; those in Mathematics who are
interested in examining a detailed analysis of a delay differential equation,
which includes a summary of existing results, simulations, and an exact
solution; and those in Physics who are interested in non-traditional
applications of traditional methods of modeling.
