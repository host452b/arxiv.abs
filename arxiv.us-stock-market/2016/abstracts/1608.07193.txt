Title: Quantile Dependence between Stock Markets and its Application in Volatility Forecasting
Authors: Heejoon Han
ID: 1608.07193
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1608.07193

This paper examines quantile dependence between international stock markets and
evaluates its use for improving volatility forecasting. First, we analyze
quantile dependence and directional predictability between the US stock market
and stock markets in the UK, Germany, France and Japan. We use the cross-
quantilogram, which is a correlation statistic of quantile hit processes. The
detailed dependence between stock markets depends on specific quantile ranges
and this dependence is generally asymmetric; the negative spillover effect is
stronger than the positive spillover effect and there exists strong directional
predictability from the US market to the UK, Germany, France and Japan markets.
Second, we consider a simple quantile-augmented volatility model that
accommodates the quantile dependence and directional predictability between the
US market and these other markets. The quantile-augmented volatility model
provides superior in-sample and out-of-sample volatility forecasts.
