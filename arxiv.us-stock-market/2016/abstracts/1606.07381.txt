Title: Spread, volatility, and volume relationship in financial markets and market making profit optimization
Authors: Jack Sarkissian
ID: 1606.07381
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1606.07381

We study the relationship between price spread, volatility and trading volume.
We find that spread forms as a result of interplay between order liquidity and
order impact. When trading volume is small adding more liquidity helps improve
price accuracy and reduce spread, but after some point additional liquidity
begins to deteriorate price. The model allows to connect the bid-ask spread and
high-low bars to measurable microstructural parameters and express their
dependence on trading volume, volatility and time horizon. Using the established
relations, we address the operating spread optimization problem to maximize
market-making profit.
