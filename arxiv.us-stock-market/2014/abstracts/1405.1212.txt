Title: Market risk modelling in Solvency II regime and hedging options not using underlying
Authors: Przemysław Klusik
ID: 1405.1212
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1405.1212

In the paper we develop mathematical tools of quantile hedging in incomplete
market. Those could be used for two significant applications:   o calculating
the \textbf{optimal capital requirement imposed by Solvency II} (Directive
2009/138/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council) when the market and
non-market risk is present in insurance company. We show hot to find the minimal
capital $V_0$ to provide with the one-year hedging strategy for insurance
company satisfying $E\left[{\mathbf 1}_{\{V_1 \geq D\}}\right]=0.995$, where
$V_1$ denotes the value of insurance company in one year time and $D$ is the
payoff of the contract.   o finding a hedging strategy for derivative not using
underlying but an asset with dynamics correlated or in some other way dependent
(no deterministically) on underlying. The work is a generalisation of the work
of Klusik and Palmowski \cite{KluPal}.   Keywords: quantile hedging, solvency
II, capital modelling, hedging options on nontradable asset.
