Title: Long Term Optimal Investment in Matrix Valued Factor Models
Authors: Scott Robertson, Hao Xing
ID: 1408.7010
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1408.7010

Long term optimal investment problems are studied in a factor model with matrix
valued state variables. Explicit parameter restrictions are obtained under
which, for an isoelastic investor, the finite horizon value function and optimal
strategy converge to their long-run counterparts as the investment horizon
approaches infinity. This convergence also yields portfolio turnpikes for
general utilities. By using results on large time behaviour of semi-linear
partial differential equations, our analysis extends affine models, where the
Wishart process drives investment opportunities, to a non-affine setting.
Furthermore, in the affine setting, an example is constructed where the value
function is not exponentially affine, in contrast to models with vector-valued
state variables.
