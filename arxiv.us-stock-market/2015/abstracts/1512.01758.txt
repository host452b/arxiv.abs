Title: Financial market models in discrete time beyond the concave case
Authors: Mario Sikic
ID: 1512.01758
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1512.01758

In this article we propose a study of market models starting from a set of
axioms, as one does in the case of risk measures. We define a market model
simply as a mapping from the set of adapted strategies to the set of random
variables describing the outcome of trading. We do not make any concavity
assumptions. The first result is that under sequential upper-semicontinuity the
market model can be represented as a normal integrand. We then extend the
concept of no-arbitrage to this setup and study its consequences as the super-
hedging theorem and utility maximization. Finally, we show how to extend the
concepts and results to the case of vector-valued market models, an example of
which is the Kabanov model of currency markets.
