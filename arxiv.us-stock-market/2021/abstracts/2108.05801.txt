Title: A Hybrid Learning Approach to Detecting Regime Switches in Financial Markets
Authors: Peter Akioyamen, Yi Zhou Tang, Hussien Hussien
ID: 2108.05801
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.05801

Financial markets are of much interest to researchers due to their dynamic and
stochastic nature. With their relations to world populations, global economies
and asset valuations, understanding, identifying and forecasting trends and
regimes are highly important. Attempts have been made to forecast market trends
by employing machine learning methodologies, while statistical techniques have
been the primary methods used in developing market regime switching models used
for trading and hedging. In this paper we present a novel framework for the
detection of regime switches within the US financial markets. Principal
component analysis is applied for dimensionality reduction and the k-means
algorithm is used as a clustering technique. Using a combination of cluster
analysis and classification, we identify regimes in financial markets based on
publicly available economic data. We display the efficacy of the framework by
constructing and assessing the performance of two trading strategies based on
detected regimes.
