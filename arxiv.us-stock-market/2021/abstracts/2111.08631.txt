Title: Spillovers of US Interest Rates: Monetary Policy & Information Effects
Authors: Santiago Camara
ID: 2111.08631
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.08631

This paper quantifies the international spillovers of US monetary policy by
exploiting the high-frequency movement of multiple financial assets around FOMC
announcements. I use the identification strategy introduced by Jarocinski &
Karadi (2022) to identify two FOMC shocks: a pure US monetary policy and an
information disclosure shock. These two FOMC shocks have intuitive and very
different international spillovers. On the one hand, a US tightening caused by a
pure US monetary policy shock leads to an economic recession, an exchange rate
depreciation and tighter financial conditions. On the other hand, a tightening
of US monetary policy caused by the FOMC disclosing positive information about
the state of the US economy leads to an economic expansion, an exchange rate
appreciation and looser financial conditions. Ignoring the disclosure of
information by the FOMC biases the impact of a US monetary policy tightening and
may explain recent atypical findings.
