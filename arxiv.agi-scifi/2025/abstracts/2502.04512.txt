Title: Safety is Essential for Responsible Open-Ended Systems
Authors: Ivaxi Sheth, Jan Wehner, Sahar Abdelnabi, Ruta Binkyte, Mario Fritz
ID: 2502.04512
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.04512

Abstract:
AI advancements have been significantly driven by a combination of foundation
models and curiosity-driven learning aimed at increasing capability and
adaptability. A growing area of interest within this field is Open-Endedness -
the ability of AI systems to continuously and autonomously generate novel and
diverse artifacts or solutions. This has become relevant for accelerating
scientific discovery and enabling continual adaptation in AI agents. This
position paper argues that the inherently dynamic and self-propagating nature of
Open-Ended AI introduces significant, underexplored risks, including challenges
in maintaining alignment, predictability, and control. This paper systematically
examines these challenges, proposes mitigation strategies, and calls for action
for different stakeholders to support the safe, responsible and successful
development of Open-Ended AI.
