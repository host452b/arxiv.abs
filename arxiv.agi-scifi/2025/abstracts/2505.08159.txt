Title: Self-Optimizing Machine Learning Potential Assisted Automated Workflow
for Highly Efficient Complex Systems Material Design
Authors: Jiaxiang Li, Junwei Feng, Jie Luo, Bowen Jiang, Xiangyu Zheng, Qigang
Song, Jian Lv, Keith Butler, Hanyu Liu, Congwei Xie, Yu Xie, Yanming Ma
ID: 2505.08159
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.08159

Abstract:
Machine learning interatomic potentials have revolutionized complex materials
design by enabling rapid exploration of material configurational spaces via
crystal structure prediction with ab initio accuracy. However, critical
challenges persist in ensuring robust generalization to unknown structures and
minimizing the requirement for substantial expert knowledge and time-consuming
manual interventions. Here, we propose an automated crystal structure prediction
framework built upon the attention-coupled neural networks potential to address
these limitations. The generalizability of the potential is achieved by sampling
regions across the local minima of the potential energy surface, where the self-
evolving pipeline autonomously refines the potential iteratively while
minimizing human intervention. The workflow is validated on Mg-Ca-H ternary and
Be-P-N-O quaternary systems by exploring nearly 10 million configurations,
demonstrating substantial speedup compared to first-principles calculations.
These results underscore the effectiveness of our approach in accelerating the
exploration and discovery of complex multi-component functional materials.
