Title: AI-assisted 3D Preservation and Reconstruction of Temple Arts
Authors: Naai-Jung Shih
ID: 2503.10031
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.10031

Abstract:
How does AI connect to the past in conservation? What can 17 years old photos be
helpful in a renewed effort of preservation? This research aims to use AI to
connect both in a seamless 3D reconstruction of heritage from imagery data taken
from Gongfan Palace, Yunlin Taiwan. AI-assisted 3D modeling was used to
reconstruct correspondent details across different 3D platforms of 3DGS or NeRF
models generated by Postshot or KIRI Engine. Polygon or point models by Zephyr
were referred to and assessed in two sets. The results also include AI-assist
modeling outcomes in Stable Diffusion and Postshot-based animation. The evolved
documenta-tion and interpretation in AI presents a novel arrangement of working
processes contributed by new structure and management of resources, formats, and
interfaces, as a continuous preservation effort.
