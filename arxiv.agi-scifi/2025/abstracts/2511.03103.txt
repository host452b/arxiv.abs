Title: Adaptive Detection of Software Aging under Workload Shift
Authors: Rafael Jose Moura Silva, Maria Gizele Nascimento, Fumio Machida,
Ermeson Andrade
ID: 2511.03103
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.03103

Abstract:
Software aging is a phenomenon that affects long-running systems, leading to
progressive performance degradation and increasing the risk of failures. To
mitigate this problem, this work proposes an adaptive approach based on machine
learning for software aging detection in environments subject to dynamic
workload conditions. We evaluate and compare a static model with adaptive models
that incorporate adaptive detectors, specifically the Drift Detection Method
(DDM) and Adaptive Windowing (ADWIN), originally developed for concept drift
scenarios and applied in this work to handle workload shifts. Experiments with
simulated sudden, gradual, and recurring workload transitions show that static
models suffer a notable performance drop when applied to unseen workload
profiles, whereas the adaptive model with ADWIN maintains high accuracy,
achieving an F1-Score above 0.93 in all analyzed scenarios.
