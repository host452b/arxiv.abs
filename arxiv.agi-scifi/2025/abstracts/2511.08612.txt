Title: Learning based Modelling of Throttleable Engine Dynamics for Lunar
Landing Mission
Authors: Suraj Kumar, Aditya Rallapalli, Bharat Kumar GVP
ID: 2511.08612
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.08612

Abstract:
Typical lunar landing missions involve multiple phases of braking to achieve
soft-landing. The propulsion system configuration for these missions consists of
throttleable engines. This configuration involves complex interconnected
hydraulic, mechanical, and pneumatic components each exhibiting non-linear
dynamic characteristics. Accurate modelling of the propulsion dynamics is
essential for analyzing closed-loop guidance and control schemes during descent.
This paper presents a learning-based system identification approach for
modelling of throttleable engine dynamics using data obtained from high-fidelity
propulsion model. The developed model is validated with experimental results and
used for closed-loop guidance and control simulations.
