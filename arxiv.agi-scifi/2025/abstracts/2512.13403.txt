Title: diffhydro: Inverse Multiphysics Modeling and Embedded Machine Learning in
Astrophysical Flows
Authors: Benjamin Horowitz, Zarija Lukić, Kentaro Nagamine, Yuri Oku
ID: 2512.13403
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.13403

Abstract:
We present the extension of the differentiable hydrodynamics code, diffhydro,
enabling scalable PDE-constrained inference and integrated hybrid physics-ML
models for a wide range of astrophysical applications. New physics additions
include radiative heating/cooling, OU-driven turbulence, and self-gravity via
multigrid Poisson. We demonstrate good agreement with the Athena++ code on
standard validation tests such as Sedov-Taylor, Kelvin-Helmholtz, and
driven/decaying turbulence. We further introduce a solver-in-the-loop neural
corrector that reduces coarse-grid errors during time integration while
preserving stability. The addition of custom adjoints facilitates efficient end-
to-end gradients and multi-device scaling. We present simulations up to 1024^3
elements, run on distributed GPU systems, and we show gradient-based
reconstructions of complex initial conditions in turbulent, self-gravitating,
radiatively cooling flows. The code is written in JAX, and the solver's modular
finite-volume components are compiled by XLA into fused accelerator kernels,
delivering high-throughput forward runs and tractable differentiation through
long integrations.
