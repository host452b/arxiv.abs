Title: Multispin Physics of AI Tipping Points and Hallucinations
Authors: Neil F. Johnson, Frank Yingjie Huo
ID: 2508.01097
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.01097

Abstract:
Output from generative AI such as ChatGPT, can be repetitive and biased. But
more worrying is that this output can mysteriously tip mid-response from good
(correct) to bad (misleading or wrong) without the user noticing. In 2024 alone,
this reportedly caused $67 billion in losses and several deaths. Establishing a
mathematical mapping to a multispin thermal system, we reveal a hidden tipping
instability at the scale of the AI's 'atom' (basic Attention head). We derive a
simple but essentially exact formula for this tipping point which shows directly
the impact of a user's prompt choice and the AI's training bias. We then show
how the output tipping can get amplified by the AI's multilayer architecture. As
well as helping improve AI transparency, explainability and performance, our
results open a path to quantifying users' AI risk and legal liabilities.
