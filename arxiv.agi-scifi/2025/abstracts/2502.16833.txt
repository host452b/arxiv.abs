Title: An Exploratory Study on How AI Awareness Impacts Human-AI Design
Collaboration
Authors: Zhuoyi Cheng, Pei Chen, Wenzheng Song, Hongbo Zhang, Zhuoshu Li,
Lingyun Sun
ID: 2502.16833
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.16833

Abstract:
The collaborative design process is intrinsically complicated and dynamic, and
researchers have long been exploring how to enhance efficiency in this process.
As Artificial Intelligence technology evolves, it has been widely used as a
design tool and exhibited the potential as a design collaborator. Nevertheless,
problems concerning how designers should communicate with AI in collaborative
design remain unsolved. To address this research gap, we referred to how
designers communicate fluently in human-human design collaboration, and found
awareness to be an important ability for facilitating communication by
understanding their collaborators and current situation. However, previous
research mainly studied and supported human awareness, the possible impact AI
awareness would bring to the human-AI collaborative design process, and the way
to realize AI awareness remain unknown. In this study, we explored how AI
awareness will impact human-AI collaboration through a Wizard-of-Oz experiment.
Both quantitative and qualitative results supported that enabling AI to have
awareness can enhance the communication fluidity between human and AI, thus
enhancing collaboration efficiency. We further discussed the results and
concluded design implications for future human-AI collaborative design systems.
