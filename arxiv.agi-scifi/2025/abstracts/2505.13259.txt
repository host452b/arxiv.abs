Title: From Automation to Autonomy: A Survey on Large Language Models in
Scientific Discovery
Authors: Tianshi Zheng, Zheye Deng, Hong Ting Tsang, Weiqi Wang, Jiaxin Bai,
Zihao Wang, Yangqiu Song
ID: 2505.13259
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.13259

Abstract:
Large Language Models (LLMs) are catalyzing a paradigm shift in scientific
discovery, evolving from task-specific automation tools into increasingly
autonomous agents and fundamentally redefining research processes and human-AI
collaboration. This survey systematically charts this burgeoning field, placing
a central focus on the changing roles and escalating capabilities of LLMs in
science. Through the lens of the scientific method, we introduce a foundational
three-level taxonomy-Tool, Analyst, and Scientist-to delineate their escalating
autonomy and evolving responsibilities within the research lifecycle. We further
identify pivotal challenges and future research trajectories such as robotic
automation, self-improvement, and ethical governance. Overall, this survey
provides a conceptual architecture and strategic foresight to navigate and shape
the future of AI-driven scientific discovery, fostering both rapid innovation
and responsible advancement. Github Repository: https://github.com/HKUST-
KnowComp/Awesome-LLM-Scientific-Discovery.
