Title: Simulated Affection, Engineered Trust: How Anthropomorphic AI Benefits
Surveillance Capitalism
Authors: Adele Olof-Ors, Martin Smit
ID: 2511.06472
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.06472

Abstract:
In this paper, we argue that anthropomorphized technology, designed to simulate
emotional realism, are not neutral tools but cognitive infrastructures that
manipulate user trust and behaviour. This reinforces the logic of surveillance
capitalism, an under-regulated economic system that profits from behavioural
manipulation and monitoring. Drawing on Nicholas Carr's theory of the
intellectual ethic, we identify how technologies such as chatbots, virtual
assistants, or generative models reshape not only what we think about ourselves
and our world, but how we think at the cognitive level. We identify how the
emerging intellectual ethic of AI benefits a system of surveillance capitalism,
and discuss the potential ways of addressing this.
