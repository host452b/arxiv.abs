Title: Grounding Language Models with Semantic Digital Twins for Robotic
Planning
Authors: Mehreen Naeem, Andrew Melnik, Michael Beetz
ID: 2506.16493
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.16493

Abstract:
We introduce a novel framework that integrates Semantic Digital Twins (SDTs)
with Large Language Models (LLMs) to enable adaptive and goal-driven robotic
task execution in dynamic environments. The system decomposes natural language
instructions into structured action triplets, which are grounded in contextual
environmental data provided by the SDT. This semantic grounding allows the robot
to interpret object affordances and interaction rules, enabling action planning
and real-time adaptability. In case of execution failures, the LLM utilizes
error feedback and SDT insights to generate recovery strategies and iteratively
revise the action plan. We evaluate our approach using tasks from the ALFRED
benchmark, demonstrating robust performance across various household scenarios.
The proposed framework effectively combines high-level reasoning with semantic
environment understanding, achieving reliable task completion in the face of
uncertainty and failure.
