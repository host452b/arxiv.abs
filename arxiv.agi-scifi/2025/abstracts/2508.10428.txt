Title: SC2Arena and StarEvolve: Benchmark and Self-Improvement Framework for
LLMs in Complex Decision-Making Tasks
Authors: Pengbo Shen, Yaqing Wang, Ni Mu, Yao Luan, Runpeng Xie, Senhao Yang,
Lexiang Wang, Hao Hu, Shuang Xu, Yiqin Yang, Bo Xu
ID: 2508.10428
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.10428

Abstract:
Evaluating large language models (LLMs) in complex decision-making is essential
for advancing AI's ability for strategic planning and real-time adaptation.
However, existing benchmarks for tasks like StarCraft II fail to capture the
game's full complexity, such as its complete game context, diverse action
spaces, and all playable races. To address this gap, we present SC2Arena, a
benchmark that fully supports all playable races, low-level action spaces, and
optimizes text-based observations to tackle spatial reasoning challenges.
Complementing this, we introduce StarEvolve, a hierarchical framework that
integrates strategic planning with tactical execution, featuring iterative self-
correction and continuous improvement via fine-tuning on high-quality gameplay
data. Its key components include a Planner-Executor-Verifier structure to break
down gameplay, and a scoring system for selecting high-quality training samples.
Comprehensive analysis using SC2Arena provides valuable insights into developing
generalist agents that were not possible with previous benchmarks. Experimental
results also demonstrate that our proposed StarEvolve achieves superior
performance in strategic planning. Our code, environment, and algorithms are
publicly available.
