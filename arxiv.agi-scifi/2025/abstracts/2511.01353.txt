Title: AI Literacy in UAE Libraries: Assessing Competencies, Training Needs, and
Ethical Considerations for the Digital Age
Authors: Zafar Imam Khan
ID: 2511.01353
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.01353

Abstract:
The study explores the current state of artificial intelligence (AI) literacy
levels among library professionals employing a quantitative approach consisting
of 92 surveys of LIS professionals in the United Arab Emirates (UAE). Findings
of the study revealed the presence of strong cognitive competencies, while there
were gaps observed in behavioral and normative competencies, especially related
to AI biases, AI-powered learning, and ethical considerations. There was a
disconnect observed between the perceived importance of AI skills and the
effectiveness of the current training programs.
