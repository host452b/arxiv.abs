Title: The Popularity Hypothesis in Software Security: A Large-Scale Replication
with PHP Packages
Authors: Jukka Ruohonen, Qusai Ramadan
ID: 2502.16670
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.16670

Abstract:
There has been a long-standing hypothesis that a software's popularity is
related to its security or insecurity in both research and popular discourse.
There are also a few empirical studies that have examined the hypothesis, either
explicitly or implicitly. The present work continues with and contributes to
this research with a replication-motivated large-scale analysis of software
written in the PHP programming language. The dataset examined contains nearly
four hundred thousand open source software packages written in PHP. According to
the results based on reported security vulnerabilities, the hypothesis does
holds; packages having been affected by vulnerabilities over their release
histories are generally more popular than packages without having been affected
by a single vulnerability. With this replication results, the paper contributes
to the efforts to strengthen the empirical knowledge base in cyber and software
security.
