Title: Entanglement Entropy in Loop Quantum Gravity through Quantum Error
Correction
Authors: Sean Tobin
ID: 2510.26911
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.26911

Abstract:
We introduce a novel method for computing entanglement entropy across surfaces
in Loop Quantum Gravity by employing techniques from quantum error correcting
codes. In this construction, the redundancy encoded in the gauge invariant
subspace is made manifest by embedding it in a larger Hilbert space. The
enlarged Hilbert space of a surface does not factorize, which necessitates an
algebraic formulation of the entanglement entropy using von Neumann algebras.
Using this approach, we are able to reproduce the expected black hole entropy
through the canonical ensemble. This includes a direct realization of the Ryu-
Takayanagi formula, providing a first principles derivation of the black hole
entropy within a kinematical framework of loop quantum gravity. The algebraic
techniques developed in this work can be used to compute the entanglement
entropy across arbitrary surfaces.
