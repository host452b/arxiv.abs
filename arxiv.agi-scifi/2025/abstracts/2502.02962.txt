Title: Intrinsic motivation as constrained entropy maximization
Authors: Alex B. Kiefer
ID: 2502.02962
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.02962

Abstract:
"Intrinsic motivation" refers to the capacity for intelligent systems to be
motivated endogenously, i.e. by features of agential architecture itself rather
than by learned associations between action and reward. This paper views active
inference, empowerment, and other formal accounts of intrinsic motivation as
variations on the theme of constrained maximum entropy inference, providing a
general perspective on intrinsic motivation complementary to existing
frameworks. The connection between free energy and empowerment noted in previous
literature is further explored, and it is argued that the maximum-occupancy
approach in practice incorporates an implicit model-evidence constraint.
