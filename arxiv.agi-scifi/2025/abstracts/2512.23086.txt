Title: Phase transition revealed by eigen microstate entropy
Authors: Teng Liu, Xuezhi Niu, Mingli Zhang, Gaoke Hu, Yuhan Chen, Yongwen
Zhang, Rui Shi, Jingyuan Li, Peng Tan, Maoxin Liu, Hui Li, Xiaosong Chen
ID: 2512.23086
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.23086

Abstract:
We introduce the eigen microstate entropy ($S_{\text{EM}}$), a novel metric of
complexity derived from the probabilities of statistically independent eigen
microstates. After establishing its scaling behavior in equilibrium systems and
demonstrating its utility in critical phenomena (mean spherical, Ising, and
Potts models), we apply $S_{\text{EM}}$ to non-equilibrium complex systems. Our
analysis reveals a consistent precursor signal: a significant increase in
$S_{\text{EM}}$ precedes major phase transitions. Specifically, we observe this
entropy rise before biomolecular condensate formation in liquid-liquid phase
separation in living cells and months ahead of El Niño events. These findings
position $S_{\text{EM}}$ as a general framework for detecting and interpreting
phase transitions in non-equilibrium systems.
