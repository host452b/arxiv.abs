Title: Refining Integration-by-Parts Reduction of Feynman Integrals with Machine
Learning
Authors: Matt von Hippel, Matthias Wilhelm
ID: 2502.05121
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.05121

Abstract:
Integration-by-parts reductions of Feynman integrals pose a frequent bottle-neck
in state-of-the-art calculations in theoretical particle and gravitational-wave
physics, and rely on heuristic approaches for selecting integration-by-parts
identities, whose quality heavily influences the performance. In this paper, we
investigate the use of machine-learning techniques to find improved heuristics.
We use funsearch, a genetic programming variant based on code generation by a
Large Language Model, in order to explore possible approaches, then use strongly
typed genetic programming to zero in on useful solutions. Both approaches manage
to re-discover the state-of-the-art heuristics recently incorporated into
integration-by-parts solvers, and in one example find a small advance on this
state of the art.
