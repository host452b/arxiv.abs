Title: Local Entropy Search over Descent Sequences for Bayesian Optimization
Authors: David Stenger, Armin Lindicke, Alexander von Rohr, Sebastian Trimpe
ID: 2511.19241
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.19241

Abstract:
Searching large and complex design spaces for a global optimum can be infeasible
and unnecessary. A practical alternative is to iteratively refine the
neighborhood of an initial design using local optimization methods such as
gradient descent. We propose local entropy search (LES), a Bayesian optimization
paradigm that explicitly targets the solutions reachable by the descent
sequences of iterative optimizers. The algorithm propagates the posterior belief
over the objective through the optimizer, resulting in a probability
distribution over descent sequences. It then selects the next evaluation by
maximizing mutual information with that distribution, using a combination of
analytic entropy calculations and Monte-Carlo sampling of descent sequences.
Empirical results on high-complexity synthetic objectives and benchmark problems
show that LES achieves strong sample efficiency compared to existing local and
global Bayesian optimization methods.
