Title: Interdisciplinary Digital Twin Engine InterTwin for calorimeter
simulation
Authors: Corentin Allaire, Vera Maiboroda, David Rousseau
ID: 2509.26527
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.26527

Abstract:
Calorimeter shower simulations are computationally expensive, and generative
models offer an efficient alternative. However, achieving a balance between
accuracy and speed remains a challenge, with distribution tail modeling being a
key limitation. Invertible generative network CaloINN provides a trade-off
between simulation quality and efficiency. The ongoing study targets introducing
a set of post-processing modifications of analysis-level observables aimed at
improving the accuracy of distribution tails. As part of interTwin project
initiative developing an open-source Digital Twin Engine, we implemented the
CaloINN within the interTwin AI framework.
