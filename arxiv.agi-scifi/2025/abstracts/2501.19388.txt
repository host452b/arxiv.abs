Title: Online Decision-Making in Tree-Like Multi-Agent Games with Transfers
Authors: Antoine Scheid, Etienne Boursier, Alain Durmus, Eric Moulines, Michael
I. Jordan
ID: 2501.19388
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.19388

Abstract:
The widespread deployment of Machine Learning systems everywhere raises
challenges, such as dealing with interactions or competition between multiple
learners. In that goal, we study multi-agent sequential decision-making by
considering principal-agent interactions in a tree structure. In this problem,
the reward of a player is influenced by the actions of her children, who are all
self-interested and non-cooperative, hence the complexity of making good
decisions. Our main finding is that it is possible to steer all the players
towards the globally optimal set of actions by simply allowing single-step
transfers between them. A transfer is established between a principal and one of
her agents: the principal actually offers the proposed payment if the agent
picks the recommended action. The analysis poses specific challenges due to the
intricate interactions between the nodes of the tree and the propagation of the
regret within this tree. Considering a bandit setup, we propose algorithmic
solutions for the players to end up being no-regret with respect to the optimal
pair of actions and incentives. In the long run, allowing transfers between
players makes them act as if they were collaborating together, although they
remain self-interested non-cooperative: transfers restore efficiency.
