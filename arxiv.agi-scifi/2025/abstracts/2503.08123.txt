Title: LLM4MAC: An LLM-Driven Reinforcement Learning Framework for MAC Protocol
Emergence
Authors: Renxuan Tan, Rongpeng Li, Zhifeng Zhao
ID: 2503.08123
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.08123

Abstract:
With the advent of 6G systems, emerging hyper-connected ecosystems necessitate
agile and adaptive medium access control (MAC) protocols to contend with network
dynamics and diverse service requirements. We propose LLM4MAC, a novel framework
that harnesses large language models (LLMs) within a reinforcement learning
paradigm to drive MAC protocol emergence. By reformulating uplink data
transmission scheduling as a semantics-generalized partially observable Markov
game (POMG), LLM4MAC encodes network operations in natural language, while
proximal policy optimization (PPO) ensures continuous alignment with the
evolving network dynamics. A structured identity embedding (SIE) mechanism
further enables robust coordination among heterogeneous agents. Extensive
simulations demonstrate that on top of a compact LLM, which is purposefully
selected to balance performance with resource efficiency, the protocol emerging
from LLM4MAC outperforms comparative baselines in throughput and generalization.
