Title: Learning Interestingness in Automated Mathematical Theory Formation
Authors: George Tsoukalas, Rahul Saha, Amitayush Thakur, Sabrina Reguyal, Swarat
Chaudhuri
ID: 2511.14778
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.14778

Abstract:
We take two key steps in automating the open-ended discovery of new mathematical
theories, a grand challenge in artificial intelligence. First, we introduce
$\emph{FERMAT}$, a reinforcement learning (RL) environment that models concept
discovery and theorem-proving using a set of symbolic actions, opening up a
range of RL problems relevant to theory discovery. Second, we explore a specific
problem through $\emph{FERMAT}$: automatically scoring the
$\emph{interestingness}$ of mathematical objects. We investigate evolutionary
algorithms for synthesizing nontrivial interestingness measures. In particular,
we introduce an LLM-based evolutionary algorithm that features function
abstraction, leading to notable improvements in discovering elementary number
theory and finite fields over hard-coded baselines. We open-source the
$\emph{FERMAT}$ environment at this URL(https://github.com/trishullab/Fermat).
