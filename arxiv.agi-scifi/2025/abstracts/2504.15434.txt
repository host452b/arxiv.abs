Title: AGI Is Coming... Right After AI Learns to Play Wordle
Authors: Sarath Shekkizhar, Romain Cosentino
ID: 2504.15434
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.15434

Abstract:
This paper investigates multimodal agents, in particular, OpenAI's Computer-User
Agent (CUA), trained to control and complete tasks through a standard computer
interface, similar to humans. We evaluated the agent's performance on the New
York Times Wordle game to elicit model behaviors and identify shortcomings. Our
findings revealed a significant discrepancy in the model's ability to recognize
colors correctly depending on the context. The model had a $5.36\%$ success rate
over several hundred runs across a week of Wordle. Despite the immense
enthusiasm surrounding AI agents and their potential to usher in Artificial
General Intelligence (AGI), our findings reinforce the fact that even simple
tasks present substantial challenges for today's frontier AI models. We conclude
with a discussion of the potential underlying causes, implications for future
development, and research directions to improve these AI systems.
