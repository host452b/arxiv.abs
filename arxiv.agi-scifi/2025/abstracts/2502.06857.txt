Title: Gemstones: A Model Suite for Multi-Faceted Scaling Laws
Authors: Sean McLeish, John Kirchenbauer, David Yu Miller, Siddharth Singh,
Abhinav Bhatele, Micah Goldblum, Ashwinee Panda, Tom Goldstein
ID: 2502.06857
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.06857

Abstract:
Scaling laws are typically fit using a family of models with a narrow range of
frozen hyperparameter choices. In this work we study scaling laws using multiple
architectural shapes and hyperparameter choices, highlighting their impact on
resulting prescriptions. As a primary artifact of our research, we release the
Gemstones: an open-source scaling law dataset, consisting of over 4000
checkpoints from transformers with up to 2 billion parameters and diverse
architectural shapes; including ablations over learning rate and cooldown. Our
checkpoints enable more complex studies of scaling, such as analyzing the
relationship between width and depth. By examining our model suite, we find that
the prescriptions of scaling laws can be highly sensitive to the experimental
design process and the specific model checkpoints used during fitting.
