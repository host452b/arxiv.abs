Title: Physics-informed digital twins of brainbots
Authors: Isa Mammadli, Jayant Pande, Martial Noirhomme, Felix Novkoski, Andreas
Maier, Nicolas Vandewalle, Ana-Suncana Smith
ID: 2511.00384
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.00384

Abstract:
A brainbot is a robotic device powered by a battery-driven motor that induces
horizontal vibrations which lead to controlled two-dimensional motion. While the
physical design and capabilities of a brainbot have been discussed in previous
work, here we present a detailed theoretical analysis of its motion. We show
that the various autonomous trajectories executed by a brainbot -- linear,
spinning, orbital and helical -- are explained by a kinematic model that
ascribes angular and translational velocities to the brainbot's body. This model
also uncovers some trajectories that have not so far been observed
experimentally. Using this kinematic framework, we present a simulation system
that accurately reproduces the experimental trajectories. This can be used to
parameterize a digital twin of a brainbot that executes synthetic trajectories
that faithfully mimic the required statistical features of the experimental
trajectories while being as long as required, such as for machine learning
applications.
