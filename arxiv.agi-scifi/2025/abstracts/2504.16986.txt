Title: A Nonperturbative Toolkit for Quantum Gravity
Authors: Vijay Balasubramanian, Tom Yildirim
ID: 2504.16986
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.16986

Abstract:
We propose a method for demonstrating equivalences beyond the saddlepoint
approximation between quantities in quantum gravity that are defined by the
Euclidean path integral, without assumptions about holographic duality. The
method involves three ingredients: (1) a way of resolving the identity with an
overcomplete basis of microstates that is under semiclassical control, (2) a
drastic simplification of the sum over topologies in the limit where the basis
is infinitely overcomplete, and (3) a way of cutting and splicing geometries to
demonstrate equality between two different gravitational path integrals even if
neither can be explicitly computed. We illustrate our methods by giving a
general argument that the thermal partition function of quantum gravity with two
boundaries factorises. One implication of our results is that universes
containing a horizon can sometimes be understood as superpositions of
horizonless geometries entangled with a closed universe.
