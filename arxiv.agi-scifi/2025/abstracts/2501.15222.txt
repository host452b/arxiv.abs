Title: Explainable autoencoder for neutron star dense matter parameter
estimation
Authors: Francesco Di Clemente, Matteo Scialpi, Michał Bejger
ID: 2501.15222
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.15222

Abstract:
We present a physics-informed autoencoder designed to encode the equation of
state of neutron stars into an interpretable latent space. In particular the
input will be encoded in the mass, radius, and tidal deformability values of a
neutron star. Unlike traditional black-box models, our approach incorporates
additional loss functions to enforce explainability in the encoded
representations. This method enhances the transparency of machine learning
models in physics, providing a robust proof-of-concept tool to study compact
stars data. Our results demonstrate that the proposed autoencoder not only
accurately estimates the EoS parameters and central density/pressure but also
offers insights into the physical connection between equation of state and
observable physical quantities. This framework conceptualizes the physical
differential equations themselves as the ``encoders", allowing interpretability
of the latent space.
