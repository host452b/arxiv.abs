Title: Quantum and Hybrid Machine-Learning Models for Materials-Science Tasks
Authors: Leyang Wang, Yilun Gong, Zongrui Pei
ID: 2507.08155
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.08155

Abstract:
Quantum computing has become increasingly practical in solving real-world
problems due to advances in hardware and algorithms. In this paper, we aim to
design and estimate quantum machine learning and hybrid quantum-classical models
in a few practical materials science tasks, i.e., predicting stacking fault
energies and solutes that can ductilize magnesium. To this end, we adopt two
different representative quantum algorithms, i.e., quantum support vector
machines (QSVM) and quantum neural networks (QNN), and adjust them to our
application scenarios. We systematically test the performance with respect to
the hyperparameters of selected ansatzes. We identify a few combinations of
hyperparameters that yield validation scores of approximately 90\% for QSVM and
hybrid QNN in both tasks. Eventually, we construct quantum models with optimized
parameters for regression and classification that predict targeted solutes based
on the elemental volumes, electronegativities, and bulk moduli of chemical
elements.
