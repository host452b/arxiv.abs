Title: Words Matter: Forecasting Economic Downside Risks with Corporate Textual
Data
Authors: Cansu Isler
ID: 2511.04935
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.04935

Abstract:
Accurate forecasting of downside risks to economic growth is critically
important for policymakers and financial institutions, particularly in the wake
of recent economic crises. This paper extends the Growth-at-Risk (GaR) approach
by introducing a novel daily sentiment indicator derived from textual analysis
of mandatory corporate disclosures (SEC 10-K and 10-Q reports) to forecast
downside risks to economic growth. Using the Loughran--McDonald dictionary and a
word-count methodology, I compute firm-level tone growth as the year-over-year
difference between positive and negative sentiment expressed in corporate
filings. These firm-specific sentiment metrics are aggregated into a weekly tone
index, weighted by firms' market capitalizations to capture broader, economy-
wide sentiment dynamics. Integrated into a mixed-data sampling (MIDAS) quantile
regression framework, this sentiment-based indicator enhances the prediction of
GDP growth downturns, outperforming traditional financial market indicators such
as the National Financial Conditions Index (NFCI). The findings underscore
corporate textual data as a powerful and timely resource for macroeconomic risk
assessment and informed policymaking.
