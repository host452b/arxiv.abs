Title: Human Digital Twin: Data, Models, Applications, and Challenges
Authors: Rong Pan, Hongyue Sun, Xiaoyu Chen, Giulia Pedrielli, Jiapeng Huang
ID: 2508.13138
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.13138

Abstract:
Human digital twins (HDTs) are dynamic, data-driven virtual representations of
individuals, continuously updated with multimodal data to simulate, monitor, and
predict health trajectories. By integrating clinical, physiological, behavioral,
and environmental inputs, HDTs enable personalized diagnostics, treatment
planning, and anomaly detection. This paper reviews current approaches to HDT
modeling, with a focus on statistical and machine learning techniques, including
recent advances in anomaly detection and failure prediction. It also discusses
data integration, computational methods, and ethical, technological, and
regulatory challenges in deploying HDTs for precision healthcare.
