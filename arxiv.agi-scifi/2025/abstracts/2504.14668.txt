Title: A Byzantine Fault Tolerance Approach towards AI Safety
Authors: John deVadoss, Matthias Artzt
ID: 2504.14668
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.14668

Abstract:
Ensuring that an AI system behaves reliably and as intended, especially in the
presence of unexpected faults or adversarial conditions, is a complex challenge.
Inspired by the field of Byzantine Fault Tolerance (BFT) from distributed
computing, we explore a fault tolerance architecture for AI safety. By drawing
an analogy between unreliable, corrupt, misbehaving or malicious AI artifacts
and Byzantine nodes in a distributed system, we propose an architecture that
leverages consensus mechanisms to enhance AI safety and reliability.
