Title: Scaling Laws for Deepfake Detection
Authors: Wenhao Wang, Longqi Cai, Taihong Xiao, Yuxiao Wang, Ming-Hsuan Yang
ID: 2510.16320
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.16320

Abstract:
This paper presents a systematic study of scaling laws for the deepfake
detection task. Specifically, we analyze the model performance against the
number of real image domains, deepfake generation methods, and training images.
Since no existing dataset meets the scale requirements for this research, we
construct ScaleDF, the largest dataset to date in this field, which contains
over 5.8 million real images from 51 different datasets (domains) and more than
8.8 million fake images generated by 102 deepfake methods. Using ScaleDF, we
observe power-law scaling similar to that shown in large language models (LLMs).
Specifically, the average detection error follows a predictable power-law decay
as either the number of real domains or the number of deepfake methods
increases. This key observation not only allows us to forecast the number of
additional real domains or deepfake methods required to reach a target
performance, but also inspires us to counter the evolving deepfake technology in
a data-centric manner. Beyond this, we examine the role of pre-training and data
augmentations in deepfake detection under scaling, as well as the limitations of
scaling itself.
