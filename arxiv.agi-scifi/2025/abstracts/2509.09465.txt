Title: Enhancing Optical Imaging via Quantum Computation
Authors: Aleksandr Mokeev, Babak Saif, Mikhail D. Lukin, Johannes Borregaard
ID: 2509.09465
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.09465

Abstract:
Extracting information from weak optical signals is a critical challenge across
a broad range of technologies. Conventional imaging techniques, constrained to
integrating over detected signals and classical post-processing, are limited in
signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) from shot noise accumulation in the post-processing
algorithms. We show that these limitations can be circumvented by coherently
encoding photonic amplitude information into qubit registers and applying
quantum algorithms to process the stored information from asynchronously
arriving optical signals. As a specific example, we develop a quantum algorithm
for imaging unresolved point sources and apply it to exoplanet detection. We
demonstrate that orders-of-magnitude improvements in performance can be achieved
under realistic imaging conditions using relatively small scale quantum
processors.
