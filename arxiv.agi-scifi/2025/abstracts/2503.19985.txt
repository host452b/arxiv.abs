Title: Scaling laws for passive polymer dynamics in active turbulence
Authors: Zahra K. Valei, Tyler N. Shendruk
ID: 2503.19985
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.19985

Abstract:
Biological systems commonly combine intrinsically out-of-equilibrium active
components with passive polymeric inclusions to produce unique material
properties. To explore these composite systems, idealized models - such as
polymers in active fluids - are essential to develop a predictive theoretical
framework. We simulate a single, freely jointed passive chain in two-dimensional
active turbulence. Active flows advect the polymer, producing a substantially
enhanced diffusivity. Our results reveal that the dimensionless diffusivity
obeys scaling laws governed by the Péclet, Weissenberg, and Ericksen numbers,
which paves the way for designing active/polymeric hybrid materials with
predictable properties that differ significantly from those of nondeformable
inclusions.
