Title: Arecibo Wow! II: Revised Properties of the Wow! Signal from Archival Ohio
SETI Data
Authors: Abel Méndez, Kevin N. Ortiz Ceballos, Jorge I. Zuluaga, Kelby D.
Palencia-Torres, Allison J. Smith, Alondra Cardona Rodríguez, Héctor Socas-
Navarro, David Kipping, Hodari-Sadiki Hubbard-James, Mai Le, Alejandro Rincón-
Torres
ID: 2508.10657
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.10657

Abstract:
The Wow! Signal, detected in 1977 by the Ohio State University SETI project,
remains one of the most intriguing unexplained radio transients. The most recent
significant revision of its properties took place in the late 1990s; however,
further advances were limited by readily available data from this event. Here we
retrieved and analyzed decades of previously unpublished Ohio SETI observations,
enabling the most comprehensive re-evaluation of the properties of the Wow!
Signal to date with modern methods. Our results reveal significant revisions to
its parameters that may help explain why its source has been so difficult to
identify. We refine its potential origin to two adjacent fields centered on the
right ascension $α=19^{\mathrm h}25^{\mathrm m}02^{\mathrm s} \pm 3^{\mathrm s}$
or $19^{\mathrm h}27^{\mathrm m}55^{\mathrm s} \pm 3^{\mathrm s}$, and the
declination $δ=-26^°57' \pm 20'$ (J2000), a location both narrower and slightly
displaced from earlier estimates. We measure a higher peak flux density
exceeding 250 Jy and a frequency of $1420.726 \pm 0.005$ MHz, implying a
galactic source with a substantially higher radial velocity than previously
assumed. Our analysis provides additional support for the hypothesis that the
Wow! Signal most likely had an astrophysical origin rather than being attributed
to radio interference. In particular, we confirm that small, cold HI clouds can
produce narrowband signals similar to its detection, which might suggest a
common origin. These findings provide the most precise constraints to date on
the location, intensity, and frequency of the Wow! Signal and offer a new path
to identify its origin.
