Title: Balancing Safety and Helpfulness in Healthcare AI Assistants through
Iterative Preference Alignment
Authors: Huy Nghiem, Swetasudha Panda, Devashish Khatwani, Huy V. Nguyen,
Krishnaram Kenthapadi, Hal Daumé
ID: 2512.04210
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.04210

Abstract:
Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly used in healthcare, yet ensuring
their safety and trustworthiness remains a barrier to deployment. Conversational
medical assistants must avoid unsafe compliance without over-refusing benign
queries. We present an iterative post-deployment alignment framework that
applies Kahneman-Tversky Optimization (KTO) and Direct Preference Optimization
(DPO) to refine models against domain-specific safety signals. Using the
CARES-18K benchmark for adversarial robustness, we evaluate four LLMs
(Llama-3B/8B, Meditron-8B, Mistral-7B) across multiple cycles. Our results show
up to 42% improvement in safety-related metrics for harmful query detection,
alongside interesting trade-offs against erroneous refusals, thereby exposing
architecture-dependent calibration biases. We also perform ablation studies to
identify when self-evaluation is reliable and when external or finetuned judges
are necessary to maximize performance gains. Our findings underscore the
importance of adopting best practices that balance patient safety, user trust,
and clinical utility in the design of conversational medical assistants.
