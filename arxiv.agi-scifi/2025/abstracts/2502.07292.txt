Title: Investigating Creativity in Humans and Generative AI Through Circles
Exercises
Authors: Runlin Duan, Shao-Kang Hsia, Yuzhao Chen, Yichen Hu, Ming Yin, Karthik
Ramani
ID: 2502.07292
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.07292

Abstract:
Generative AI (GenAI) is transforming the creativity process. However, as
presented in this paper, GenAI encounters "narrow creativity" barriers. We
observe that both humans and GenAI focus on limited subsets of the design space.
We investigate this phenomenon using the "Circles Exercise," a creativity test
widely used to examine the creativity of humans. Quantitative analysis reveals
that humans tend to generate familiar, high-frequency ideas, while GenAI
produces a larger volume of incremental innovations at a low cost. However,
similar to humans, it struggles to significantly expand creative boundaries.
Moreover, advanced prompting strategies, such as Chain-of-Thought (CoT)
prompting, mitigate narrow creativity issues but still fall short of
substantially broadening the creative scope of humans and GenAI. These findings
underscore both the challenges and opportunities for advancing GenAI-powered
human creativity support tools.
