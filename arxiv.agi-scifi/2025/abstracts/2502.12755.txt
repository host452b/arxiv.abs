Title: Efficient Machine Translation Corpus Generation: Integrating Human-in-
the-Loop Post-Editing with Large Language Models
Authors: Kamer Ali Yuksel, Ahmet Gunduz, Abdul Baseet Anees, Hassan Sawaf
ID: 2502.12755
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.12755

Abstract:
This paper introduces an advanced methodology for machine translation (MT)
corpus generation, integrating semi-automated, human-in-the-loop post-editing
with large language models (LLMs) to enhance efficiency and translation quality.
Building upon previous work that utilized real-time training of a custom MT
quality estimation metric, this system incorporates novel LLM features such as
Enhanced Translation Synthesis and Assisted Annotation Analysis, which improve
initial translation hypotheses and quality assessments, respectively.
Additionally, the system employs LLM-Driven Pseudo Labeling and a Translation
Recommendation System to reduce human annotator workload in specific contexts.
These improvements not only retain the original benefits of cost reduction and
enhanced post-edit quality but also open new avenues for leveraging cutting-edge
LLM advancements. The project's source code is available for community use,
promoting collaborative developments in the field. The demo video can be
accessed here.
