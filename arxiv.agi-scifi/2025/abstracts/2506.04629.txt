Title: Analog dual classifier via a time-modulated neuromorphic metasurface
Authors: M. Mousa, M. Moghaddaszadeh, M. Nouh
ID: 2506.04629
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.04629

Abstract:
A neuromorphic metasurface embodies mechanical intelligence by realizing
physical neural architectures. It exploits guided wave scattering to conduct
computations in an analog manner. Through multiple tuned waveguides, the
neuromorphic system recognizes the features of an input signal and self-
identifies its classification label. The computational input is introduced to
the system through mechanical excitations at one edge, generating elastic waves
that traverse multiple layers of resonant metasurfaces. These metasurfaces
possess a tunable phase akin to trainable parameters in deep learning
algorithms. While early efforts have been promising, the well-established
constraints on wave propagation in finite media limit such systems to single-
task realizations. In this work, we devise a dual classifier neuromorphic
metasurface and demonstrate its effectiveness in carrying out two completely
independent classification problems that are concurrently carried out in
parallel, thus addressing a major bottleneck in physical computing systems.
Parallelization is achieved through smart multiplexing of the carrier
computational frequency, enabled by prescribed temporal modulations of the
embedded waveguides. The presented theory and results pave the way for new
paradigms in wave-based computing systems, which have been elusive thus far.
