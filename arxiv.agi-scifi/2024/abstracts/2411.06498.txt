Title: Barriers to Complexity-Theoretic Proofs that Achieving AGI Using Machine
Learning is Intractable
Authors: Michael Guerzhoy
ID: 2411.06498
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.06498

Abstract:
A recent paper (van Rooij et al. 2024) claims to have proved that achieving
human-like intelligence using learning from data is intractable in a complexity-
theoretic sense. We identify that the proof relies on an unjustified assumption
about the distribution of (input, output) pairs to the system. We briefly
discuss that assumption in the context of two fundamental barriers to repairing
the proof: the need to precisely define ``human-like," and the need to account
for the fact that a particular machine learning system will have particular
inductive biases that are key to the analysis.
