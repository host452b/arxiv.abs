Title: Stability of Quantum Computers
Authors: Samudra Dasgupta
ID: 2404.19082
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.19082

Abstract:
Quantum computing's potential is immense, promising super-polynomial reductions
in execution time, energy use, and memory requirements compared to classical
computers. This technology has the power to revolutionize scientific
applications such as simulating many-body quantum systems for molecular
structure understanding, factorization of large integers, enhance machine
learning, and in the process, disrupt industries like telecommunications,
material science, pharmaceuticals and artificial intelligence. However, quantum
computing's potential is curtailed by noise, further complicated by non-
stationary noise parameter distributions across time and qubits. This
dissertation focuses on the persistent issue of noise in quantum computing,
particularly non-stationarity of noise parameters in transmon processors.
