Title: On the Scaling Laws of Geographical Representation in Language Models
Authors: Nathan Godey, Éric de la Clergerie, Benoît Sagot
ID: 2402.19406
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.19406

Abstract:
Language models have long been shown to embed geographical information in their
hidden representations. This line of work has recently been revisited by
extending this result to Large Language Models (LLMs). In this paper, we propose
to fill the gap between well-established and recent literature by observing how
geographical knowledge evolves when scaling language models. We show that
geographical knowledge is observable even for tiny models, and that it scales
consistently as we increase the model size. Notably, we observe that larger
language models cannot mitigate the geographical bias that is inherent to the
training data.
