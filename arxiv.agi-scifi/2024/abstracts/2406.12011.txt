Title: The Benefits and Risks of Transductive Approaches for AI Fairness
Authors: Muhammed Razzak, Andreas Kirsch, Yarin Gal
ID: 2406.12011
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.12011

Abstract:
Recently, transductive learning methods, which leverage holdout sets during
training, have gained popularity for their potential to improve speed, accuracy,
and fairness in machine learning models. Despite this, the composition of the
holdout set itself, particularly the balance of sensitive sub-groups, has been
largely overlooked. Our experiments on CIFAR and CelebA datasets show that
compositional changes in the holdout set can substantially influence fairness
metrics. Imbalanced holdout sets exacerbate existing disparities, while balanced
holdouts can mitigate issues introduced by imbalanced training data. These
findings underline the necessity of constructing holdout sets that are both
diverse and representative.
