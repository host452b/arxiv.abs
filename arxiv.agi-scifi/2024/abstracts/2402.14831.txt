Title: Roles of Energy and Entropy in Multiscale Dynamics and Thermodynamics
Authors: Miroslav Grmela
ID: 2402.14831
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.14831

Abstract:
Multiscale thermodynamics is a theory of relations among levels of description.
Energy and entropy are its two main ingredients. Their roles in the time
evolution describing approach of a level (starting level) to another level
involving less details (target level) is examined on several examples, including
the level on which macroscopic systems are seen as composed of microscopic
particles, mesoscopic levels as kinetic theory of ideal and van der Waals gases,
fluid mechanics, the level of chemical kinetics, and the level of equilibrium
thermodynamics. The entropy enters the emergence of the target level in two
roles. It expresses internal energy, that is the part of the energy that cannot
be expressed in terms of the state variables used on the starting level, and it
reveals emerging features characterizing the target level by sweeping away
unimportant details. In the case when the target level is a mesoscopic level
involving time evolution the roles of the energy and the entropy is taken by two
different potentials that are related to their rates.
