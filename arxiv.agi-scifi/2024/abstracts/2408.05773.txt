Title: Neurosymbolic Methods for Rule Mining
Authors: Agnieszka Lawrynowicz, Luis Galarraga, Mehwish Alam, Berenice Jaulmes,
Vaclav Zeman, Tomas Kliegr
ID: 2408.05773
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05773

Abstract:
In this chapter, we address the problem of rule mining, beginning with essential
background information, including measures of rule quality. We then explore
various rule mining methodologies, categorized into three groups: inductive
logic programming, path sampling and generalization, and linear programming.
Following this, we delve into neurosymbolic methods, covering topics such as the
integration of deep learning with rules, the use of embeddings for rule
learning, and the application of large language models in rule learning.
