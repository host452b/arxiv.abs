Title: Mechanical Self-replication
Authors: Ralph P. Lano
ID: 2407.14556
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.14556

Abstract:
This study presents a theoretical model for a self-replicating mechanical system
inspired by biological processes within living cells and supported by computer
simulations. The model decomposes self-replication into core components, each of
which is executed by a single machine constructed from a set of basic block
types. Key functionalities such as sorting, copying, and building, are
demonstrated. The model provides valuable insights into the constraints of self-
replicating systems. The discussion also addresses the spatial and timing
behavior of the system, as well as its efficiency and complexity. This work
provides a foundational framework for future studies on self-replicating
mechanisms and their information-processing applications.
