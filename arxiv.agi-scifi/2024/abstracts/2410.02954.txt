Title: Digital Twin for O-RAN Towards 6G
Authors: Huan X. Nguyen, Kexuan Sun, Duc To, Quoc-Tuan Vien, Tuan Anh Le
ID: 2410.02954
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02954

Abstract:
In future wireless systems of beyond 5G and 6G, addressing diverse applications
with varying quality requirements is essential. Open Radio Access Network
(O-RAN) architectures offer the potential for dynamic resource adaptation based
on traffic demands. However, achieving real-time resource orchestration remains
a challenge. Simultaneously, Digital Twin (DT) technology holds promise for
testing and analysing complex systems, offering a unique platform for addressing
dynamic operation and automation in O-RAN architectures. Yet, developing DTs for
complex 5G/6G networks poses challenges, including data exchanges, ML model
training data availability, network dynamics, processing power limitations,
interdisciplinary collaboration needs, and a lack of standardized methodologies.
This paper provides an overview of Open RAN architecture, trend and challenges,
proposing the DT concepts for O-RAN with solution examples showcasing its
integration into the framework.
