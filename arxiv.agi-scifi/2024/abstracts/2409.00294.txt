Title: Quantum Machine Learning for Anomaly Detection in Consumer Electronics
Authors: Sounak Bhowmik, Himanshu Thapliyal
ID: 2409.00294
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.00294

Abstract:
Anomaly detection is a crucial task in cyber security. Technological advancement
brings new cyber-physical threats like network intrusion, financial fraud,
identity theft, and property invasion. In the rapidly changing world, with
frequently emerging new types of anomalies, classical machine learning models
are insufficient to prevent all the threats. Quantum Machine Learning (QML) is
emerging as a powerful computational tool that can detect anomalies more
efficiently. In this work, we have introduced QML and its applications for
anomaly detection in consumer electronics. We have shown a generic framework for
applying QML algorithms in anomaly detection tasks. We have also briefly
discussed popular supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning-based QML
algorithms and included five case studies of recent works to show their
applications in anomaly detection in the consumer electronics field.
