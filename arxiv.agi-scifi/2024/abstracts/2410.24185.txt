Title: DexMimicGen: Automated Data Generation for Bimanual Dexterous
Manipulation via Imitation Learning
Authors: Zhenyu Jiang, Yuqi Xie, Kevin Lin, Zhenjia Xu, Weikang Wan, Ajay
Mandlekar, Linxi Fan, Yuke Zhu
ID: 2410.24185
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.24185

Abstract:
Imitation learning from human demonstrations is an effective means to teach
robots manipulation skills. But data acquisition is a major bottleneck in
applying this paradigm more broadly, due to the amount of cost and human effort
involved. There has been significant interest in imitation learning for bimanual
dexterous robots, like humanoids. Unfortunately, data collection is even more
challenging here due to the challenges of simultaneously controlling multiple
arms and multi-fingered hands. Automated data generation in simulation is a
compelling, scalable alternative to fuel this need for data. To this end, we
introduce DexMimicGen, a large-scale automated data generation system that
synthesizes trajectories from a handful of human demonstrations for humanoid
robots with dexterous hands. We present a collection of simulation environments
in the setting of bimanual dexterous manipulation, spanning a range of
manipulation behaviors and different requirements for coordination among the two
arms. We generate 21K demos across these tasks from just 60 source human demos
and study the effect of several data generation and policy learning decisions on
agent performance. Finally, we present a real-to-sim-to-real pipeline and deploy
it on a real-world humanoid can sorting task. Generated datasets, simulation
environments and additional results are at https://dexmimicgen.github.io/
