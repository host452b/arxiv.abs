Title: Inferring Belief States in Partially-Observable Human-Robot Teams
Authors: Jack Kolb, Karen M. Feigh
ID: 2403.11955
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.11955

Abstract:
We investigate the real-time estimation of human situation awareness using
observations from a robot teammate with limited visibility. In human factors and
human-autonomy teaming, it is recognized that individuals navigate their
environments using an internal mental simulation, or mental model. The mental
model informs cognitive processes including situation awareness, contextual
reasoning, and task planning. In teaming domains, the mental model includes a
team model of each teammate's beliefs and capabilities, enabling fluent teamwork
without the need for explicit communication. However, little work has applied
team models to human-robot teaming. In this work we compare the performance of
two models, logical predicates and large language models, at estimating user
situation awareness over varying visibility conditions. Our results indicate
that the methods are largely resilient to low-visibility conditions in our
domain, however opportunities exist to improve their overall performance.
