Title: Face processing emerges from object-trained convolutional neural networks
Authors: Zhenhua Zhao, Ji Chen, Zhicheng Lin, Haojiang Ying
ID: 2405.18800
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.18800

Abstract:
Whether face processing depends on unique, domain-specific neurocognitive
mechanisms or domain-general object recognition mechanisms has long been
debated. Directly testing these competing hypotheses in humans has proven
challenging due to extensive exposure to both faces and objects. Here, we
systematically test these hypotheses by capitalizing on recent progress in
convolutional neural networks (CNNs) that can be trained without face exposure
(i.e., pre-trained weights). Domain-general mechanism accounts posit that face
processing can emerge from a neural network without specialized pre-training on
faces. Consequently, we trained CNNs solely on objects and tested their ability
to recognize and represent faces as well as objects that look like faces (face
