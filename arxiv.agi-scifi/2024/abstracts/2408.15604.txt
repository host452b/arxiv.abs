Title: Traces of Quantum Gravity Effects at Late time Cosmological Dynamics via
Distance Measures
Authors: Maryam Roushan, Narges Rashidi, Kourosh Nozari
ID: 2408.15604
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.15604

Abstract:
Inspired by the entropy-area relation of black hole thermodynamics, we study the
thermodynamics of cosmological apparent horizon in a spatially flat Friedmann-
Robertson-Walker (FRW) universe in the framework of an Extended Uncertainty
Principle (EUP). The adopted EUP naturally admits a minimal measurable momentum
(equivalently a maximal measurable length), as an infrared cutoff in the theory.
We derive the modified Friedmann equations in this setup and explore some
predictions of these equations for the late time universe via distance measures.
We show that in this framework it is possible to realize the late time cosmic
speed-up and transition to the phantom phase of the equation of state parameter
of the effective cosmic fluid without recourse to any dark energy component or
modified gravity. Inspection of various distance measures in this framework
shows that an EUP with a negative deformation parameter suffices for the
interpretation of the late time asymptotically de Sitter universe with standard
non-relativistic matter.
