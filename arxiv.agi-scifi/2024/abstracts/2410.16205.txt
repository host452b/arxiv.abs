Title: Comparative analysis of 3D-CNN models, GARCH-ANN, and VAR models for
determining equity prices
Authors: Sydney Anuyah Mary Akinyemi, Chika Yinka-Banjo
ID: 2410.16205
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.16205

Abstract:
Financial models have increasingly become popular in recent times, and the focus
of researchers has been to find the perfect model which fits all circumstances;
however, this has not been thoroughly achieved, and as a result, many financial
models have been created. Artificial Intelligence modelling has increasingly
become more popular in the financial space as an answer to the weakness of the
advanced mathematical models studied in Economics. This paper introduces three
commonly used models and tests them on the S&P500 to give a strong projection as
to the future values of the prices. It then introduces various error metrics
like the root mean square error (RMSE) to ascertain the viability of the models.
The results show that a longer-term forecast indeed has more arduous
consequences as there is a veer between the actual and the forecasted readings.
The models can produce a strong growth estimate for businesses and policymakers
to plan more appropriately in the short term. Overall, the GARCH-LSTM model
produced the least RMSE which also shows that complex models tend to perform
better than simpler models
