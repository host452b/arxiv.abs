Title: EVINCE: Optimizing Multi-LLM Dialogues Using Conditional Statistics and
Information Theory
Authors: Edward Y. Chang
ID: 2408.14575
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.14575

Abstract:
EVINCE (Entropy and Variation IN Conditional Exchanges) is a novel framework for
optimizing multi-LLM dialogues using conditional statistics and information
theory. It addresses limitations in multi-agent debate (MAS) frameworks, where
multiple LLMs ``chat'' without behavior modulation or mutual information quality
assessment. Using dual entropy optimization to balance perspective diversity and
prior knowledge, $\EVINCE$ provides quantitative tools to dynamically regulate
LLM linguistic behaviors. When mutual information is low and both cross-entropy
and Wasserstein distance are high, EVINCE promotes contentious dialogues to
expose diverse perspectives and uncover inconsistencies. Conversely, as cross-
entropy decreases and mutual information stabilizes, it transitions discussions
into a conciliatory phase, encouraging compromise and acknowledgment of valid
points. Using information-theoretic metrics and optimizing mutual information,
$\EVINCE$ emerges as a structured and highly effective framework for multi-LLM
collaboration.
