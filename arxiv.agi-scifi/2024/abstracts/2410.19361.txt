Title: Engineering Trustworthy AI: A Developer Guide for Empirical Risk
Minimization
Authors: Diana Pfau, Alexander Jung
ID: 2410.19361
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.19361

Abstract:
AI systems increasingly shape critical decisions across personal and societal
domains. While empirical risk minimization (ERM) drives much of the AI success,
it typically prioritizes accuracy over trustworthiness, often resulting in
biases, opacity, and other adverse effects. This paper discusses how key
requirements for trustworthy AI can be translated into design choices for the
components of ERM. We hope to provide actionable guidance for building AI
systems that meet emerging standards for trustworthiness of AI.
