Title: Deep Learning Based Recalibration of SDSS and DESI BAO Alleviates Hubble
and Clustering Tensions
Authors: Rahul Shah, Purba Mukherjee, Soumadeep Saha, Utpal Garain, Supratik Pal
ID: 2412.14750
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.14750

Abstract:
Conventional calibration of Baryon Acoustic Oscillations (BAO) data relies on
estimation of the sound horizon at drag epoch $r_d$ from early universe
observations by assuming a cosmological model. We present a recalibration of two
independent BAO datasets, SDSS and DESI, by employing deep learning techniques
for model-independent estimation of $r_d$, and explore the impacts on $Λ$CDM
cosmological parameters. Significant reductions in both Hubble ($H_0$) and
clustering ($S_8$) tensions are observed for both the recalibrated datasets.
Moderate shifts in some other parameters hint towards further exploration of
such data-driven approaches.
