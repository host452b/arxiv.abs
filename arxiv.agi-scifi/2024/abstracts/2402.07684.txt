Title: Towards a Foundation Model for Brain Age Prediction using coVariance
Neural Networks
Authors: Saurabh Sihag, Gonzalo Mateos, Alejandro Ribeiro
ID: 2402.07684
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.07684

Abstract:
Brain age is the estimate of biological age derived from neuroimaging datasets
using machine learning algorithms. Increasing brain age with respect to
chronological age can reflect increased vulnerability to neurodegeneration and
cognitive decline. In this paper, we study NeuroVNN, based on coVariance neural
networks, as a paradigm for foundation model for the brain age prediction
application. NeuroVNN is pre-trained as a regression model on healthy population
to predict chronological age using cortical thickness features and fine-tuned to
estimate brain age in different neurological contexts. Importantly, NeuroVNN
adds anatomical interpretability to brain age and has a `scale-free'
characteristic that allows its transference to datasets curated according to any
arbitrary brain atlas. Our results demonstrate that NeuroVNN can extract
biologically plausible brain age estimates in different populations, as well as
transfer successfully to datasets of dimensionalities distinct from that for the
dataset used to train NeuroVNN.
