Title: Combinatorial Optimization and Machine Learning for Dynamic Inventory
Routing
Authors: Toni Greif, Louis Bouvier, Christoph M. Flath, Axel Parmentier, Sonja
U. K. Rohmer, Thibaut Vidal
ID: 2402.04463
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.04463

Abstract:
We introduce a combinatorial optimization-enriched machine learning pipeline and
a novel learning paradigm to solve inventory routing problems with stochastic
demand and dynamic inventory updates. After each inventory update, our approach
reduces replenishment and routing decisions to an optimal solution of a
capacitated prize-collecting traveling salesman problem for which well-
established algorithms exist. Discovering good prize parametrizations is non-
trivial; therefore, we have developed a machine learning approach. We evaluate
the performance of our pipeline in settings with steady-state and more complex
demand patterns. Compared to previous works, the policy generated by our
algorithm leads to significant cost savings, achieves lower inference time, and
can even leverage contextual information.
