Title: SpeechDPR: End-to-End Spoken Passage Retrieval for Open-Domain Spoken
Question Answering
Authors: Chyi-Jiunn Lin, Guan-Ting Lin, Yung-Sung Chuang, Wei-Lun Wu, Shang-Wen
Li, Abdelrahman Mohamed, Hung-yi Lee, Lin-shan Lee
ID: 2401.13463
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.13463

Abstract:
Spoken Question Answering (SQA) is essential for machines to reply to user's
question by finding the answer span within a given spoken passage. SQA has been
previously achieved without ASR to avoid recognition errors and Out-of-
Vocabulary (OOV) problems. However, the real-world problem of Open-domain SQA
(openSQA), in which the machine needs to first retrieve passages that possibly
contain the answer from a spoken archive in addition, was never considered. This
paper proposes the first known end-to-end framework, Speech Dense Passage
Retriever (SpeechDPR), for the retrieval component of the openSQA problem.
SpeechDPR learns a sentence-level semantic representation by distilling
knowledge from the cascading model of unsupervised ASR (UASR) and text dense
retriever (TDR). No manually transcribed speech data is needed. Initial
experiments showed performance comparable to the cascading model of UASR and
TDR, and significantly better when UASR was poor, verifying this approach is
more robust to speech recognition errors.
