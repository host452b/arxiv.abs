Title: Black Holes, Warp Drives, and Energy Conditions
Authors: Remo Garattini, Kirill Zatrimaylov
ID: 2408.04495
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.04495

Abstract:
Following the work of H. Ellis, we study warp drives in the gravitational field
of a Schwarzschild black hole. We find that as long as the warp drive crosses
the black hole horizon at a subluminal speed, the horizon would be effectively
absent inside the warp bubble. Moreover, we discover that the black hole's
gravitational field can alleviate the violations of the weak energy condition
(WEC) and the null energy condition (NEC) and therefore decrease the amount of
negative energy required to sustain a warp drive, which may be instrumental for
creating microscopic warp drives in lab experiments. We also consider the
thermodynamics of a warp bubble interacting with a black hole and point out some
paradoxes that may indicate a gap in our understanding of them from the
thermodynamic point of view.
