Title: Machine Learning and Quantum Intelligence for Health Data Scenarios
Authors: Sanjeev Naguleswaran
ID: 2410.21339
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.21339

Abstract:
The advent of quantum computing has opened new possibilities in data science,
offering unique capabilities for addressing complex, data-intensive problems.
Traditional machine learning algorithms often face challenges in high-
dimensional or limited-quality datasets, which are common in healthcare. Quantum
Machine Learning leverages quantum properties, such as superposition and
entanglement, to enhance pattern recognition and classification, potentially
surpassing classical approaches. This paper explores QML's application in
healthcare, focusing on quantum kernel methods and hybrid quantum-classical
networks for heart disease prediction and COVID-19 detection, assessing their
feasibility and performance.
