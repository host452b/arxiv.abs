Title: An AI System Evaluation Framework for Advancing AI Safety: Terminology,
Taxonomy, Lifecycle Mapping
Authors: Boming Xia, Qinghua Lu, Liming Zhu, Zhenchang Xing
ID: 2404.05388
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.05388

Abstract:
The advent of advanced AI underscores the urgent need for comprehensive safety
evaluations, necessitating collaboration across communities (i.e., AI, software
engineering, and governance). However, divergent practices and terminologies
across these communities, combined with the complexity of AI systems-of which
models are only a part-and environmental affordances (e.g., access to tools),
obstruct effective communication and comprehensive evaluation. This paper
proposes a framework for AI system evaluation comprising three components: 1)
harmonised terminology to facilitate communication across communities involved
in AI safety evaluation; 2) a taxonomy identifying essential elements for AI
system evaluation; 3) a mapping between AI lifecycle, stakeholders, and
requisite evaluations for accountable AI supply chain. This framework catalyses
a deeper discourse on AI system evaluation beyond model-centric approaches.
