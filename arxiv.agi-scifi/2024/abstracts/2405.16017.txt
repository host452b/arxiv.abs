Title: On the measurements in Quantum Gravity
Authors: Juanca Carrasco-Martinez
ID: 2405.16017
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.16017

Abstract:
In this essay, we argue that certain aspects of the measurement require revision
in Quantum Gravity. Using entropic arguments, we propose that the number of
measurement outcomes and the accuracy (or the range) of the measurement are
limited by the entropy of the black hole associated with the observer scale.
This also implies the necessity of modifying the algebra of commutation
relationships to ensure a finite representation of observables, changing the
Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle in this manner.
