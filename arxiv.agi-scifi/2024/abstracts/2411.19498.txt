Title: Protecting Multiple Types of Privacy Simultaneously in EEG-based Brain-
Computer Interfaces
Authors: Lubin Meng, Xue Jiang, Tianwang Jia, Dongrui Wu
ID: 2411.19498
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.19498

Abstract:
A brain-computer interface (BCI) enables direct communication between the brain
and an external device. Electroencephalogram (EEG) is the preferred input signal
in non-invasive BCIs, due to its convenience and low cost. EEG-based BCIs have
been successfully used in many applications, such as neurological
rehabilitation, text input, games, and so on. However, EEG signals inherently
carry rich personal information, necessitating privacy protection. This paper
demonstrates that multiple types of private information (user identity, gender,
and BCI-experience) can be easily inferred from EEG data, imposing a serious
privacy threat to BCIs. To address this issue, we design perturbations to
convert the original EEG data into privacy-protected EEG data, which conceal the
private information while maintaining the primary BCI task performance.
Experimental results demonstrated that the privacy-protected EEG data can
significantly reduce the classification accuracy of user identity, gender and
BCI-experience, but almost do not affect at all the classification accuracy of
the primary BCI task, enabling user privacy protection in EEG-based BCIs.
