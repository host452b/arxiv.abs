Title: Quantum Gravity Effective Action Provides Entropy of The Universe
Authors: Ken-ji Hamada
ID: 2401.09672
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.09672

Abstract:
The effective action in renormalizable quantum theory of gravity provides
entropy because the total Hamiltonian vanishes. Since it is a renormalization
group invariant that is constant in the process of cosmic evolution, we can show
conservation of entropy, which is an ansatz in the standard cosmology. Here, we
study renormalizable quantum gravity that exhibits conformal dominance at high
energy beyond the Planck scale. The current entropy of the universe is derived
by calculating the effective action under the scenario of quantum gravity
inflation caused by its dynamics. We then argue that ghost modes must be
unphysical but are necessary for the Hamiltonian to vanish and for entropy to
exist in gravitational systems.
