Title: The Manhattan Trap: Why a Race to Artificial Superintelligence is Self-
Defeating
Authors: Corin Katzke, Gideon Futerman
ID: 2501.14749
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.14749

Abstract:
This paper examines the strategic dynamics of international competition to
develop Artificial Superintelligence (ASI). We argue that the same assumptions
that might motivate the US to race to develop ASI also imply that such a race is
extremely dangerous. These assumptions--that ASI would provide a decisive
military advantage and that states are rational actors prioritizing survival--
imply that a race would heighten three critical risks: great power conflict,
loss of control of ASI systems, and the undermining of liberal democracy. Our
analysis shows that ASI presents a trust dilemma rather than a prisoners
dilemma, suggesting that international cooperation to control ASI development is
both preferable and strategically sound. We conclude that cooperation is
achievable.
