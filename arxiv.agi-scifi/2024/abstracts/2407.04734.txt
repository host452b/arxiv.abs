Title: Neuro-Symbolic Fusion of Wi-Fi Sensing Data for Passive Radar with Inter-
Modal Knowledge Transfer
Authors: Marco Cominelli, Francesco Gringoli, Lance M. Kaplan, Mani B.
Srivastava, Trevor Bihl, Erik P. Blasch, Nandini Iyer, Federico Cerutti
ID: 2407.04734
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.04734

Abstract:
Wi-Fi devices, akin to passive radars, can discern human activities within
indoor settings due to the human body's interaction with electromagnetic
signals. Current Wi-Fi sensing applications predominantly employ data-driven
learning techniques to associate the fluctuations in the physical properties of
the communication channel with the human activity causing them. However, these
techniques often lack the desired flexibility and transparency. This paper
introduces DeepProbHAR, a neuro-symbolic architecture for Wi-Fi sensing,
providing initial evidence that Wi-Fi signals can differentiate between simple
movements, such as leg or arm movements, which are integral to human activities
like running or walking. The neuro-symbolic approach affords gathering such
evidence without needing additional specialised data collection or labelling.
The training of DeepProbHAR is facilitated by declarative domain knowledge
obtained from a camera feed and by fusing signals from various antennas of the
Wi-Fi receivers. DeepProbHAR achieves results comparable to the state-of-the-art
in human activity recognition. Moreover, as a by-product of the learning
process, DeepProbHAR generates specialised classifiers for simple movements that
match the accuracy of models trained on finely labelled datasets, which would be
particularly costly.
