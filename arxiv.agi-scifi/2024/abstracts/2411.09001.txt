Title: Virtual teaching assistant for undergraduate students using natural
language processing & deep learning
Authors: Sadman Jashim Sakib, Baktiar Kabir Joy, Zahin Rydha, Md. Nuruzzaman,
Annajiat Alim Rasel
ID: 2411.09001
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.09001

Abstract:
Online education's popularity has been continuously increasing over the past few
years. Many universities were forced to switch to online education as a result
of COVID-19. In many cases, even after more than two years of online
instruction, colleges were unable to resume their traditional classroom
programs. A growing number of institutions are considering blended learning with
some parts in-person and the rest of the learning taking place online.
Nevertheless, many online education systems are inefficient, and this results in
a poor rate of student retention. In this paper, we are offering a primary
dataset, the initial implementation of a virtual teaching assistant named VTA-
bot, and its system architecture. Our primary implementation of the suggested
system consists of a chatbot that can be queried about the content and topics of
the fundamental python programming language course. Students in their first year
of university will be benefited from this strategy, which aims to increase
student participation and involvement in online education.
