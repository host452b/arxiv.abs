Title: AI-as-exploration: Navigating intelligence space
Authors: Dimitri Coelho Mollo
ID: 2401.07964
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.07964

Abstract:
Artificial Intelligence is a field that lives many lives, and the term has come
to encompass a motley collection of scientific and commercial endeavours. In
this paper, I articulate the contours of a rather neglected but central
scientific role that AI has to play, which I dub `AI-as-exploration'.The basic
thrust of AI-as-exploration is that of creating and studying systems that can
reveal candidate building blocks of intelligence that may differ from the forms
of human and animal intelligence we are familiar with. In other words, I suggest
that AI is one of the best tools we have for exploring intelligence space,
namely the space of possible intelligent systems. I illustrate the value of AI-
as-exploration by focusing on a specific case study, i.e., recent work on the
capacity to combine novel and invented concepts in humans and Large Language
Models. I show that the latter, despite showing human-level accuracy in such a
task, probably solve it in ways radically different, but no less relevant to
intelligence research, to those hypothesised for humans.
