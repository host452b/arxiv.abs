Title: Time Matters: Scaling Laws for Any Budget
Authors: Itay Inbar, Luke Sernau
ID: 2406.18922
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.18922

Abstract:
A primary cost driver for training large models is wall-clock training time. We
show that popular time estimates based on FLOPs are poor estimates, and
construct a more accurate proxy based on memory copies. This allows us to
accurately estimate the training speed of a transformer model from its
hyperparameters. Combined with a scaling law curve like Chinchilla, this allows
us to accurately predict the final loss of a model from a simple equation. We
show that this expression is accurate across a wide range of model
hyperparameter values, enabling us to analytically make architectural decisions
and train models more efficiently. Crucially, this analysis predicts that in
contrast to existing literature, models should be wider rather than deeper, as
the benefits of speed outweigh the benefits of depth.
