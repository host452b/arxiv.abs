Title: QKD as a Quantum Machine Learning task
Authors: T. Decker, M. Gallezot, S. F. Kerstan, A. Paesano, A. Ginter, W.
Wormsbecher
ID: 2410.01904
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.01904

Abstract:
We propose considering Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) protocols as a use case
for Quantum Machine Learning (QML) algorithms. We define and investigate the QML
task of optimizing eavesdropping attacks on the quantum circuit implementation
of the BB84 protocol. QKD protocols are well understood and solid security
proofs exist enabling an easy evaluation of the QML model performance. The power
of easy-to-implement QML techniques is shown by finding the explicit circuit for
optimal individual attacks in a noise-free setting. For the noisy setting we
find, to the best of our knowledge, a new cloning algorithm, which can
outperform known cloning methods. Finally, we present a QML construction of a
collective attack by using classical information from QKD post-processing within
the QML algorithm.
