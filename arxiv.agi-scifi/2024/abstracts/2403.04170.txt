Title: The Power of Lorentz Quantum Computer
Authors: Qi Zhang, Biao Wu
ID: 2403.04170
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.04170

Abstract:
We demonstrate the superior capabilities of the recently proposed Lorentz
quantum computer (LQC) compared to conventional quantum computers. We introduce
an associated computational complexity class termed bounded-error Lorentz
quantum polynomial-time (BLQP), demonstrating its equivalence to the complexity
class ${\text P}^{\sharp \text{P}}$. We present LQC algorithms that efficiently
solve the problem of maximum independent set, PP (probabilistic polynomial-
time), and consequently ${\text P}^{\sharp \text{P}}$, all within polynomial
time. Additionally, we show that the quantum computing with postselection
proposed by Aaronson can be efficiently simulated by LQC, but not vice versa.
