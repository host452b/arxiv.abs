Title: Cost-informed dimensionality reduction for structural digital twin
technologies
Authors: Aidan J. Hughes, Keith Worden, Nikolaos Dervilis, Timothy J. Rogers
ID: 2409.11236
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.11236

Abstract:
Classification models are a key component of structural digital twin
technologies used for supporting asset management decision-making. An important
consideration when developing classification models is the dimensionality of the
input, or feature space, used. If the dimensionality is too high, then the
`curse of dimensionality' may rear its ugly head; manifesting as reduced
predictive performance. To mitigate such effects, practitioners can employ
dimensionality reduction techniques. The current paper formulates a decision-
theoretic approach to dimensionality reduction for structural asset management.
In this approach, the aim is to keep incurred misclassification costs to a
minimum, as the dimensionality is reduced and discriminatory information may be
lost. This formulation is constructed as an eigenvalue problem, with
separabilities between classes weighted according to the cost of misclassifying
them when considered in the context of a decision process. The approach is
demonstrated using a synthetic case study.
