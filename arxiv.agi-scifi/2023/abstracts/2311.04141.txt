Title: Benchmarking a Neutral-Atom Quantum Computer
Authors: N. Wagner, C. Poole, T. M. Graham, M. Saffman
ID: 2311.04141
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.04141

Abstract:
In this study, we simulated the algorithmic performance of a small neutral atom
quantum computer and compared its performance when operating with all-to-all
versus nearest-neighbor connectivity. This comparison was made using a suite of
algorithmic benchmarks developed by the Quantum Economic Development Consortium.
Circuits were simulated with a noise model consistent with experimental data
from Nature 604, 457 (2022). We find that all-to-all connectivity improves
simulated circuit fidelity by $10\%-15\%$, compared to nearest-neighbor
connectivity.
