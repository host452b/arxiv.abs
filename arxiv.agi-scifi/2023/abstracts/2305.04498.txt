Title: Leveraging Deep Learning and Digital Twins to Improve Energy Performance
of Buildings
Authors: Zhongjun Ni, Chi Zhang, Magnus Karlsson, Shaofang Gong
ID: 2305.04498
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.04498

Abstract:
Digital transformation in buildings accumulates massive operational data, which
calls for smart solutions to utilize these data to improve energy performance.
This study has proposed a solution, namely Deep Energy Twin, for integrating
deep learning and digital twins to better understand building energy use and
identify the potential for improving energy efficiency. Ontology was adopted to
create parametric digital twins to provide consistency of data format across
different systems in a building. Based on created digital twins and collected
data, deep learning methods were used for performing data analytics to identify
patterns and provide insights for energy optimization. As a demonstration, a
case study was conducted in a public historic building in Norrköping, Sweden, to
compare the performance of state-of-the-art deep learning architectures in
building energy forecasting.
