Title: Hydrophobically gated memristive nanopores for neuromorphic applications
Authors: Gonçalo Paulo, Ke Sun, Giovanni di Muccio, Alberto Gubbiotti, Blasco
Morozzo della Rocca, Jia Geng, Giovanni Maglia, Mauro Chinappi, Alberto
Giacomello
ID: 2306.12187
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.12187

Abstract:
Brain-inspired computing has the potential to revolutionise the current von
Neumann architecture, advancing machine learning applications. Signal
transmission in the brain relies on voltage-gated ion channels, which exhibit
the electrical behaviour of memristors, resistors with memory. State-of-the-art
technologies currently employ semiconductor-based neuromorphic approaches, which
have already demonstrated their efficacy in machine learning systems. However,
these approaches still cannot match performance achieved by biological neurons
in terms of energy efficiency and size. In this study, we utilise molecular
dynamics simulations, continuum models, and electrophysiological experiments to
propose and realise a bioinspired hydrophobically gated memristive nanopore. Our
findings indicate that hydrophobic gating enables memory through an
electrowetting mechanism, and we establish simple design rules accordingly.
Through the engineering of a biological nanopore, we successfully replicate the
characteristic hysteresis cycles of a memristor \tr{and construct a synaptic
device capable of learning and forgetting}. This advancement offers a promising
pathway for the realization of nanoscale, cost- and energy-effective, and
adaptable bioinspired memristors.
