Title: Visual tracking brain computer interface
Authors: Changxing Huang, Nanlin Shi, Yining Miao, Xiaogang Chen, Yijun Wang,
Xiaorong Gao
ID: 2311.12592
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.12592

Abstract:
Brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) offer a way to interact with computers without
relying on physical movements. Non-invasive electroencephalography (EEG)-based
visual BCIs, known for efficient speed and calibration ease, face limitations in
continuous tasks due to discrete stimulus design and decoding methods. To
achieve continuous control, we implemented a novel spatial encoding stimulus
paradigm and devised a corresponding projection method to enable continuous
modulation of decoded velocity. Subsequently, we conducted experiments involving
17 participants and achieved Fitt's ITR of 0.55 bps for the fixed tracking task
and 0.37 bps for the random tracking task. The proposed BCI with a high Fitt's
ITR was then integrated into two applications, including painting and gaming. In
conclusion, this study proposed a visual BCI-based control method to go beyond
discrete commands, allowing natural continuous control based on neural activity.
