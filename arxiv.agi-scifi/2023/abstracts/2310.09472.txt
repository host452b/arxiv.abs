Title: An Information Theory Approach to Identifying Signs of Life on Transiting
Planets
Authors: Sara Vannah, Marcelo Gleiser, Lisa Kaltenegger
ID: 2310.09472
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.09472

Abstract:
Can information theory provide insights into whether exoplanets are habitable?
Here we apply information theory to a range of simulated exoplanet transmission
spectra as a diagnostic tool to search for potential signatures of life on
Earth-analog planets. We test the algorithms on three epochs of evolution for
Earth-like planets orbiting a range of host stars. The James Webb Space
Telescope and upcoming ground- and space-based missions promise to achieve
sufficient high-resolution data that information theory can be applied to assess
habitability. This approach provides a framework and a tool for observers to
assess whether an exoplanet shows signs of habitability.
