Title: Relative Entropy from Coherent States in Black Hole Thermodynamics and
Cosmology
Authors: Edoardo D'Angelo
ID: 2309.01548
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.01548

Abstract:
The aim of this work is to study the role of relative entropy in the
thermodynamics of black holes and cosmological horizons. We adapt some recent
results on the relative entropy of coherent excitations of the vacuum, to find
the variation of generalised entropy of static and dynamical black holes and for
cosmological horizons.
  We review the argument for static black holes by Hollands and Ishibashi (2019)
with simple modifications. We link the variation of relative entropy to the
growth of the horizon using a conservation law for the stress-energy tensor, and
we recover the known results. We then study the application of the same
framework to the case of dynamical horizons. We study in detail the case of
Vaidya spacetime, and we find that a notion of black hole entropy naturally
emerges, equals to one-fourth of the area of the dynamical horizon. In the case
of dynamical black holes we find an additional term, which is not present in the
static case, and that represents the work done on the black hole. We finally
show in a simple case that it is possible to follow the same scheme to assign an
entropy to the horizons emerging in cosmological scenarios.
