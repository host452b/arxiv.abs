Title: Black Holes and Warp Drive
Authors: Remo Garattini, Kirill Zatrimaylov
ID: 2311.06757
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.06757

Abstract:
We study the generalizations of the original Alcubierre warp drive metric to the
case of curved spacetime background. We find that the presence of a horizon is
essential when one moves from spherical coordinates to Cartesian coordinates in
order to avoid additional singularities. For the specific case of Schwarzschild
black hole, the horizon would be effectively absent for the observers inside the
warp bubble, implying that warp drives may provide a safe route to cross
horizons. Moreover, we discover that the black hole's gravitational field can
decrease the amount of negative energy required to sustain a warp drive, which
may be instrumental for creating microscopic warp drives in lab experiments. A
BEC model is also introduced to propose possible test in the Analogue Gravity
framework.
