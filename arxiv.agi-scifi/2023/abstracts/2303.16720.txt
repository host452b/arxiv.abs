Title: Convolutional neural network search for long-duration transient
gravitational waves from glitching pulsars
Authors: Luana M. Modafferi, Rodrigo Tenorio, David Keitel
ID: 2303.16720
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.16720

Abstract:
Machine learning can be a powerful tool to discover new signal types in
astronomical data. We here apply it to search for long-duration transient
gravitational waves triggered by pulsar glitches, which could yield physical
insight into the mostly unknown depths of the pulsar. Current methods to search
for such signals rely on matched filtering and a brute-force grid search over
possible signal durations, which is sensitive but can become very
computationally expensive. We develop a method to search for post-glitch signals
on combining matched filtering with convolutional neural networks, which reaches
similar sensitivities to the standard method at false-alarm probabilities
relevant for practical searches, while being significantly faster. We specialize
to the Vela glitch during the LIGO-Virgo O2 run, and set upper limits on the
gravitational-wave strain amplitude from the data of the two LIGO detectors for
both constant-amplitude and exponentially decaying signals.
