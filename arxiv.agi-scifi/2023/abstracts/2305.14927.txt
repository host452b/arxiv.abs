Title: Scalable wavelength-multiplexing photonic reservoir computing
Authors: Rui-Qian Li, Yi-Wei Shen, Bao-De Lin, Jingyi Yu, Xuming He, Cheng Wang
ID: 2305.14927
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.14927

Abstract:
Photonic reservoir computing (PRC) is a special hardware recurrent neural
network, which is featured with fast training speed and low training cost. This
work shows a wavelength-multiplexing PRC architecture, taking advantage of the
numerous longitudinal modes in a Fabry-Perot semiconductor laser. These modes
construct connected physical neurons in parallel, while an optical feedback loop
provides interactive virtual neurons in series. We experimentally demonstrate a
four-channel wavelength-multiplexing PRC, which runs four times faster than the
single-channel case. It is proved that the multiplexing PRC exhibits superior
performance on the task of signal equalization in an optical fiber communication
link. Particularly, this scheme is highly scalable owing to the rich mode
resources in Fabry-Perot lasers.
