Title: The Vacuum Energy Problem in Quantum Gravity and the Masses of Elementary
Particles
Authors: Djordje Minic
ID: 2305.12593
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.12593

Abstract:
This talk summarizes a new understanding of the cosmological constant problem,
which essentially relies on a phase-space-like computation of the vacuum energy,
both in the realm of quantum field theory coupled to gravity, and in the realm
of a consistent formulation of a quantum theory of gravity and matter, such as
string theory, combined with central properties of gravitational entropy as
captured by the Bekenstein bound. This new understanding of the vacuum in a
quantum theory of gravity and matter sheds light on the Higgs mass as well as
the masses of quarks and leptons.
