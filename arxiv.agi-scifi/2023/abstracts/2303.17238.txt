Title: Non-commutative coordinates from quantum gravity
Authors: Markus B. Fröb, Albert Much, Kyriakos Papadopoulos
ID: 2303.17238
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.17238

Abstract:
Local observables in (perturbative) quantum gravity are notoriously hard to
define, since the gauge symmetry of gravity -- diffeomorphisms -- moves points
on the manifold. In particular, this is a problem for backgrounds of high
symmetry such as Minkowski space or de Sitter spacetime which describes the
early inflationary phase of our universe. Only recently this obstacle has been
overcome, and a field-dependent coordinate system has been constructed to all
orders in perturbation theory, in which observables are fully gauge-invariant.
We show that these field-dependent coordinates are non-commutative, and compute
their commutator to second order in the Planck length. This provides the first
systematic derivation of non-commutativity that arises due to quantum gravity
effects.
