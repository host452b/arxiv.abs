Title: Uniform Tests and Algorithmic Thermodynamic Entropy
Authors: Samuel Epstein
ID: 2303.05619
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.05619

Abstract:
We prove that given a computable metric space and two computable measures, the
set of points that have high universal uniform test scores with respect to the
first measure will have a lower bound with respect to the second measure. This
result is transferred to thermodynamics, showing that algorithmic thermodynamic
entropy must oscillate in the presence of dynamics. Another application is that
outliers will become emergent in computable dynamics of computable metric
spaces.
