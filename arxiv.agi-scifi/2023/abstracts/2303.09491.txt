Title: Challenges and Opportunities in Quantum Machine Learning
Authors: M. Cerezo, Guillaume Verdon, Hsin-Yuan Huang, Lukasz Cincio, Patrick J.
Coles
ID: 2303.09491
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.09491

Abstract:
At the intersection of machine learning and quantum computing, Quantum Machine
Learning (QML) has the potential of accelerating data analysis, especially for
quantum data, with applications for quantum materials, biochemistry, and high-
energy physics. Nevertheless, challenges remain regarding the trainability of
QML models. Here we review current methods and applications for QML. We
highlight differences between quantum and classical machine learning, with a
focus on quantum neural networks and quantum deep learning. Finally, we discuss
opportunities for quantum advantage with QML.
