Title: Learnable Digital Twin for Efficient Wireless Network Evaluation
Authors: Boning Li, Timofey Efimov, Abhishek Kumar, Jose Cortes, Gunjan Verma,
Ananthram Swami, Santiago Segarra
ID: 2306.06574
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.06574

Abstract:
Network digital twins (NDTs) facilitate the estimation of key performance
indicators (KPIs) before physically implementing a network, thereby enabling
efficient optimization of the network configuration. In this paper, we propose a
learning-based NDT for network simulators. The proposed method offers a holistic
representation of information flow in a wireless network by integrating node,
edge, and path embeddings. Through this approach, the model is trained to map
the network configuration to KPIs in a single forward pass. Hence, it offers a
more efficient alternative to traditional simulation-based methods, thus
allowing for rapid experimentation and optimization. Our proposed method has
been extensively tested through comprehensive experimentation in various
scenarios, including wired and wireless networks. Results show that it
outperforms baseline learning models in terms of accuracy and robustness.
Moreover, our approach achieves comparable performance to simulators but with
significantly higher computational efficiency.
