Title: Bilevel Inverse Problems in Neuromorphic Imaging
Authors: Harbir Antil, David Sayre
ID: 2302.02052
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.02052

Abstract:
Event or Neuromorphic cameras are novel biologically inspired sensors that
record data based on the change in light intensity at each pixel asynchronously.
They have a temporal resolution of microseconds. This is useful for scenes with
fast moving objects that can cause motion blur in traditional cameras, which
record the average light intensity over an exposure time for each pixel
synchronously. This paper presents a bilevel inverse problem framework for
neuromorphic imaging. Existence of solution to the inverse problem is
established. Second order sufficient conditions are derived under special
situations for this nonconvex problem. A second order Newton type solver is
derived to solve the problem. The efficacy of the approach is shown on several
examples.
