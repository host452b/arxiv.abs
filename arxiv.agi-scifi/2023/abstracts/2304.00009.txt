Title: The challenge of redundancy on multi-agent value factorisation
Authors: Siddarth Singh, Benjamin Rosman
ID: 2304.00009
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.00009

Abstract:
In the field of cooperative multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL), the
standard paradigm is the use of centralised training and decentralised execution
where a central critic conditions the policies of the cooperative agents based
on a central state. It has been shown, that in cases with large numbers of
redundant agents these methods become less effective. In a more general case,
there is likely to be a larger number of agents in an environment than is
required to solve the task. These redundant agents reduce performance by
enlarging the dimensionality of both the state space and and increasing the size
of the joint policy used to solve the environment. We propose leveraging
layerwise relevance propagation (LRP) to instead separate the learning of the
joint value function and generation of local reward signals and create a new
MARL algorithm: relevance decomposition network (RDN). We find that although the
performance of both baselines VDN and Qmix degrades with the number of redundant
agents, RDN is unaffected.
