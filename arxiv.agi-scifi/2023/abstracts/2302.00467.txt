Title: Review on Quantum Computing for Lattice Field Theory
Authors: Lena Funcke, Tobias Hartung, Karl Jansen, Stefan Kühn
ID: 2302.00467
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.00467

Abstract:
In these proceedings, we review recent advances in applying quantum computing to
lattice field theory. Quantum computing offers the prospect to simulate lattice
field theories in parameter regimes that are largely inaccessible with the
conventional Monte Carlo approach, such as the sign-problem afflicted regimes of
finite baryon density, topological terms, and out-of-equilibrium dynamics. First
proof-of-concept quantum computations of lattice gauge theories in (1+1)
dimensions have been accomplished, and first resource-efficient quantum
algorithms for lattice gauge theories in (1+1) and (2+1) dimensions have been
developed. The path towards quantum computations of (3+1)-dimensional lattice
gauge theories, including Lattice QCD, requires many incremental steps of
improving both quantum hardware and quantum algorithms. After reviewing these
requirements and recent advances, we discuss the main challenges and future
directions.
