Title: A Review of the Applications of Quantum Machine Learning in Optical
Communication Systems
Authors: Ark Modi, Alonso Viladomat Jasso, Roberto Ferrara, Christian Deppe,
Janis Noetzel, Fred Fung, Maximilian Schaedler
ID: 2309.05205
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.05205

Abstract:
In the context of optical signal processing, quantum and quantum-inspired
machine learning algorithms have massive potential for deployment. One of the
applications is in error correction protocols for the received noisy signals. In
some scenarios, non-linear and unknown errors can lead to noise that bypasses
linear error correction protocols that optical receivers generally implement. In
those cases, machine learning techniques are used to recover the transmitted
signal from the received signal through various estimation procedures. Since
quantum machine learning algorithms promise advantage over classical algorithms,
we expect that optical signal processing can benefit from these advantages. In
this review, we survey several proposed quantum and quantum-inspired machine
learning algorithms and their applicability with current technology to optical
signal processing.
