Title: Shaping photons: quantum computation with bosonic cQED
Authors: Adrian Copetudo, Clara Yun Fontaine, Fernando Valadares, Yvonne Y. Gao
ID: 2311.03846
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.03846

Abstract:
With its rich dynamics, the quantum harmonic oscillator is an innate platform
for understanding real-world quantum systems and could even excel as the heart
of a quantum computer. A particularly promising and rapidly advancing platform
that harnesses quantum harmonic oscillators for information processing is the
bosonic circuit quantum electrodynamics (cQED) system. In this article, we
provide perspectives on the progress, challenges, and future directions in
building a bosonic cQED quantum computer. We describe the main hardware building
blocks and how they facilitate quantum error correction, metrology, and
simulation. We conclude with our views of the key challenges that lie on the
horizon, as well as scientific and cultural strategies for overcoming them and
building a practical quantum computer with bosonic cQED hardware.
