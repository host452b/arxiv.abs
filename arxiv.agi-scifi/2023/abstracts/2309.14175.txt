Title: The Influence of Cognitive Biases on Architectural Technical Debt
Authors: Klara Borowa, Andrzej Zalewski, Szymon Kijas
ID: 2309.14175
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.14175

Abstract:
Cognitive biases exert a significant influence on human thinking and decision-
making. In order to identify how they influence the occurrence of architectural
technical debt, a series of semi-structured interviews with software architects
was performed. The results show which classes of architectural technical debt
originate from cognitive biases, and reveal the antecedents of technical debt
items (classes) through biases. This way, we analysed how and when cognitive
biases lead to the creation of technical debt. We also identified a set of
debiasing techniques that can be used in order to prevent the negative influence
of cognitive biases. The observations of the role of organisational culture in
the avoidance of inadvertent technical debt throw a new light on that issue.
