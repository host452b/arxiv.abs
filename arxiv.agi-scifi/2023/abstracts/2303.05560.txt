Title: Applications of Machine Learning to Detecting Fast Neutrino Flavor
Instabilities in Core-Collapse Supernova and Neutron Star Merger Models
Authors: Sajad Abbar
ID: 2303.05560
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.05560

Abstract:
Neutrinos propagating in a dense neutrino gas, such as those expected in core-
collapse supernovae (CCSNe) and neutron star mergers (NSMs), can experience fast
flavor conversions on relatively short scales. This can happen if the neutrino
electron lepton number ($ν$ELN) angular distribution crosses zero in a certain
direction. Despite this, most of the state-of-the-art CCSN and NSM simulations
do not provide such detailed angular information and instead, supply only a few
moments of the neutrino angular distributions. In this study we employ, for the
\emph{first} time, a machine learning (ML) approach to this problem and show
that it can be extremely successful in detecting $ν$ELN crossings on the basis
of its zeroth and first moments. We observe that an accuracy of $\sim95\%$ can
be achieved by the ML algorithms, which almost corresponds to the Bayes error
rate of our problem. Considering its remarkable efficiency and agility, the ML
approach provides one with an unprecedented opportunity to evaluate the
occurrence of FFCs in CCSN and NSM simulations \emph{on the fly}. We also
provide our ML methodologies on GitHub.
