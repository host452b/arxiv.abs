Title: Multi-Epoch Machine Learning 2: Identifying physical drivers of galaxy
properties in simulations
Authors: Robert McGibbon, Sadegh Khochfar
ID: 2306.07728
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.07728

Abstract:
Using a novel machine learning method, we investigate the buildup of galaxy
properties in different simulations, and in various environments within a single
simulation. The aim of this work is to show the power of this approach at
identifying the physical drivers of galaxy properties within simulations. We
compare how the stellar mass is dependent on the value of other galaxy and halo
properties at different points in time by examining the feature importance
values of a machine learning model. By training the model on IllustrisTNG we
show that stars are produced at earlier times in higher density regions of the
universe than they are in low density regions. We also apply the technique to
the Illustris, EAGLE, and CAMELS simulations. We find that stellar mass is built
up in a similar way in EAGLE and IllustrisTNG, but significantly differently in
the original Illustris, suggesting that subgrid model physics is more important
than the choice of hydrodynamics method. These differences are driven by the
efficiency of supernova feedback. Applying principal component analysis to the
CAMELS simulations allows us to identify a component associated with the
importance of a halo's gravitational potential and another component
representing the time at which galaxies form. We discover that the speed of
galactic winds is a more critical subgrid parameter than the total energy per
unit star formation. Finally we find that the Simba black hole feedback model
has a larger effect on galaxy formation than the IllustrisTNG black hole
feedback model.
