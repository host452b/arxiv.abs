Title: Machine-learned Adversarial Attacks against Fault Prediction Systems in
Smart Electrical Grids
Authors: Carmelo Ardito, Yashar Deldjoo, Tommaso Di Noia, Eugenio Di Sciascio,
Fatemeh Nazary, Giovanni Servedio
ID: 2303.18136
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.18136

Abstract:
In smart electrical grids, fault detection tasks may have a high impact on
society due to their economic and critical implications. In the recent years,
numerous smart grid applications, such as defect detection and load forecasting,
have embraced data-driven methodologies. The purpose of this study is to
investigate the challenges associated with the security of machine learning (ML)
applications in the smart grid scenario. Indeed, the robustness and security of
these data-driven algorithms have not been extensively studied in relation to
all power grid applications. We demonstrate first that the deep neural network
method used in the smart grid is susceptible to adversarial perturbation. Then,
we highlight how studies on fault localization and type classification
illustrate the weaknesses of present ML algorithms in smart grids to various
adversarial attacks
