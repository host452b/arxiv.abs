Title: Safe Multimodal Communication in Human-Robot Collaboration
Authors: Davide Ferrari, Andrea Pupa, Alberto Signoretti, Cristian Secchi
ID: 2308.03690
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.03690

Abstract:
The new industrial settings are characterized by the presence of human and
robots that work in close proximity, cooperating in performing the required job.
Such a collaboration, however, requires to pay attention to many aspects.
Firstly, it is crucial to enable a communication between this two actors that is
natural and efficient. Secondly, the robot behavior must always be compliant
with the safety regulations, ensuring always a safe collaboration. In this
paper, we propose a framework that enables multi-channel communication between
humans and robots by leveraging multimodal fusion of voice and gesture commands
while always respecting safety regulations. The framework is validated through a
comparative experiment, demonstrating that, thanks to multimodal communication,
the robot can extract valuable information for performing the required task and
additionally, with the safety layer, the robot can scale its speed to ensure the
operator's safety.
