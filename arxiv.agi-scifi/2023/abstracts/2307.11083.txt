Title: Quantum Logspace Computations are Verifiable
Authors: Uma Girish, Ran Raz, Wei Zhan
ID: 2307.11083
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.11083

Abstract:
In this note, we observe that quantum logspace computations are verifiable by
classical logspace algorithms, with unconditional security. More precisely,
every language in BQL has an (information-theoretically secure) streaming proof
with a quantum logspace prover and a classical logspace verifier. The prover
provides a polynomial-length proof that is streamed to the verifier. The
verifier has a read-once one-way access to that proof and is able to verify that
the computation was performed correctly. That is, if the input is in the
language and the prover is honest, the verifier accepts with high probability,
and, if the input is not in the language, the verifier rejects with high
probability even if the prover is adversarial. Moreover, the verifier uses only
$O(\log n)$ random bits.
