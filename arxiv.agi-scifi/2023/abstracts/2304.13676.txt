Title: Multimodal Grounding for Embodied AI via Augmented Reality Headsets for
Natural Language Driven Task Planning
Authors: Selma Wanna, Fabian Parra, Robert Valner, Karl Kruusamäe, Mitch Pryor
ID: 2304.13676
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.13676

Abstract:
Recent advances in generative modeling have spurred a resurgence in the field of
Embodied Artificial Intelligence (EAI). EAI systems typically deploy large
language models to physical systems capable of interacting with their
environment. In our exploration of EAI for industrial domains, we successfully
demonstrate the feasibility of co-located, human-robot teaming. Specifically, we
construct an experiment where an Augmented Reality (AR) headset mediates
information exchange between an EAI agent and human operator for a variety of
inspection tasks. To our knowledge the use of an AR headset for multimodal
grounding and the application of EAI to industrial tasks are novel contributions
within Embodied AI research. In addition, we highlight potential pitfalls in
EAI's construction by providing quantitative and qualitative analysis on prompt
robustness.
