Title: Quantum reservoir computing with classical and nonclassical states in an
integrated optical circuit
Authors: S. Świerczewski, W. Verstraelen, P. Deuar, T. C. H. Liew, A. Opala, M.
Matuszewski
ID: 2603.17103
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17103

Abstract:
Quantum reservoir computing (QRC) is a hardware-implementation-friendly quantum
neural network scheme with minimal physical system requirements and a proven
advantage over classical counterparts. We use an extension of the positive-P
phase space method to efficiently simulate a bosonic, linear silicon-chip based
QRC system excited with a single nonclassical state, a "kitten" state. In
combination with input-encoding coherent states, our method allows to obtain
exact results for all correlation functions without Hilbert space cutoff.
Surprisingly, we find that such a setting - where the only "quantumness''
derives from a single input mode, is sufficient to obtain significant (over
9-fold) reduction of classification error over the classical counterpart. Our
work provides a promising direction toward efficient quantum computation with
accessible optical hardware.
