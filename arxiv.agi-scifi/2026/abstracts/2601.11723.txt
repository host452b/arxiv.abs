Title: A Proof of Concept for a Digital Twin of an Ultrasonic Fermentation
System
Authors: Francesco Saverio Sconocchia Pisoni, Andrea Vitaletti, Davide
Appolloni, Federico Ortenzi, Blasco Morozzo della Rocca, Mariano José Guillén,
Alessandro Contaldo
ID: 2601.11723
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.11723

Abstract:
This paper presents the design and implementation of a proof of concept digital
twin for an innovative ultrasonic-enhanced beer-fermentation system, developed
to enable intelligent monitoring, prediction, and actuation in yeast-growth
environments. A traditional fermentation tank is equipped with a piezoelectric
transducer able to irradiate the tank with ultrasonic waves, providing an
external abiotic stimulus to enhance the growth of yeast and accelerate the
fermentation process. At its core, the digital twin incorporates a predictive
model that estimates yeast's culture density over time based on the surrounding
environmental conditions. To this end, we implement, tailor and extend the model
proposed in Palacios et al., allowing us to effectively handle the limited
number of available training samples by using temperature, ultrasonic frequency,
and duty cycle as inputs. The results obtained along with the assessment of
model performance demonstrate the feasibility of the proposed approach.
