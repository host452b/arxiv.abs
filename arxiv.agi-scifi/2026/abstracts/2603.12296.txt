Title: Synthetic Data Generation for Brain-Computer Interfaces: Overview,
Benchmarking, and Future Directions
Authors: Ziwei Wang, Zhentao He, Xingyi He, Hongbin Wang, Tianwang Jia, Jingwei
Luo, Siyang Li, Xiaoqing Chen, Dongrui Wu
ID: 2603.12296
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12296

Abstract:
Deep learning has achieved transformative performance across diverse domains,
largely driven by the large-scale, high-quality training data. In contrast, the
development of brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) is fundamentally constrained by
the limited, heterogeneous, and privacy-sensitive neural recordings. Generating
synthetic yet physiologically plausible brain signals has therefore emerged as a
compelling way to mitigate data scarcity and enhance model capacity. This survey
provides a comprehensive review of brain signal generation for BCIs, covering
methodological taxonomies, benchmark experiments, evaluation metrics, and key
applications. We systematically categorize existing generative algorithms into
four types: knowledge-based, feature-based, model-based, and translation-based
approaches. Furthermore, we benchmark existing brain signal generation
approaches across four representative BCI paradigms to provide an objective
performance comparison. Finally, we discuss the potentials and challenges of
current generation approaches and prospect future research on accurate, data-
efficient, and privacy-aware BCI systems. The benchmark codebase is publicized
at https://github.com/wzwvv/DG4BCI.
