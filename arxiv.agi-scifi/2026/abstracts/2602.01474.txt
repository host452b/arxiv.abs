Title: Legal Infrastructure for Transformative AI Governance
Authors: Gillian K. Hadfield
ID: 2602.01474
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.01474

Abstract:
Most of our AI governance efforts focus on substance: what rules do we want in
place? What limits or checks do we want to impose on AI development and
deployment? But a key role for law is not only to establish substantive rules
but also to establish legal and regulatory infrastructure to generate and
implement rules. The transformative nature of AI calls especially for attention
to building legal and regulatory frameworks. In this PNAS Perspective piece I
review three examples I have proposed: the creation of registration regimes for
frontier models; the creation of registration and identification regimes for
autonomous agents; and the design of regulatory markets to facilitate a role for
private companies to innovate and deliver AI regulatory services.
