Title: The AI Layoff Trap
Authors: Brett Hemenway Falk, Gerry Tsoukalas
ID: 2603.20617
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20617

Abstract:
If AI displaces human workers faster than the economy can reabsorb them, it
risks eroding the very consumer demand firms depend on. We show that knowing
this is not enough for firms to stop it. In a competitive task-based model,
demand externalities trap rational firms in an automation arms race, displacing
workers well beyond what is collectively optimal. The resulting loss harms both
workers and firm owners. More competition and "better" AI amplify the excess;
wage adjustments and free entry cannot eliminate it. Neither can capital income
taxes, worker equity participation, universal basic income, upskilling, or
Coasian bargaining. Only a Pigouvian automation tax can. The results suggest
that policy should address not only the aftermath of AI labor displacement but
also the competitive incentives that drive it.
