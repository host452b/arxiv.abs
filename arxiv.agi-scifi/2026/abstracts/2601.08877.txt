Title: Silenced by Design Censorship, Governance, and the Politics of Access in
Generative AI Refusal Behavior
Authors: Kariema El Touny
ID: 2601.08877
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.08877

Abstract:
This paper examines refusal behavior in generative AI systems through a
governance lens. Drawing on historical frameworks of censorship and contemporary
design logics, it argues that refusal is not a neutral safeguard but a site of
power, shaped by institutional risk management and opaque decision-making. The
analysis concludes with user-centered recommendations for ethical refusal
design.
  Keywords: Generative AI governance, AI refusal behavior, Censorship, Ethical
design
