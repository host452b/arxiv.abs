Title: Thinker: A vision-language foundation model for embodied intelligence
Authors: Baiyu Pan, Daqin Luo, Junpeng Yang, Jiyuan Wang, Yixuan Zhang, Hailin
Shi, Jichao Jiao
ID: 2601.21199
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.21199

Abstract:
When large vision-language models are applied to the field of robotics, they
encounter problems that are simple for humans yet error-prone for models. Such
issues include confusion between third-person and first-person perspectives and
a tendency to overlook information in video endings during temporal reasoning.
To address these challenges, we propose Thinker, a large vision-language
foundation model designed for embodied intelligence. We tackle the
aforementioned issues from two perspectives. Firstly, we construct a large-scale
dataset tailored for robotic perception and reasoning, encompassing ego-view
videos, visual grounding, spatial understanding, and chain-of-thought data.
Secondly, we introduce a simple yet effective approach that substantially
enhances the model's capacity for video comprehension by jointly incorporating
key frames and full video sequences as inputs. Our model achieves state-of-the-
art results on two of the most commonly used benchmark datasets in the field of
task planning.
