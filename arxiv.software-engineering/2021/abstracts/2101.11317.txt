Title: Testing in Global Software Development -- A Pattern Approach
Authors: Anneke Pehmöller, Frank Salger, Stefan Wagner
ID: 2101.11317
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.11317

Abstract:
Although testing is critical in GSD, its application in this context has not
been deeply investigated so far. This work investigates testing in GSD. It
provides support for test managers acting in a globally distributed environment.
With this it closes a gap. The leading question is "What problems exist in
testing in GSD and how can they be addressed in projects?" Decomposing this
question we a) identify problems of testing in GSD projects and b) provide good
practices to support practitioners in testing in GSD projects. The research is
realized in the context of Capgemini Germany. Our contribution to solving the
stated research problem is a collection of 16 patterns for testing in GSD
projects. For practitioners the usage of the patterns is simplified by various
views on the patterns. Herewith we stipulate research and support project
managers and test managers in the realization of testing in GSD projects.
