Title: Progress Report on a Proposed Theory for Software Development
Authors: Diana Kirk, Stephen G. MacDonell
ID: 2103.03400
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.03400

Abstract:
There is growing acknowledgement within the software engineering community that
a theory of software development is needed to integrate the myriad methodologies
that are currently popular, some of which are based on opposing perspectives. We
have been developing such a theory for a number of years. In this position
paper, we overview our theory along with progress made thus far. We suggest
that, once fully developed, this theory, or one similar to it, may be applied to
support situated software development, by providing an overarching model within
which software initiatives might be categorised and understood. Such
understanding would inevitably lead to greater predictability with respect to
outcomes.
