Title: Energy Savings by Task Offloading to a Fog Considering Radio Front-End Characteristics
Authors: Pawel Kryszkiewicz, Filip Idzikowski, Bartosz Bossy, Bartosz Kopras, Hanna Bogucka
ID: 2104.12913
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.12913

Abstract:
Fog computing can be used to offload computationally intensive tasks from
battery powered Internet of Things (IoT) devices. Although it reduces energy
required for computations in an IoT device, it uses energy for communications
with the fog. This paper analyzes when usage of fog computing is more energy
efficient than local computing. Detailed energy consumption models are built in
both scenarios with the focus set on the relation between energy consumption and
distortion introduced by a Power Amplifier (PA). Numerical results show that
task offloading to a fog is the most energy efficient for short, wideband links.
