Title: Towards Modal Software Engineering
Authors: Ramy Shahin
ID: 2102.02966
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.02966

Abstract:
In this paper we introduce the notion of Modal Software Engineering:
automatically turning sequential, deterministic programs into semantically
equivalent programs efficiently operating on inputs coming from multiple
overlapping worlds. We are drawing an analogy between modal logics, and software
application domains where multiple sets of inputs (multiple worlds) need to be
processed efficiently. Typically those sets highly overlap, so processing them
independently would involve a lot of redundancy, resulting in lower performance,
and in many cases intractability. Three application domains are presented:
reasoning about feature-based variability of Software Product Lines (SPLs),
probabilistic programming, and approximate programming.
