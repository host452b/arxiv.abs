Title: 2020 State of the Octoverse: Securing the World's Software
Authors: Nicole Forsgren, Bas Alberts, Kevin Backhouse, Grey Baker, Greg Cecarelli et al.
ID: 2110.10246
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.10246

Abstract:
Open source is the connective tissue for much of the information economy. You
would be hard-pressed to find a scenario where your data does not pass through
at least one open source component. Many of the services and technology we all
rely on, from banking to healthcare, also rely on open source software. The
artifacts of open source code serve as critical i infrastructure for much of the
global economy, making the security of open source software mission-critical to
the world.
