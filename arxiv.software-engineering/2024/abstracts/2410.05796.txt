Title: Software analytics for software engineering: A tertiary review
Authors: Muhammad Laiq, Nauman bin Ali, Jürgen Börstler, Emelie Engström
ID: 2410.05796
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.05796

Abstract:
Software analytics (SA) is frequently proposed as a tool to support
practitioners in software engineering (SE) tasks. We have observed that several
secondary studies on SA have been published. Some of these studies have
overlapping aims and some have even been published in the same calendar year.
This presents an opportunity to analyze the congruence or divergence of the
conclusions in these studies. Such an analysis can help identify broader
generalizations beyond any of the individual secondary studies. We identified
five secondary studies on the use of SA for SE. These secondary studies cover
primary research from 2000 to 2021. Despite the overlapping objectives and
search time frames of these secondary studies, there is negligible overlap of
primary studies between these secondary studies. Thus, each of them provides an
isolated view, and together, they provide a fragmented view, i.e., there is no
``common picture'' of the area. Thus, we conclude that an overview of the
literature identified by these secondary studies would be useful in providing a
more comprehensive overview of the topic.
