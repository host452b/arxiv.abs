Title: Generalization of the Apollonius theorem for simplices and related problems
Authors: Michael N. Vrahatis
ID: 2401.03232
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.03232

Abstract:
The Apollonius theorem gives the length of a median of a triangle in terms of
the lengths of its sides. The straightforward generalization of this theorem
obtained for m-simplices in the n-dimensional Euclidean space for n greater than
or equal to m is given. Based on this, generalizations of properties related to
the medians of a triangle are presented. In addition, applications of the
generalized Apollonius' theorem and the related to the medians results, are
given for obtaining: (a) the minimal spherical surface that encloses a given
simplex or a given bounded set, (b) the thickness of a simplex that it provides
a measure for the quality or how well shaped a simplex is, and (c) the
convergence and error estimates of the root-finding bisection method applied on
simplices.
