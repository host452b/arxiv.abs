Title: Security of AI Agents
Authors: Yifeng He, Ethan Wang, Yuyang Rong, Zifei Cheng, Hao Chen
ID: 2406.08689
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.08689

Abstract:
AI agents have been boosted by large language models. AI agents can function as
intelligent assistants and complete tasks on behalf of their users with access
to tools and the ability to execute commands in their environments. Through
studying and experiencing the workflow of typical AI agents, we have raised
several concerns regarding their security. These potential vulnerabilities are
not addressed by the frameworks used to build the agents, nor by research aimed
at improving the agents. In this paper, we identify and describe these
vulnerabilities in detail from a system security perspective, emphasizing their
causes and severe effects. Furthermore, we introduce defense mechanisms
corresponding to each vulnerability with design and experiments to evaluate
their viability. Altogether, this paper contextualizes the security issues in
the current development of AI agents and delineates methods to make AI agents
safer and more reliable.
