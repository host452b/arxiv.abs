Title: VernaCopter: Disambiguated Natural-Language-Driven Robot via Formal Specifications
Authors: Teun van de Laar, Zengjie Zhang, Shuhao Qi, Sofie Haesaert, Zhiyong Sun
ID: 2409.09536
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.09536

Abstract:
It has been an ambition of many to control a robot for a complex task using
natural language (NL). The rise of large language models (LLMs) makes it closer
to coming true. However, an LLM-powered system still suffers from the ambiguity
inherent in an NL and the uncertainty brought up by LLMs. This paper proposes a
novel LLM-based robot motion planner, named \textit{VernaCopter}, with signal
temporal logic (STL) specifications serving as a bridge between NL commands and
specific task objectives. The rigorous and abstract nature of formal
specifications allows the planner to generate high-quality and highly consistent
paths to guide the motion control of a robot. Compared to a conventional
NL-prompting-based planner, the proposed VernaCopter planner is more stable and
reliable due to less ambiguous uncertainty. Its efficacy and advantage have been
validated by two small but challenging experimental scenarios, implying its
potential in designing NL-driven robots.
