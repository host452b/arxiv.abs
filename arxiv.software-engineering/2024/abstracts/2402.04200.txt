Title: Information Systems and Software Engineering: The Case for Convergence
Authors: Brian Fitzgerald
ID: 2402.04200
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.04200

Abstract:
The Information Systems (IS) and Software Engineering (SE) fields share a
remarkable number of similarities in their historical evolution to date. These
similarities are briefly outlined below. An analysis of 10 years (2001-2010) of
publications in the primary journals in both fields also reveals a good deal of
overlap in research topics. Given the challenges faced by both as young
disciplines, there is potentially much to gain from a closer interaction between
both fields than has traditionally been the case. This article seeks to
encourage such interaction, and illustrates how this might usefully occur in the
area of design. It concludes by proposing a number of practical initiatives that
could stimulate and facilitate interaction between the IS and SE fields
