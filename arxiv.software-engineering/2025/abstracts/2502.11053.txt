Title: Demystifying 5G Polar and LDPC Codes: A Comprehensive Review and Foundations
Authors: Mody Sy
ID: 2502.11053
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.11053

Abstract:
Understanding how 5G networks correct errors is no trivial matter. At the heart
of the process lie two sophisticated families of codes: LDPC and polar codes.
This paper opens the black box, not only by explaining how these codes are
designed, but also by showing how they are encoded and decoded in practice. To
map where research currently stands, we present a detailed survey of the
literature supplemented with insights that are often buried deep within
technical standards. These foundations are not just historical footnotes: they
are strong candidates for powering error correction in 6G and beyond. In
bringing clarity to these building blocks, we aim to help engineers and
researchers navigate what is both a complex and increasingly vital part of
wireless communication.
