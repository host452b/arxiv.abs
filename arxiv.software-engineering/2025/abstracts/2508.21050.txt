Title: Dynamics of Gender Bias in Software Engineering
Authors: Thomas J. Misa
ID: 2508.21050
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.21050

Abstract:
The field of software engineering is embedded in both engineering and computer
science, and may embody gender biases endemic to both. This paper surveys
software engineering's origins and its long-running attention to engineering
professionalism, profiling five leaders; it then examines the field's recent
attention to gender issues and gender bias. It next quantitatively analyzes
women's participation as research authors in the field's leading International
Conference of Software Engineering (1976-2010), finding a dozen years with
statistically significant gender exclusion. Policy dimensions of research on
gender bias in computing are suggested.
