Title: Do Large Language Models Understand Performance Optimization?
Authors: Bowen Cui, Tejas Ramesh, Oscar Hernandez, Keren Zhou
ID: 2503.13772
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.13772

Abstract:
Large Language Models (LLMs) have emerged as powerful tools for software
development tasks such as code completion, translation, and optimization.
However, their ability to generate efficient and correct code, particularly in
complex High-Performance Computing (HPC) contexts, has remained underexplored.
To address this gap, this paper presents a comprehensive benchmark suite
encompassing multiple critical HPC computational motifs to evaluate the
performance of code optimized by state-of-the-art LLMs, including OpenAI o1,
Claude-3.5, and Llama-3.2. In addition to analyzing basic computational kernels,
we developed an agent system that integrates LLMs to assess their effectiveness
in real HPC applications. Our evaluation focused on key criteria such as
execution time, correctness, and understanding of HPC-specific concepts. We also
compared the results with those achieved using traditional HPC optimization
tools. Based on the findings, we recognized the strengths of LLMs in
understanding human instructions and performing automated code transformations.
However, we also identified significant limitations, including their tendency to
generate incorrect code and their challenges in comprehending complex control
and data flows in sophisticated HPC code.
