Title: Towards Fast and Scalable Normal Integration using Continuous Components
Authors: Francesco Milano, Jen Jen Chung, Lionel Ott, Roland Siegwart
ID: 2510.11508
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.11508

Abstract:
Surface normal integration is a fundamental problem in computer vision, dealing
with the objective of reconstructing a surface from its corresponding normal
map. Existing approaches require an iterative global optimization to jointly
estimate the depth of each pixel, which scales poorly to larger normal maps. In
this paper, we address this problem by recasting normal integration as the
estimation of relative scales of continuous components. By constraining pixels
belonging to the same component to jointly vary their scale, we drastically
reduce the number of optimization variables. Our framework includes a heuristic
to accurately estimate continuous components from the start, a strategy to
rebalance optimization terms, and a technique to iteratively merge components to
further reduce the size of the problem. Our method achieves state-of-the-art
results on the standard normal integration benchmark in as little as a few
seconds and achieves one-order-of-magnitude speedup over pixel-level approaches
on large-resolution normal maps.
