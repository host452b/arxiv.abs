Title: Manifesto from Dagstuhl Perspectives Workshop 24452 -- Reframing Technical Debt
Authors: Paris Avgeriou, Ipek Ozkaya, Heiko Koziolek, Zadia Codabux, Neil Ernst
ID: 2505.13009
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.13009

Abstract:
This is the Dagstuhl Perspectives Workshop 24452 manifesto on Reframing
Technical Debt. The manifesto begins with a one-page summary of Values, Beliefs,
and Principles. It then elaborates on each Value, Belief, and Principle to
explain their rationale and clarify their meaning. Subsequently, the paper
describes the current landscape of Technical Debt Management methods and tools
and explains why the current practice is inadequate and where current research
falls short. The current landscape is organized into five major topics:
Technical Debt as Value-Creation, Tooling, Data Collection, the role of
Architecture, and Socio-Technical Aspects. Finally, the paper outlines a roadmap
to realize the stated principles, with concrete milestones to be addressed by
researchers, software practitioners, and tool vendors. The manifesto is signed
by the workshop participants.
