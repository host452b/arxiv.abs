Title: Three Patterns to Support Empathy in Computer-Mediated Human Interaction
Authors: Michael J. Lyons, Daniel Kluender
ID: 2011.09088
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.09088

Abstract:
We present three patterns for computer-mediated interaction which we discovered
during the design and development of a platform for remote teaching and learning
of kanji, the Chinese characters used in written Japanese. Our aim in developing
this system was to provide a basis for embodiment in remote interaction, and in
particular to support the experience of empathy by both teacher and student.
From this study, the essential elements are abstracted and suggested as design
patterns for other computer-mediated interaction systems.
