Title: Performance Benchmarking of State-of-the-Art Software Switches for NFV
Authors: Tianzhu Zhang, Leonardo Linguaglossa, Paolo Giaccone, Luigi Iannone, James Roberts
ID: 2003.13489
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.13489

Abstract:
With the ultimate goal of replacing proprietary hardware appliances with Virtual
Network Functions (VNFs) implemented in software, Network Function
Virtualization (NFV) has been gaining popularity in the past few years. Software
switches route traffic between VNFs and physical Network Interface Cards (NICs).
It is of paramount importance to compare the performance of different switch
designs and architectures. In this paper, we propose a methodology to compare
fairly and comprehensively the performance of software switches. We first
explore the design spaces of seven state-of-the-art software switches and then
compare their performance under four representative test scenarios. Each
scenario corresponds to a specific case of routing NFV traffic between NICs
and/or VNFs. In our experiments, we evaluate the throughput and latency between
VNFs in two of the most popular virtualization environments, namely virtual
machines (VMs) and containers. Our experimental results show that no single
software switch prevails in all scenarios. It is, therefore, crucial to choose
the most suitable solution for the given use case. At the same time, the
presented results and analysis provide a deeper insight into the design
tradeoffs and identifies potential performance bottlenecks that could inspire
new designs.
