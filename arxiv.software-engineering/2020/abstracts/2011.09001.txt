Title: Stimulating Entrepreneurship in Teaching Human Computer Interaction
Authors: Dusanka Boskovic, Nihad Borovina, Merima Zukic
ID: 2011.09001
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.09001

Abstract:
Software development requires understanding of users, user needs, user tasks and
context in which they are operating. These skills are familiar to entrepreneurs,
product managers, and marketing experts. However, our teaching experience
suggests that students generally find these topics less attractive as they
perceive them to be far too theoretical and thus, not as useful. During the
years of teaching the Human Computer Interaction course we have noticed
students' preferences for learning technology oriented methods, or what we refer
to topics belonging to solution domain. The changes in the modernized HCI course
introduced Product Market Fit canvas in order to bridge the gap between
'theoretical' and 'practical' part of the course.
