Title: Is the OWASP Top 10 list comprehensive enough for writing secure code?
Authors: Parth Sane
ID: 2002.11269
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2002.11269

Abstract:
The OWASP Top 10 is a list that is published by the Open Web Application
Security Project (OWASP). The general purpose is to serve as a watchlist for
bugs to avoid while writing code. This paper compares how many of those weakness
as described in the top ten list are actually reported in vulnerabilities listed
in the National Vulnerability Database (NVD). That way it makes it possible to
empirically show whether the OWASP Top 10 list is comprehensive enough or not,
for code weaknesses that have been found in the past decade.
