Title: On the Empirical Evidence of Microservice Logical Coupling. A Registered Report
Authors: Dario Amoroso d Aragona, Luca Pascarella, Andrea Janes, Valentina Lenarduzzi, Rafael Penaloza et al.
ID: 2306.02036
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.02036

Abstract:
[Context] Coupling is a widely discussed metric by software engineers while
developing complex software systems, often referred to as a crucial factor and
symptom of a poor or good design. Nevertheless, measuring the logical coupling
among microservices and analyzing the interactions between services is
non-trivial because it demands runtime information in the form of log files,
which are not always accessible. [Objective and Method] In this work, we propose
the design of a study aimed at empirically validating the Microservice Logical
Coupling (MLC) metric presented in our previous study. In particular, we plan to
empirically study Open Source Systems (OSS) built using a microservice
architecture. [Results] The result of this work aims at corroborating the
effectiveness and validity of the MLC metric. Thus, we will gather empirical
evidence and develop a methodology to analyze and support the claims regarding
the MLC metric. Furthermore, we establish its usefulness in evaluating and
understanding the logical coupling among microservices.
