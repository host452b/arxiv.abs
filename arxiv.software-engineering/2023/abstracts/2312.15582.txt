Title: Decoding Fear: Exploring User Experiences in Virtual Reality Horror Games
Authors: He Zhang, Xinyang Li, Christine Qiu, Xinyi Fu
ID: 2312.15582
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.15582

Abstract:
This preliminary study investigated user experiences in VR horror games,
highlighting fear-triggering and gender-based differences in perception. By
utilizing a scientifically validated and specially designed questionnaire, we
successfully collected questionnaire data from 23 subjects for an early
empirical study of fear induction in a virtual reality gaming environment. The
early findings suggest that visual restrictions and ambient sound-enhanced
realism may be more effective in intensifying the fear experience. Participants
exhibited a tendency to avoid playing alone or during nighttime, underscoring
the significant psychological impact of VR horror games. The study also revealed
a distinct gender difference in fear perception, with female participants
exhibiting a higher sensitivity to fear stimuli. However, the preference for
different types of horror games was not solely dominated by males; it varied
depending on factors such as the game's pace, its objectives, and the nature of
the fear stimulant.
