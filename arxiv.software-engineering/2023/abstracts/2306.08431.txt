Title: Team Composition in Software Engineering Education
Authors: Sajid Ibrahim Hashmi, Jouni Markkula
ID: 2306.08431
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.08431

Abstract:
One of the objectives of software engineering education is to make students to
learn essential teamwork skills. This is done by having the students work in
groups for course assignments. Student team composition plays a vital role in
this, as it significantly affects learning outcomes, what is learned, and how.
The study presented in this paper aims to better understand the student team
composition in software engineering education and investigate the factors
affecting it in the international software engineering education context. Those
factors should be taken into consideration by software engineering teachers when
they design group work assignments in their courses. In this paper, the initial
findings of the ongoing Action research study are presented. The results give
some identified principles that should be considered when designing student team
composition in software engineering courses.
