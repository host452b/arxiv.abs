Title: State-Of-The-Practice in Quality Assurance in Java-Based Open Source Software Development
Authors: Ali Khatami, Andy Zaidman
ID: 2306.09665
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.09665

Abstract:
To ensure the quality of software systems, software engineers can make use of a
variety of quality assurance approaches, such as software testing, modern code
review, automated static analysis, and build automation. Each of these quality
assurance practices has been studied in depth in isolation, but there is a clear
knowledge gap when it comes to our understanding of how these approaches are
being used in conjunction or not. In our study, we broadly investigate whether
and how these quality assurance approaches are being used in conjunction in the
development of 1,454 popular open source software projects on GitHub. Our study
indicates that typically projects do not follow all quality assurance practices
together with high intensity. In fact, we only observe weak correlation among
some quality assurance practices. In general, our study provides a deeper
understanding of how existing quality assurance approaches are currently being
used in Java-based open source software development. Besides, we specifically
zoomed in on the more mature projects in our dataset, and generally, we observe
that more mature projects are more intense in their application of the quality
assurance practices, with more focus on their ASAT usage and code reviewing, but
no strong change in their CI usage.
