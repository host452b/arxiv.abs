Title: Towards Maintainable Platform Software -- Delivery Cost Control in Continuous Software Development
Authors: Ning Luo, Yue Xiong
ID: 2203.15396
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.15396

Abstract:
Modern platform software delivery cost increases rapidly as it usually needs to
align with many hardware and silicon's TTMs, feature evolvement and involves
hundreds of engineers. In this paper, citing one ultra-large-scale software -
Intel Media Driver as an example, we analyze the hotspots leading to delivery
cost increase in continuous software development, the challenges on our software
design and our experiences on software delivery cost shrink against the targeted
design enhancements. We expect the identified hotspots can help more researchers
to form the corresponding research agendas and the experiences shared can help
following practitioners to apply similar enhancements.
