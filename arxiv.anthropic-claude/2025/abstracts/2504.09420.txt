Title: SaRO: Enhancing LLM Safety through Reasoning-based Alignment
Authors: Yutao Mou, Yuxiao Luo, Shikun Zhang, Wei Ye
ID: 2504.09420
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.09420

Abstract:
Current safety alignment techniques for large language models (LLMs) face two
key challenges: (1) under-generalization, which leaves models vulnerable to
novel jailbreak attacks, and (2) over-alignment, which leads to the excessive
refusal of benign instructions. Our preliminary investigation reveals semantic
overlap between jailbreak/harmful queries and normal prompts in embedding space,
suggesting that more effective safety alignment requires a deeper semantic
understanding. This motivates us to incorporate safety-policy-driven reasoning
into the alignment process. To this end, we propose the Safety-oriented
Reasoning Optimization Framework (SaRO), which consists of two stages: (1)
Reasoning-style Warmup (RW) that enables LLMs to internalize long-chain
reasoning through supervised fine-tuning, and (2) Safety-oriented Reasoning
Process Optimization (SRPO) that promotes safety reflection via direct
preference optimization (DPO). Extensive experiments demonstrate the superiority
of SaRO over traditional alignment methods.
