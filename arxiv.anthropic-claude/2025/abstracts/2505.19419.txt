Title: It's Not Just Labeling -- A Research on LLM Generated Feedback Interpretability and Image Labeling Sketch Features
Authors: Baichuan Li, Larry Powell, Tracy Hammond
ID: 2505.19419
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.19419

The quality of training data is critical to the performance of machine learning
applications in domains like transportation, healthcare, and robotics. Accurate
image labeling, however, often relies on time-consuming, expert-driven methods
with limited feedback. This research introduces a sketch-based annotation
approach supported by large language models (LLMs) to reduce technical barriers
and enhance accessibility. Using a synthetic dataset, we examine how sketch
recognition features relate to LLM feedback metrics, aiming to improve the
reliability and interpretability of LLM-assisted labeling. We also explore how
prompting strategies and sketch variations influence feedback quality. Our main
contribution is a sketch-based virtual assistant that simplifies annotation for
non-experts and advances LLM-driven labeling tools in terms of scalability,
accessibility, and explainability.
