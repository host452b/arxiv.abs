Title: A2D: Any-Order, Any-Step Safety Alignment for Diffusion Language Models
Authors: Wonje Jeung, Sangyeon Yoon, Yoonjun Cho, Dongjae Jeon, Sangwoo Shin et al.
ID: 2509.23286
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.23286

Abstract:
Diffusion large language models (dLLMs) enable any-order generation, but this
flexibility enlarges the attack surface: harmful spans may appear at arbitrary
positions, and template-based prefilling attacks such as DIJA bypass
response-level refusals. We introduce A2D (Any-Order, Any-Step Defense), a
token-level alignment method that aligns dLLMs to emit an [EOS] refusal signal
whenever harmful content arises. By aligning safety directly at the token-level
under randomized masking, A2D achieves robustness to both any-decoding-order and
any-step prefilling attacks under various conditions. It also enables real-time
monitoring: dLLMs may begin a response but automatically terminate if unsafe
continuation emerges. On safety benchmarks, A2D consistently prevents the
generation of harmful outputs, slashing DIJA success rates from over 80% to
near-zero (1.3% on LLaDA-8B-Instruct, 0.0% on Dream-v0-Instruct-7B), and
thresholded [EOS] probabilities allow early rejection, yielding up to 19.3x
faster safe termination.
