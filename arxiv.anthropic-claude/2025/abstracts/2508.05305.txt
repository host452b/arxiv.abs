Title: SONAR-LLM: Autoregressive Transformer that Thinks in Sentence Embeddings and Speaks in Tokens
Authors: Nikita Dragunov, Temurbek Rahmatullaev, Elizaveta Goncharova, Andrey Kuznetsov, Anton Razzhigaev
ID: 2508.05305
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.05305

The recently proposed Large Concept Model (LCM) generates text by predicting a
sequence of sentence-level embeddings and training with either mean-squared
error or diffusion objectives. We present SONAR-LLM, a decoder-only transformer
that "thinks" in the same continuous SONAR embedding space, yet is supervised
through token-level cross-entropy propagated via the frozen SONAR decoder. This
hybrid objective retains the semantic abstraction of LCM while eliminating its
diffusion sampler and restoring a likelihood-based training signal. Across model
sizes from 39M to 1.3B parameters, SONAR-LLM attains competitive generation
quality. We report scaling trends, ablations, benchmark results, and release the
complete training code and all pretrained checkpoints to foster reproducibility
and future research.
