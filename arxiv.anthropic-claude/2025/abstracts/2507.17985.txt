Title: How K-12 Educators Use AI: LLM-Assisted Qualitative Analysis at Scale
Authors: Alex Liu, Lief Esbenshade, Shawon Sarkar, Victor Tian, Zachary Zhang et al.
ID: 2507.17985
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.17985

Abstract:
This study investigates how K-12 educators use generative AI tools in real-world
instructional contexts and how large language models (LLMs) can support scalable
qualitative analysis of these interactions. Drawing on over 13,000 unscripted
educator-AI conversations from an open-access platform, we examine educators'
use of AI for lesson planning, differentiation, assessment, and pedagogical
reflection. Methodologically, we introduce a replicable, LLM-assisted
qualitative analysis pipeline that supports inductive theme discovery, codebook
development, and large-scale annotation while preserving researcher control over
conceptual synthesis. Empirically, the findings surface concrete patterns in how
educators prompt, adapt, and evaluate AI-generated suggestions as part of their
instructional reasoning. This work demonstrates the feasibility of combining LLM
support with qualitative rigor to analyze complex educator behaviors at scale
and inform the design of AI-powered educational tools.
