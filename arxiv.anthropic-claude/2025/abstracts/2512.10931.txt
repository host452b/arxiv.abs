Title: Asynchronous Reasoning: Training-Free Interactive Thinking LLMs
Authors: George Yakushev, Nataliia Babina, Masoud Vahid Dastgerdi, Vyacheslav Zhdanovskiy, Denis Kuznedelev et al. (+2)
ID: 2512.10931
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.10931

Many state-of-the-art LLMs are trained to think before giving their answer.
Reasoning can greatly improve language model capabilities, but it also makes
them less interactive: given a new input, a model must stop thinking before it
can respond. Real-world use cases such as voice-based or embodied assistants
require an LLM agent to respond and adapt to additional information in real
time, which is incompatible with sequential interactions. In contrast, humans
can listen, think, and act asynchronously: we begin thinking about the problem
while reading it and continue thinking while formulating the answer. In this
work, we augment LLMs capable of reasoning to operate in a similar way without
additional training. Our method uses the properties of positional embeddings to
enable LLMs built for sequential generation to simultaneously think, listen, and
write outputs. We evaluate our approach on math, commonsense, and safety
reasoning: it allows models to generate accurate thinking-augmented answers
while reducing time to first non-thinking token from minutes to ${\le}$ 5s and
the overall real-time delays by up to $12{\times}$.
