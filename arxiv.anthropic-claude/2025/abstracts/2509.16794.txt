Title: The Even Sheen of AI: Kitsch, LLMs, and Homogeneity
Authors: Gyburg Uhlmann
ID: 2509.16794
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.16794

The exploding use and impact of Chatbots such as ChatGPT that are based on Large
Language Models urgently call for a language which is fit to clearly describe
functions and problems of the production process and qualities of the Chatbots'
textual and image output. Recently, the discussion about appropriate and
illuminating metaphors to describe LLMs has gained momentum. As an alternative
to well-established metaphors such as "hallucinating" and "bullshit", we propose
"kitsch" as a new metaphor. As an internationally widespread term from literary
and cultural studies, we argue that "kitsch" is particularly suitable for
analytically illuminating a previously neglected feature of LLM-based images and
texts: their tendency to produce homogeneous and average content, which is
becoming increasingly dominant as the proportion of AI-generated content on the
internet grows. This is leading to the equalisation of language, style and
argument. In view of the potential negative consequences of this averaging,
including for human content producers on the internet, we advocate combining
methods and insights from kitsch studies with AI research, philosophy, and
communication studies in order to better understand the phenomenon and develop
countermeasures.
