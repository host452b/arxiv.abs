Title: Auditing multimodal large language models for context-aware content moderation
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.f2p7d_v2
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.f2p7d_v2

The development of multimodal large language models (MLLMs) offers new
possibilities for context-aware content moderation by integrating text, images,
and other data. This study uses a series of conjoint experiments to
systematically audit GPT-4o, a state-of-the-art MLLM, to understand how it uses
demographics and other contextual information when assessing hate speech. We
show that GPT-4o's moderation decisions align with human judgments, particularly
in recognizing norms regarding the use of reclaimed slurs by marginalized
groups, and additional prompting can further enhance sensitivity to context. We
further demonstrate that multimodal identity cues---combining visual and textual
information---facilitate more nuanced content evaluation than unimodal
approaches. This research underscores the potential and limitations of MLLMs for
automated moderation and introduces conjoint analysis as a tool for auditing AI
use in complex, context-sensitive applications.
