Title: Sobolev acceleration for neural networks
Authors: Jong Kwon Oh, Hanbaek Lyu, Hwijae Son
ID: 2509.19773
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.19773

Sobolev training, which integrates target derivatives into the loss functions,
has been shown to accelerate convergence and improve generalization compared to
conventional $L^2$ training. However, the underlying mechanisms of this training
method remain only partially understood. In this work, we present the first
rigorous theoretical framework proving that Sobolev training accelerates the
convergence of Rectified Linear Unit (ReLU) networks. Under a student-teacher
framework with Gaussian inputs and shallow architectures, we derive exact
formulas for population gradients and Hessians, and quantify the improvements in
conditioning of the loss landscape and gradient-flow convergence rates.
Extensive numerical experiments validate our theoretical findings and show that
the benefits of Sobolev training extend to modern deep learning tasks.
