Title: Align-then-Unlearn: Embedding Alignment for LLM Unlearning
Authors: Philipp Spohn, Leander Girrbach, Jessica Bader, Zeynep Akata
ID: 2506.13181
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13181

Abstract:
As large language models (LLMs) are trained on massive datasets, they have
raised significant privacy and ethical concerns due to their potential to
inadvertently retain sensitive information. Unlearning seeks to selectively
remove specific data from trained models, such as personal information or
copyrighted content. Current approaches targeting specific output sequences at
the token level often fail to achieve complete forgetting and remain susceptible
to prompt rephrasing. We propose Align-then-Unlearn, a novel framework that
performs unlearning in the semantic embedding space rather than directly on
output tokens. Align-then-Unlearn first augments the LLM with an embedding
prediction module trained to anticipate future context representations.
Unlearning is then achieved by fine-tuning the model to minimize the similarity
between these predicted embeddings and a target embedding that represents the
concept to be removed. Initial results show that Align-then-Unlearn effectively
removes targeted knowledge with minimal degradation in overall model utility.
These findings suggest that embedding-based unlearning offers a promising and
robust approach to removing conceptual knowledge. Our code is available at
https://github.com/ExplainableML/align-then-unlearn.
