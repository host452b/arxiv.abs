Title: Technique to Baseline QE Artefact Generation Aligned to Quality Metrics
Authors: Eitan Farchi, Kiran Nayak, Papia Ghosh Majumdar, Saritha Route
ID: 2511.15733
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.15733

Large Language Models (LLMs) are transforming Quality Engineering (QE) by
automating the generation of artefacts such as requirements, test cases, and
Behavior Driven Development (BDD) scenarios. However, ensuring the quality of
these outputs remains a challenge. This paper presents a systematic technique to
baseline and evaluate QE artefacts using quantifiable metrics. The approach
combines LLM-driven generation, reverse generation , and iterative refinement
guided by rubrics technique for clarity, completeness, consistency, and
testability. Experimental results across 12 projects show that reverse-generated
artefacts can outperform low-quality inputs and maintain high standards when
inputs are strong. The framework enables scalable, reliable QE artefact
validation, bridging automation with accountability.
