Title: LLM Safety Alignment is Divergence Estimation in Disguise
Authors: Rajdeep Haldar, Ziyi Wang, Qifan Song, Guang Lin, Yue Xing
ID: 2502.00657
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.00657

Abstract:
We present a theoretical framework showing that popular LLM alignment methods,
including RLHF and its variants, can be understood as divergence estimators
between aligned (safe or preferred) and unaligned (harmful or less preferred)
distributions. This perspective explains the emergence of separation in the
latent space between safe and harmful prompts after alignment. As an application
of our general divergence framework, we propose KLDO, a novel KL
divergence-based alignment method, and empirically validate its effectiveness.
We further show that using compliance-refusal datasets, rather than standard
preference-based datasets, leads to stronger separation and improved safety
alignment. Finally, to quantify the separation effect, we propose a
distance-based metric in the prompt representation space, which also acts as a
statistically significant indicator for model safety.
