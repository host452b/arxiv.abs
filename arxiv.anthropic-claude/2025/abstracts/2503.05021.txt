Title: Safety is Not Only About Refusal: Reasoning-Enhanced Fine-tuning for Interpretable LLM Safety
Authors: Yuyou Zhang, Miao Li, William Han, Yihang Yao, Zhepeng Cen et al. (+1)
ID: 2503.05021
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.05021

Large Language Models (LLMs) are vulnerable to jailbreak attacks that exploit
weaknesses in traditional safety alignment, which often relies on rigid refusal
heuristics or representation engineering to block harmful outputs. While they
are effective for direct adversarial attacks, they fall short of broader safety
challenges requiring nuanced, context-aware decision-making. To address this, we
propose Reasoning-enhanced Finetuning for interpretable LLM Safety (Rational), a
novel framework that trains models to engage in explicit safe reasoning before
response. Fine-tuned models leverage the extensive pretraining knowledge in
self-generated reasoning to bootstrap their own safety through structured
reasoning, internalizing context-sensitive decision-making. Our findings suggest
that safety extends beyond refusal, requiring context awareness for more robust,
interpretable, and adaptive responses. Reasoning is not only a core capability
of LLMs but also a fundamental mechanism for LLM safety. Rational employs
reasoning-enhanced fine-tuning, allowing it to reject harmful prompts while
providing meaningful and context-aware responses in complex scenarios.
