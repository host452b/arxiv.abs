Title: Separable Hamiltonian Neural Networks
Authors: Zi-Yu Khoo, Dawen Wu, Jonathan Sze Choong Low, Stéphane Bressan
ID: 2309.01069
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.01069

Hamiltonian neural networks (HNNs) are state-of-the-art models that regress the
vector field of a dynamical system under the learning bias of Hamilton's
equations. A recent observation is that embedding a bias regarding the additive
separability of the Hamiltonian reduces the regression complexity and improves
regression performance. We propose separable HNNs that embed additive
separability within HNNs using observational, learning, and inductive biases. We
show that the proposed models are more effective than the HNN at regressing the
Hamiltonian and the vector field. Consequently, the proposed models predict the
dynamics and conserve the total energy of the Hamiltonian system more
accurately.
