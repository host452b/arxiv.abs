Title: The Impact of Preference Agreement in Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback: A Case Study in Summarization
Authors: Sian Gooding, Hassan Mansoor
ID: 2311.04919
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.04919

Abstract:
Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) can be used to capture complex
and nuanced properties of text generation quality. As a result, the task of text
summarization has been identified as a good candidate for this process. In this
paper, we explore how preference agreement impacts the efficacy of RLHF for
summarization. We show that sampling human preferences to include a range of
annotator agreement results in (1) higher accuracy reward models and (2) alters
the characteristics of quality captured. We additionally show improvements in
downstream generation when using a reward model trained with a range of
preference agreements. Our contributions have implications for the design of
synthetic datasets as well as the importance of considering quality
differentials in comparison-based data.
