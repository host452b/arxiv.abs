Title: Copy Recurrent Neural Network Structure Network
Authors: Xiaofan Zhou, Xunzhu Tang
ID: 2305.13250
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.13250

Electronic Health Record (EHR) coding involves automatically classifying EHRs
into diagnostic codes. While most previous research treats this as a multi-label
classification task, generating probabilities for each code and selecting those
above a certain threshold as labels, these approaches often overlook the
challenge of identifying complex diseases. In this study, our focus is on
detecting complication diseases within EHRs.   We propose a novel coarse-to-fine
ICD path generation framework called the Copy Recurrent Neural Network Structure
Network (CRNNet), which employs a Path Generator (PG) and a Path Discriminator
(PD) for EHR coding. By using RNNs to generate sequential outputs and
incorporating a copy module, we efficiently identify complication diseases. Our
method achieves a 57.30\% ratio of complex diseases in predictions,
outperforming state-of-the-art and previous approaches.   Additionally, through
an ablation study, we demonstrate that the copy mechanism plays a crucial role
in detecting complex diseases.
