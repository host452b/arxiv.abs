Title: What is Multimodality?
Authors: Letitia Parcalabescu, Nils Trost, Anette Frank
ID: 2103.06304
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.06304

The last years have shown rapid developments in the field of multimodal machine
learning, combining e.g., vision, text or speech. In this position paper we
explain how the field uses outdated definitions of multimodality that prove
unfit for the machine learning era. We propose a new task-relative definition of
(multi)modality in the context of multimodal machine learning that focuses on
representations and information that are relevant for a given machine learning
task. With our new definition of multimodality we aim to provide a missing
foundation for multimodal research, an important component of language grounding
and a crucial milestone towards NLU.
