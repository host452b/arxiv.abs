Title: The educational wealth divide in Europe: Post-secondary enrollment gaps across wealth components and countries
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.s4qx6_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.s4qx6_v1

Wealth is a key dimension of socioeconomic status (SES) and its
intergenerational transmission, with parental wealth positively affecting
children's educational outcomes. Previous research, however, has been limited to
a small number of countries, and effect sizes that are hardly comparable across
institutional contexts. Furthermore, the specific role of different wealth
components remain unclear. This study addresses these gaps by comparing wealth
gaps in education – measured as enrolment in post-secondary education – and
examining the role of various wealth components across 15 European countries
using harmonized data from the Household Finance and Consumption Survey. We find
significant wealth gaps in education in all countries. In 10 countries, these
gaps remain after controlling for other dimensions of parental SES. Regarding
net wealth, our study documents substantial education gaps in Southern and
Eastern European and smaller gaps in most Continental countries. In most
countries, real wealth is the primary driver of these educational disparities.
Conversely, the association between debt and post-secondary enrolment varies
from positive to negative across countries. Our findings highlight the
importance of parental real assets for education and the ambivalent and context
specific role of debt.
