Title: Congressional Witnesses Matter: Proving Witness Testimony Impact Using Large Language Models
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.zhyjv_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.zhyjv_v1

Inspired by anecdotal evidence of congressional witness testimony impact, we
analyze a dataset of 661 testimonies from hearings between 2007 and 2017
associated with the legislative history of the Patient Protection and Affordable
Care Act (PL 111-148) to demonstrate that witness testimony impacts legislative,
administrative, and judicial outcomes. We analyze 76,138 pages of legislative
documents, administrative guidance, and judicial proceedings, which we designate
as the “Legislative History and Impact Corpus” (LHIC). We fine tune a large
language model (LLM) to conduct a comprehensive search for high-semantic
similarity sentence pairings or strong associations between witness testimony
and outcomes. The direct references and high-similarity statements we identify
prove that congressional witness testimony does impact outcomes across the
legislative and judicial branches. We identify testimony from witnesses serving
myriad functions impacting legislative and judicial outcomes, and these impacts
were reflected irrespective of political party or role in Congress and at
multiple points during judicial challenges. Additionally, we found (1) instances
where witness testimony has impacts outside of the legislative process; and (2)
long-term relationships between witnesses and Congress influence legislative
outcomes.   While our findings may be obvious to those who have ever worked on
Capitol Hill or testified before Congress, these findings are not well-
documented in the existing political science literature. Ours is one of the
first papers to use LLMs to investigate congressional hearings, and these tools
expand the potential for political textual analysis.
