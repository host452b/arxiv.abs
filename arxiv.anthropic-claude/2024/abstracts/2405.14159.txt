Title: Super Tiny Language Models
Authors: Dylan Hillier, Leon Guertler, Cheston Tan, Palaash Agrawal, Chen Ruirui et al. (+1)
ID: 2405.14159
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.14159

The rapid advancement of large language models (LLMs) has led to significant
improvements in natural language processing but also poses challenges due to
their high computational and energy demands. This paper introduces a series of
research efforts focused on Super Tiny Language Models (STLMs), which aim to
deliver high performance with significantly reduced parameter counts. We explore
innovative techniques such as byte-level tokenization with a pooling mechanism,
weight tying, and efficient training strategies. These methods aim to
significantly reduce reduce the parameter count compared to traditional models
-- in future works, we aim to build on these in a way that maintains and
improves upon the performance of base transformer models. This series of papers
will explore into various subproblems, including tokenizer-free models, self-
play based training, and alternative training objectives. We will target models
with 10M, 50M, and 100M parameters. Our ultimate goal is to make high-
performance language models more accessible and practical for a wide range of
applications.
