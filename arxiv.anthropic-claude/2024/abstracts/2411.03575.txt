Title: Semantic Navigation for AI-assisted Ideation
Authors: Thomas Sandholm, Sarah Dong, Sayandev Mukherjee, John Feland, Bernardo A. Huberman
ID: 2411.03575
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.03575

Abstract:
We present a novel AI-based ideation assistant and evaluate it in a user study
with a group of innovators. The key contribution of our work is twofold: we
propose a method of idea exploration in a constrained domain by means of
LLM-supported semantic navigation of problem and solution spaces, and employ
novel automated data input filtering to improve generations. We found that
semantic exploration is preferred to the traditional prompt-output interactions,
measured both in explicit survey rankings, and in terms of innovation assistant
engagement, where 2.1x more generations were performed using semantic
exploration. We also show that filtering input data with metrics such as
relevancy, coherence and human alignment leads to improved generations in the
same metrics as well as enhanced quality of experience among innovators.
