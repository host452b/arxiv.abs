Title: Multimodal Generalized Category Discovery
Authors: Yuchang Su, Renping Zhou, Siyu Huang, Xingjian Li, Tianyang Wang et al. (+2)
ID: 2409.11624
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.11624

Generalized Category Discovery (GCD) aims to classify inputs into both known and
novel categories, a task crucial for open-world scientific discoveries. However,
current GCD methods are limited to unimodal data, overlooking the inherently
multimodal nature of most real-world data. In this work, we extend GCD to a
multimodal setting, where inputs from different modalities provide richer and
complementary information. Through theoretical analysis and empirical
validation, we identify that the key challenge in multimodal GCD lies in
effectively aligning heterogeneous information across modalities. To address
this, we propose MM-GCD, a novel framework that aligns both the feature and
output spaces of different modalities using contrastive learning and
distillation techniques. MM-GCD achieves new state-of-the-art performance on the
UPMC-Food101 and N24News datasets, surpassing previous methods by 11.5\% and
4.7\%, respectively.
