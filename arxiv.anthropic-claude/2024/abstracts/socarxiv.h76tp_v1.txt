Title: Navigating the Digital Turn (Again): ChatGPT and Large Language Models in Anthropological Education
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.h76tp_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.h76tp_v1

The integration of Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT into
anthropological teaching presents a host of questions, problems, and
possibilities. This chapter aims to provide a comprehensive introduction to how
anthropologists may use LLM-powered chatbots like ChatGPT in higher education.
Concretely, I identify three primary avenues of practical LLM application:
teacher preparation, student homework, and student supervision, each offering
unique opportunities and challenges. To navigate this new digital turn in
anthropological education, this paper advocates for an affordance-based view of
GenAI, understanding its potential and limitations while being acutely aware of
its role in shaping new social and political contexts for learning and teaching.
Through this lens, the chapter calls for a balanced integration of AI in
academia, aligning technological advancements with core values of
anthropological education in exploration of hybrid educational models.
