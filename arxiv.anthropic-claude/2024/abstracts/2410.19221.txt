Title: Can Stories Help LLMs Reason? Curating Information Space Through Narrative
Authors: Vahid Sadiri Javadi, Johanne R. Trippas, Yash Kumar Lal, Lucie Flek
ID: 2410.19221
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.19221

Abstract:
Narratives are widely recognized as a powerful tool for structuring information
and facilitating comprehension of complex ideas in various domains such as
science communication. This paper investigates whether incorporating narrative
elements can assist Large Language Models (LLMs) in solving complex problems
more effectively. We propose a novel approach, Story of Thought (SoT),
integrating narrative structures into prompting techniques for problem-solving.
This approach involves constructing narratives around problem statements and
creating a framework to identify and organize relevant information. Our
experiments show that using various LLMs with SoT consistently surpasses using
them with other techniques on physics, chemistry, math, and biology questions in
both the GPQA and JEEBench datasets. The narrative-based information curation
process in SoT enhances problem comprehension by contextualizing critical
in-domain information and highlighting causal relationships within the problem
space.
