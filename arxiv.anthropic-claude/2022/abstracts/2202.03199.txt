Title: AI Research Associate for Early-Stage Scientific Discovery
Authors: Morad Behandish, John Maxwell, Johan de Kleer
ID: 2202.03199
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.03199

Abstract:
Artificial intelligence (AI) has been increasingly applied in scientific
activities for decades; however, it is still far from an insightful and
trustworthy collaborator in the scientific process. Most existing AI methods are
either too simplistic to be useful in real problems faced by scientists or too
domain-specialized (even dogmatized), stifling transformative discoveries or
paradigm shifts. We present an AI research associate for early-stage scientific
discovery based on (a) a novel minimally-biased ontology for physics-based
modeling that is context-aware, interpretable, and generalizable across
classical and relativistic physics; (b) automatic search for viable and
parsimonious hypotheses, represented at a high-level (via domain-agnostic
constructs) with built-in invariants, e.g., postulated forms of conservation
principles implied by a presupposed spacetime topology; and (c) automatic
compilation of the enumerated hypotheses to domain-specific, interpretable, and
trainable/testable tensor-based computation graphs to learn phenomenological
relations, e.g., constitutive or material laws, from sparse (and possibly noisy)
data sets.
