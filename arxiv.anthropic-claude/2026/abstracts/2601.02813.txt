Title: HAL: Inducing Human-likeness in LLMs with Alignment
Authors: Masum Hasan, Junjie Zhao, Ehsan Hoque
ID: 2601.02813
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.02813

Abstract:
Conversational human-likeness plays a central role in human-AI interaction, yet
it has remained difficult to define, measure, and optimize. As a result,
improvements in human-like behavior are largely driven by scale or broad
supervised training, rather than targeted alignment. We introduce Human Aligning
LLMs (HAL), a framework for aligning language models to conversational
human-likeness using an interpretable, data-driven reward. HAL derives explicit
conversational traits from contrastive dialogue data, combines them into a
compact scalar score, and uses this score as a transparent reward signal for
alignment with standard preference optimization methods. Using this approach, we
align models of varying sizes without affecting their overall performance. In
large-scale human evaluations, models aligned with HAL are more frequently
perceived as human-like in conversation. Because HAL operates over explicit,
interpretable traits, it enables inspection of alignment behavior and diagnosis
of unintended effects. More broadly, HAL demonstrates how soft, qualitative
properties of language--previously outside the scope for alignment--can be made
measurable and aligned in an interpretable and explainable way.
