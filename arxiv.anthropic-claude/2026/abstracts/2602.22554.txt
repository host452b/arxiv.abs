Title: Multilingual Safety Alignment Via Sparse Weight Editing
Authors: Jiaming Liang, Zhaoxin Wang, Handing Wang
ID: 2602.22554
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22554

Abstract:
Large Language Models (LLMs) exhibit significant safety disparities across
languages, with low-resource languages (LRLs) often bypassing safety guardrails
established for high-resource languages (HRLs) like English. Existing solutions,
such as multilingual supervised fine-tuning (SFT) or Reinforcement Learning from
Human Feedback (RLHF), are computationally expensive and dependent on scarce
multilingual safety data. In this work, we propose a novel, training-free
alignment framework based on Sparse Weight Editing. Identifying that safety
capabilities are localized within a sparse set of safety neurons, we formulate
the cross-lingual alignment problem as a constrained linear transformation. We
derive a closed-form solution to optimally map the harmful representations of
LRLs to the robust safety subspaces of HRLs, while preserving general utility
via a null-space projection constraint. Extensive experiments across 8 languages
and multiple model families (Llama-3, Qwen-2.5) demonstrate that our method
substantially reduces Attack Success Rate (ASR) in LRLs with negligible impact
on general reasoning capabilities, all achieved with a single, data-efficient
calculation.
