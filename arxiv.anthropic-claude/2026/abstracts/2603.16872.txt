Title: Trust, Safety, and Accuracy: Assessing LLMs for Routine Maternity Advice
Authors: V Sai Divya, A Bhanusree,  Rimjhim, K Venkata Krishna Rao
ID: 2603.16872
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.16872

Access to reliable maternal healthcare information is a major challenge in rural
India due to limited medical resources and infrastructure. With over 830 million
internet users and nearly half of rural women online, digital tools offer new
opportunities for health education. This study evaluates large language models
(LLMs) like ChatGPT-4o, Perplexity AI, and GeminiAI to provide reliable and
understandable pregnancy-related information. Seventeen pregnancy-focused
questions were posed to each model and compared with responses from maternal
health professionals. Evaluations used semantic similarity, noun overlap, and
readability metrics to measure content quality. Results show Perplexity closely
matched expert semantics, while ChatGPT-4o produced clearer, more understandable
text with better medical terminology. As internet access grows in rural areas,
LLMs could serve as scalable aids for maternal health education. The study
highlights the need for AI tools that balance accuracy and clarity to improve
healthcare communication in underserved regions.
