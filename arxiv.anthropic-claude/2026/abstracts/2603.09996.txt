Title: There Are No Silly Questions: Evaluation of Offline LLM Capabilities from a Turkish Perspective
Authors: Edibe Yilmaz, Kahraman Kostas
ID: 2603.09996
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09996

Abstract:
The integration of large language models (LLMs) into educational processes
introduces significant constraints regarding data privacy and reliability,
particularly in pedagogically vulnerable contexts such as Turkish heritage
language education. This study aims to systematically evaluate the robustness
and pedagogical safety of locally deployable offline LLMs within the context of
Turkish heritage language education. To this end, a Turkish Anomaly Suite (TAS)
consisting of 10 original edge-case scenarios was developed to assess the
models' capacities for epistemic resistance, logical consistency, and
pedagogical safety. Experiments conducted on 14 different models ranging from
270M to 32B parameters reveal that anomaly resistance is not solely dependent on
model scale and that sycophancy bias can pose pedagogical risks even in
large-scale models. The findings indicate that reasoning-oriented models in the
8B--14B parameter range represent the most balanced segment in terms of
cost-safety trade-off for language learners.
