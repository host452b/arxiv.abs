Title: Large Language Models, Artificial General Intelligence, and the Transformation of Labour Markets: A Convergent Mixed-Methods Empirical Study
Authors: None
ID: socarxiv.rnkhg_v1
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/socarxiv.rnkhg_v1

This paper examines AI's labour market effects through a convergent mixed-
methods design combining a questionnaire survey (n=237), expert interviews
(n=16), econometric analysis of merged occupational datasets (n=92), and a
controlled experiment (n=48). The central finding is that unrestricted AI access
produces full task delegation rather than augmentation behaviour, with task
completion time reduced by 78.6% (Cohen's d=4.64, p&lt;0.001). Econometric
analysis finds routine-task intensity positively predicts automation risk
(β=+0.086, p=0.007) while wage level strongly moderates it (β=−0.446,
p&lt;0.001). Original analysis of the Anthropic Economic Index documents India's
15.5x productivity speedup — the highest globally — while identifying the
exclusion of India's informal workforce from all existing measurement
frameworks.
