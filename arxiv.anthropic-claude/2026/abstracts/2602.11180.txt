Title: Mechanistic Interpretability for Large Language Model Alignment: Progress, Challenges, and Future Directions
Authors: Usman Naseem
ID: 2602.11180
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11180

Large language models (LLMs) have achieved remarkable capabilities across
diverse tasks, yet their internal decision-making processes remain largely
opaque. Mechanistic interpretability (i.e., the systematic study of how neural
networks implement algorithms through their learned representations and
computational structures) has emerged as a critical research direction for
understanding and aligning these models. This paper surveys recent progress in
mechanistic interpretability techniques applied to LLM alignment, examining
methods ranging from circuit discovery to feature visualization, activation
steering, and causal intervention. We analyze how interpretability insights have
informed alignment strategies including reinforcement learning from human
feedback (RLHF), constitutional AI, and scalable oversight. Key challenges are
identified, including the superposition hypothesis, polysemanticity of neurons,
and the difficulty of interpreting emergent behaviors in large-scale models. We
propose future research directions focusing on automated interpretability,
cross-model generalization of circuits, and the development of interpretability-
driven alignment techniques that can scale to frontier models.
